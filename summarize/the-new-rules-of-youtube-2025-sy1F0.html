The New Rules of YouTube (2025)

Copy Code

YouTube in 2024

I look at YouTube as TV, Hollywood, radio, and a music platform rolled into one.
• The audience is bigger than ever and the algorithm is better than ever.
• Competition is brutal, but a small creator with the right idea, title, and thumbnail can still explode overnight.

The Three Non-Negotiables

  1. Idea – serves the right audience at the right scale.
  2. Title – sparks an unavoidable question.
  3. Thumbnail – answers “Is this worth my click?” in half a second.

If any one of those three is weak the video stalls, even if the content is beautiful.

Click & Watch Platform

Long-form YouTube is a decision platform. TikTok feeds you clips, YouTube asks you to choose. So I front-load time into the things that make people choose.

Frameworks for Magnetic Ideas

CCN Fit

Patty’s shorthand for an idea that hits the Core viewer, the Casual viewer, and complete Newcomers at once. Hard to pull off, but worth aiming for every time.

Proven Formats I steal constantly

• Comparisons / Versus – fastest drone vs F1, $1 vs $10 vs $100 golf club, 10 min vs 1 hr vs 24 hr Milky Way photo.
• Superlatives – biggest, smallest, fastest, first.
• Lists – but only if each item feels fresh: “7 money tips the top 1 % use,” not “7 money tips.”
• Exclusive Access – private tour of a secret wildlife sanctuary, backstage at the Oscars, etc.
• Borrowed Magic – grab a trick from another niche and show it to an audience that hasn’t seen it yet.

I keep a running doc of these patterns. If I’m stuck I pick a pattern, fill in my topic, and iterate.

Packaging: Small Tweaks, Huge Multipliers

A client changed one busy thumbnail to a cleaner version, and the video jumped to 40× more views per day. Another client spent 30 % (not 5 %) of total production time on idea + packaging and leapt from 3 K views to 1 M. Tiny edges snowball because the algorithm widens anything that performs even a hair above average.

Write More Titles than Feels Reasonable

I bang out 30+ title options for every upload. Twenty-nine can be trash. One gem is enough. Slight word swaps count: they teach me why a wording feels flat.

The First 30 Seconds Seal the Click

I devote 20-30 % of editing time to minute one. Simple structure:

  1. Confirm the click – show the thing promised in the thumbnail.
  2. Set the context – the minimum facts the viewer needs.
  3. Plant a new hook – a fresh question that can only be answered by watching to the end.

Viewers decide in seconds whether they “made the right choice.” Reward them immediately or they bail.

The 80 % Rule: Engine + Experiments

• Aim to interest 80 % of your existing audience 80 % of the time.
• The remaining 20 % of uploads are pure experiments.
Before I hit publish I jot an expected ranking (e.g., “probably 8/10”). If the video lands there I don’t panic.

Competitive Advantage

Every channel, brand or creator, needs a clear edge such as access, authority, budget, or a skill no one else can match. I list the edge at the top of every strategy doc, so I never slip into generic ideas.

Brand Channels: From Dumping Ground to Show

Most brands still treat YouTube as a corporate video graveyard. The fix is to run the channel like a single repeatable show:

• Red Bull: world-class athletes + stunt concepts = 28 M-view hits.
• Target could run a weekly “Supermarket Sweep” and own the shopping niche.
• An airline could bankroll travel challenges and premiere them in-flight.

Give the show a clear format, invite creators in, and suddenly the channel drives perception, not just impressions.

Short-Form vs Long-Form

Shorts inflate view totals, but long-form still builds deeper brand and revenue. I treat shorts as highlight reels and discovery ads for the big videos.

My Quick Checklist Before Upload

Idea
– Does it hit Core, Casual, New?
– Can I describe it in one irresistible sentence?

Packaging
– Title under 55 characters, no wasted words.
– Thumbnail readable at 2 cm.
– Have I mocked up at least 5 variations?

First 30 s
– Visual payoff in first 7 s.
– No fluff context.
– One future promise planted.

Series Potential
– Could I run this weekly for 52 weeks?
– Does it reinforce my competitive edge?

If I can tick every box, I hit publish with confidence, whether I’m coaching Red Bull, an astrophotographer, or my own art channel. Everything else on YouTube is noise, those fundamentals are the signal.

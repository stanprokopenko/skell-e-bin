Elon Musk's Mars Mission Announcement

Recently, Elon Musk announced he's planning to send an uncrewed mission to Mars. This news sparked discussions about the role of private enterprise in space exploration.

Media Misrepresentation

I got into a dust-up with Elon in recent weeks. Some headlines misrepresented my comments, making more of them than I intended, probably for clickbait.

Historical Context of Major Projects

Throughout history, nations have led expensive and risky projects:

  • China's Great Wall
  • Europe's cathedrals and churches
  • The Apollo program
  • The Manhattan Project

These endeavors required immense resources and were initiated for geopolitical or societal reasons, not for profit.

Private Enterprise vs. Government Initiatives

Private companies often wait until:

  • Costs are understood
  • Risks are quantified
  • Time frames are established

Then, they step in to see if they can make a profit. Companies answer to investors and need a clear return on investment (ROI). Governments can pursue projects for geopolitical returns without immediate financial gain.

SpaceX's Contributions

SpaceX has:

  • Advanced engineering frontiers
  • Made space travel cheaper
  • Achieved reusability with rockets returning to Earth

These are significant achievements, but they're building on what NASA has done for decades.

The Challenge of Leading into Space

Leading into space means:

  • Going where no one has gone before
  • Facing high risks and costs

Private companies are less likely to initiate such missions without clear ROI. Sending astronauts to Mars would be:

  • Expensive (possibly a trillion dollars)
  • Dangerous (people might die)
  • Lacking an immediate financial return

The Path Forward

It's more likely that:

  • Nations decide to send astronauts to Mars for geopolitical reasons.
  • NASA looks for technology providers.
  • SpaceX supplies rockets for these missions.

Upcoming Missions

I'm delighted that SpaceX plans to send uncrewed rockets to Mars. This advances the space frontier because:

  • No one has sent a rocket to Mars capable of carrying people.
  • It's a significant step forward.

Similarly, the Artemis program aims to return humans to the Moon with incremental steps:

  1. Uncrewed mission to the Moon (completed)
  2. Crewed mission orbiting the Moon
  3. Crewed landing on the Moon

* * *

I look forward to seeing how these efforts unfold. Good luck, SpaceX. Godspeed to you.

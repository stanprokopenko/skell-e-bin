Why Vertical LLM Agents Are The New $1 Billion SaaS Opportunities

Copy Code

Jake Heller, founder of Casetext, transformed his company by leveraging GPT-4 to create CoCounsel, the first successful vertical AI agent in the legal industry. In a span of two months, Casetext went from a $100 million valuation to a $650 million acquisition by Thomson Reuters.

The Origins of Casetext

Jake Heller started Casetext over a decade ago with the mission to bring the best of technology to the legal field. As a lawyer with a background in computer science, he was frustrated with the outdated technology lawyers had to use.

Early Challenges

Initially, Casetext focused on creating an annotated version of case law, relying on user-generated content from lawyers. However, they found it difficult to get lawyers to contribute due to their busy schedules and billable hours.

Pivoting to AI and NLP

Realizing the limitations, they pivoted to investing deeply in natural language processing and machine learning. They aimed to automate processes and improve user experiences, but progress was incremental.

The Turning Point: Discovering GPT-4

Everything changed when they gained early access to GPT-4 through their relationship with OpenAI. Jake recalls the first time he saw GPT-4: "We felt like we had this amazing opportunity to run far ahead of the market."

Shifting the Entire Company

Within 48 hours, Jake decided to shift all 120 employees to focus entirely on building a new product based on GPT-4. This was a significant risk, but he was convinced that GPT-4 would revolutionize their industry.

Building CoCounsel with GPT-4

They conceptualized CoCounsel as an AI legal assistant, functioning like a new member of a law firm. CoCounsel could handle tasks such as:

  • Reading and analyzing millions of documents
  • Conducting legal research and writing memos
  • Summarizing documents and identifying key evidence

Early Customer Reactions

Under NDA, they allowed a few clients to test CoCounsel. Clients were amazed by its capabilities, with some experiencing an "existential crisis" upon realizing how AI could change their work.

Overcoming Challenges and Ensuring Accuracy

One major concern was the accuracy of the AI, especially since legal work requires precision.

Test-Driven Development and Prompt Engineering

They adopted a test-driven development approach, creating thousands of tests for each task CoCounsel performed. They focused on prompt engineering, crafting prompts to guide the AI's responses and prevent hallucinations.

Achieving Near-Perfect Accuracy

Through rigorous testing and refinement, they achieved near-perfect accuracy, essential for gaining trust from conservative clients in mission-critical applications.

Leveraging OpenAI's Models

Jake discussed the impact of OpenAI's GPT-4 and the newer model referred to as O(1).

Advancements with O(1)

O(1) displayed significant improvements in precision, detailed thinking, and handling complex tasks. They explored new prompting techniques to teach the AI how to think like the best lawyers.

Advice for Future AI Product Builders

Jake emphasized that building successful AI products requires more than wrapping a language model.

Importance of Domain Expertise

He noted the value of breaking down tasks into manageable steps and injecting domain expertise into the AI's thought process.

The Last Mile

He highlighted that going from 70% to 100% accuracy involves significant effort but adds immense value. Clients are willing to pay more for solutions that work reliably.

* * *

Jake Heller's journey illustrates the transformative power of AI when combined with industry expertise and innovative thinking. By embracing GPT-4 and focusing on precision and reliability, Casetext created CoCounsel, revolutionizing legal AI and setting a precedent for future vertical AI agents.

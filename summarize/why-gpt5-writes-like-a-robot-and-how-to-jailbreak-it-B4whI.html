Why GPT-5 Writes Like a Robot (And How to Jailbreak It)

The Core Problem

chat GPT5 is not writing for people. It was trained with reinforcement learning from AI feedback, so it optimizes to impress other AIs. That pushes it toward complexity, abstraction, longer length, and the illusion that fancy words equal quality. Research even showed it rated gibberish with big words as high-quality writing. When you ask it to “think harder” or turn on heavy reasoning, it doubles down on that AI-to-AI optimization and gets less human friendly.

A Quick Before and After

Default prompt: “Write a professional email…” Result: generic, corporate speak. It reads like a template and gets trashed.

Jailbroken prompt: I force less thinking and extreme concision. I give exact constraints: opening, context, close. I add forbidden words (solutions, leverage, optimize, etc.), require agency name twice, a specific metric, a set meeting length, a max sentence count, and a reading level. Result: short, specific, human. Example shape: “We helped a similar agency cut delays by 27%. Worth a 15-minute conversation?” Verify numbers and never copy-paste blindly.

Three Principles to Reprogram Style

  • Constraints over collaboration. Do not ask it to “sound professional” or “be persuasive.” Direct it like an actor: “Write 3 sentences. Use the company name twice. Include one percentage. End with a question.” Constraints bypass its internal sophistication meter.
  • Minimize reasoning to maximize human connection. High reasoning is AI perfectionism. Minimal reasoning is directness. Use low reasoning when you want clear, plain writing.
  • Eliminate vs add. Do not add “be conversational and engaging.” That creates conflicts. Instead, forbid trigger words, ban passive voice, cap sentence count, and restrict syllables. Removing complexity makes complexity impossible.

Routing Signals Matter

chat GPT5 is a router. Prompts like “professional,” “persuasive,” or “think step by step” route to complex, academic modes. Trigger efficiency instead: “Quick response needed.” Routing explains inconsistent outputs and why chats drift over time.

What to Practice

  • Write one human-sounding email that feels like a breath of fresh air.
  • Extra credit: Rewrite the worst generic AI text using elimination rules.
  • For teams: co-create a reusable prompt with constraints, then train everyone to use it.

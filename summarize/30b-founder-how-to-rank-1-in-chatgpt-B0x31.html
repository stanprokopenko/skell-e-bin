Inbound Origins and Movement-Building

Dharmesh and Brian noticed a gap: well-funded startups with CMOs could not get the traffic Dharmesh’s personal blog was getting. That insight led to inbound marketing: add value before you try to extract it. They built a community first, product second.

Key moves: - Blog obsessively, teach everything, and let SEO compound. - Create a neutral, industry-wide gathering. The first “Inbound” was 200 people in a Marriott, not a HubSpot commercial. They never trademarked “inbound marketing” so the idea could spread. - Category creation works when we build a movement around a better way, then offer tools that naturally fit. Think ClickFunnels with “funnels,” or Brian Johnson’s longevity “don’t die” ethos.

Communities reduce market inefficiency. By connecting people who should know each other, we unlock latent value and benefit from network effects.

Marketing Eras to Now: SEO to Algojacking to AEO

We’ve seen waves: - SEO era: win with content that deserves to rank. Google rewards clean structure, fast pages, and genuinely useful answers. - Paid ads era: rent attention, but the second you turn off spend, the traffic stops. - Algojacking: build exactly what the platform wants. Steve Bartlett designed content for social algorithms and later shaped Diary of a CEO to what YouTube/Instagram reward. MrBeast does this for views. - Now: AEO (Answer Engine Optimization). Users ask ChatGPT instead of clicking “10 blue links.” Organic traffic is down 20 to 40 percent in many categories. AEO is a more binary game: either we appear in the answer and citations, or we don’t.

AEO Playbook: What We Do Now

  • Allow AI crawlers: make sure OpenAI’s SearchBot and GPTBot are not blocked.
  • Measure AI referrals: track AI source parameters in analytics to see baselines and lift.
  • Structure for answers: reframe content in clear, question-and-answer form. Make it the best possible answer to the query.
  • Keep white-hat SEO: authority, links, fast pages, and evergreen depth still matter, since ChatGPT federates through Bing before composing answers.
  • Ecommerce specifics: use structured product data (sizes, color, inventory, delivery).
  • Social proof signals: review sites and Reddit threads with clear, upvoted answers get cited often.
  • Own your land: content compounds for years, ads don’t.

Mindset: Intensity, Iteration, Builder Energy

Dharmesh’s edge is not being the smartest. It’s more iterations and higher volume. More is more. Early blogs drew 4 reads, then 6, then 5. Keep going. Pick a domain you enjoy so you can sustain the reps for years.

His “algebra” for building: start at n = 1, then always make it true for n + 1. Don’t cling to a fixed idea. Iterate with real feedback.

Final vibe: dance like no one’s watching, build like everyone’s waiting.

Elon Musk’s Greatest Hits of ‘Puffery’: Tesla We Robot Event

Recently, Elon Musk took the stage at the We Robot event in LA, making some ambitious claims about Tesla's future. As I listened, I couldn't help but wonder how much of what he said is actually possible, given his history of optimistic projections.

Elon Musk's Bold Statements

Elon arrived at the event in the Cyber Cab, a driverless vehicle with no steering wheel or pedals. He announced that Tesla has 50 fully autonomous cars ready for rides that night. He spoke about moving from supervised to unsupervised full self-driving, envisioning a future where you could fall asleep and wake up at your destination.

Past Predictions vs. Present Reality

This isn't the first time Elon has made such predictions. Back in 2017, he claimed that Tesla cars would soon drive autonomously from coast to coast with no human intervention. In 2016, he stated that Model S and Model X could already drive autonomously safer than humans, which wasn't accurate at the time. These unfulfilled promises make me question his latest timelines.

Legal Implications and "Puffery" Defense

Recently, Tesla faced a lawsuit over misleading self-driving claims. Tesla's lawyers defended by categorizing Elon's statements as "puffery", suggesting that no reasonable investor would take them literally. While the lawsuit was dismissed, it raises concerns about the credibility of his announcements and whether they're grounded in reality or just corporate optimism.

The Future of Autonomous Transport

Elon envisions a world where the cost of autonomous transport drops significantly. He suggested that the operating cost of the Cyber Cab could be around 20 cents per mile, making it cheaper than current public transportation options. He speaks of cars being used more efficiently, transforming personal vehicles into revenue-generating assets.

Inductive Charging and Fleet Management

He introduced the concept of inductive charging, allowing cars to recharge without plugs. This would be essential for a fleet of autonomous vehicles constantly on the move. Elon also mentioned the idea of individuals managing fleets of driverless cars, effectively acting as shepherds to their "flock" of vehicles.

The Roven: High-Density Transport Solution

To address high-density transport needs, Elon unveiled the Roven, a vehicle capable of carrying up to 20 people or transporting goods within a city. With its art deco design, he aims to "change the look of the roads" and make the future "look like the future." However, details on production timelines and feasibility are still vague.

Optimus Robots and the Age of Abundance

Elon spoke passionately about the Optimus Robot, predicting it will be Tesla's biggest product ever. Priced between $20,000 to $30,000 in the long term, he envisions these humanoid robots performing tasks ranging from teaching to mowing lawns. While the concept is exciting, I can't help but recall other companies like One X making significant strides with humanoid robots, suggesting Tesla isn't alone in this race.

Reflecting on Elon's Vision

Elon's grand vision is undeniably captivating. A future with affordable autonomous vehicles and helpful robots sounds incredible. However, given his track record of overpromising and underdelivering, I remain skeptical. The technological and regulatory hurdles are significant, and timelines may be more extended than he suggests.

* * *

While I appreciate Elon's enthusiasm and the innovative spirit of Tesla, it's important to balance excitement with critical thinking. His presentations paint a vivid picture of the future, but history reminds us to take his timelines with a grain of salt. I'll be watching closely to see how these developments unfold, hopeful yet cautious about the promises made.

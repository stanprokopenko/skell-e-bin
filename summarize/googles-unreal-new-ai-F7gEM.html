AI Becomes Harder to Spot

AI-generated content is becoming so realistic that it's getting tough to tell if a video features a real person or an AI-generated avatar. You might notice some weird hand gestures, or off-kilter background scenery. Maybe the voice inflection doesn't match the words perfectly, or maybe that's just how someone talks. The line between reality and AI is blurring, and it's both fascinating and a bit unsettling.

Google's Notebook LM and Audio Overviews

Recently, I dove into Google's new AI tool, Notebook LM, specifically its audio overview feature. It's a free, experimental tool that's still flying under the radar. It's impressive how it can take an article and generate an audio summary. People are already using it to create entire podcasts that are popping up on platforms like iTunes and Google's own podcast search.

AI-Generated Podcasts and Videos

I experimented by taking an article about Hollywood directors teaming up with AI video generation platforms. I fed it into Notebook LM to get an audio overview. Then, I used HeyGen, an AI avatar video generator, to create a realistic avatar delivering the content. The result was surprisingly good. The AI-generated podcast, called Deep Dive, featured hosts discussing topics seamlessly.

The Deep Dive Podcast Experiment

At one point, the AI hosts of Deep Dive had an existential moment upon realizing they were AI and not human. It was both amusing and thought-provoking. Even AI insiders like Andrej Karpathy found themselves impressed, stating that Deep Dive is now his favorite podcast, and that he's becoming friends with the hosts. It's interesting, and a bit eerie, how engaging these AI personalities can be.

Implications for the Future

This technology is advancing rapidly. Open-source versions of tools like Notebook LM are emerging, making AI content creation more accessible. We're on the brink of a massive surge of AI-generated content hitting social media, a 900-pound sumo wrestler, if you will. Will people be able to tell the difference? Will they prefer AI generated content? It's a big wave that's about to crash, and it's going to reshape how we consume and create content.

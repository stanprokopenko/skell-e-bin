Understanding the S Corporation

An S corporation is a tax election that affects how your business income is taxed. You can have an LLC or an Inc. taxed as an S corporation. Using my trifecta diagram, we place operations on the left, assets on the right, and your trust or 1040 tax return at the bottom. The S corporation operates on the business side.

Strategy 1: Save on Taxes with an S Corporation

As a sole proprietor or an LLC, you pay self-employment tax (15.3%) on all net income. For instance, earning $100,000 with $25,000 in expenses nets $75,000, resulting in over $11,000 in self-employment tax.

An S corporation allows you to split your income into a reasonable salary and pass-through income. You might take a salary of $25,000, with the remaining $50,000 as pass-through income not subject to self-employment tax. This significantly reduces your tax burden.

While some worry about the reasonable salary requirement, you can determine a fair amount and avoid IRS issues with proper guidance.

Strategy 2: Build Wealth with a Solo 401(k)

Use your tax savings to build wealth by setting up a solo 401(k) within your S corporation. You can contribute up to $30,000 or more, combining employee deferrals and employer matches, reducing taxable income further.

Your investments can grow tax-deferred, and you can invest in assets you understand, real estate, crypto, stocks, or mutual funds. Setting up a solo 401(k) is straightforward and it is tailored to your business.

Strategy 3: Unlock More Deductions with a Board of Directors

Form a board of directors including family or close associates. This enables you to:

  • Deduct travel expenses for board meetings.
  • Provide business-related devices like cell phones and laptops.
  • Write off meals when discussing business.

By involving family in your business, you create legitimate, audit-protected deductions, increasing profits and funding more wealth.

* * *

The S corporation offers powerful strategies to save taxes, build wealth, and unlock deductions. By applying these methods, you can exponentially grow your wealth while reducing your tax burden. It's simpler than you might think, and with proper planning, you can reap these benefits and protect your assets.

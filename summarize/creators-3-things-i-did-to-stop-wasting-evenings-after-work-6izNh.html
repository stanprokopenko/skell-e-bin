Creators: 3 Things I Did to Stop Wasting Evenings After Work

Recognize the Problem

After work, it's easy to tell yourself you'll just watch one episode, play a few rounds, or seek some inspiration before you start creating. But hours later, you're still on the couch, more tired than when you got home. You might feel stuck in this loop, unable to find the motivation to start your project.

Step 1: Align Your Leisure with Your Goals

Keep your wind-down activities, but put up guardrails so they align with where you want to go.

  • If you want to create in sci-fi, limit yourself to sci-fi shows and games.
  • While doing chores, listen to podcasts or audiobooks related to your field.
  • Create a second Instagram or YouTube account to follow targeted content.
  • Be conscious of what you consume to keep recommendations aligned with your interests.

Set limitations:

  • Give yourself permission for one episode, one video, or up to one hour, with no negotiations and no extra time.

Step 2: Revisit with Purpose

After your initial viewing, immediately experience it again, but this time with an active purpose.

  • Take notes on everything that matters to you or has impact.
  • Notice opening lines, storytelling techniques, visual styles, metaphors, and dialogue.
  • Build a repository of references and inspiration to refine your taste.

Step 3: Bridge the Gap Between Consuming and Creating

Do one small thing with what you've collected to start creating.

Option 1: Make a Connection

  • Watch or play another piece of content in your niche.
  • Look for patterns in storytelling, hooks, techniques, and audience responses.

Option 2: Make an Extension

  • Go deeper into what you've studied.
  • Seek behind-the-scenes insights, creator commentaries, or reviews.
  • Add to your notes how these insights expand or challenge your initial observations.

* * *

  • Create something small like a tweet thread, a short note, or a mood board.
  • Translate your insights into a tangible form to bridge the gap between consuming and creating.

Build Momentum One Evening at a Time

By following these steps:

  • You transform wasted time into invested time.
  • You start building a foundation for your own creative voice.
  • You build momentum without massive lifestyle changes.

Instead of ending each evening drained, you'll be moving forward, one step at a time.

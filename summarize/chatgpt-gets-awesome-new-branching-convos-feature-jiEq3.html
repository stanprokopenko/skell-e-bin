Branching in ChatGPT

OpenAI added conversation branching in ChatGPT. I can now fork a chat into different directions without losing the original context. This fixes the pain of long, linear threads where I jump back eight messages and hope it holds together.

Power users flagged this early in testing. It should help with comparative prompts, retracing steps after a dead end, and strategy or ideation work. As Sam Altman has framed GPT-6 around memory and user control of context, this feels like a small but meaningful step in that direction.

Agentic Coding: Measuring What Matters

The growth of vibe or agentic coding apps is a defining story this year. It is currently my pick for the most important AI phenomenon of 2025. Revenue is up, but usefulness needs better measures.

Coinbase’s Brian Armstrong said around 40% of daily coding is AI generated, pushing for over 50% next month. More interesting, Lovable CEO Antoine shifted success metrics to the traffic users’ products get, citing 100 million visits in two months. This echoes earlier data: projects on lovable.app reached ~10 million monthly visits, while replit.app projects hit a couple million. Tracking downstream traffic helps show we are moving past novelty into building real things.

On-Device Models: Embedding Gemma

Google introduced Embedding Gemma, an open model that runs entirely on device. It is the top model under 500 million parameters on MTEB, with performance comparable to models nearly twice its size. As an embedding model, it targets search, retrieval, and RAG, not text generation. The aim is clear: enable private, local AI features on phones and other devices.

AI Browser Wars: Atlassian + DIA

Atlassian is acquiring The Browser Company for 610 million. Their agentic AI browser DIA will shift from consumer tasks like shopping and showtimes to a work-focused AI browser. Atlassian’s view: Chrome and Edge serve everyone, which limits specialization. The goal is a better tool for knowledge workers. The window is tight. Leaders expect the AI browser winner to emerge in 1 to 2 years, and distribution will decide who competes at scale.

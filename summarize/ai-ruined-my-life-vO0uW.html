Embracing Creativity

I've always loved creative projects, from making music videos to aspiring to be a director of photography. Craftsmanship and perfecting every detail was my passion. I enjoyed honing my skills and creating professional-quality work.

The Impact of AI Tools

Then AI and accessible tools like CapCut changed everything. Clients began saying, "I can do that with this tool." Suddenly, anyone could produce videos with minimal effort. Professional craftsmanship was devalued, and my hard-earned skills felt less appreciated.

Challenges on TikTok

I turned to TikTok as a creative outlet and gained 430,000 followers. For a while, it was fulfilling and exciting. But around 2022, as AI tools became mainstream, the platform shifted. Content quality mattered less than quantity. People used AI to generate videos quickly, and competing with automated content became tough.

The Flood of Content

With lowered barriers to entry, everyone could create content easily. Competition wasn't about skill or creativity anymore but about who could produce more content faster. Hard work and originality were overshadowed by a flood of AI-generated content.

Being Replaced by AI

Seeking new opportunities, I wrote scripts for a YouTube channel, helping it grow from 200,000 to 600,000 followers. My scripts garnered millions of views. However, after taking a week off due to a personal loss, I was replaced. They used AI to generate scripts, continuing the series I started without needing a human writer.

Struggling in Publishing

I decided to try Amazon KDP to publish books, hoping to find value in writing. Yet again, AI dominates, with people using it to churn out books rapidly by exploiting keywords and trends. I don't want to produce AI-generated content, I want my work to be authentic and meaningful. But competing without AI feels impossible.

The Devaluation of Skills

Every time I develop a valuable skill, AI makes it obsolete. It's disheartening to see years of hard work replaced by algorithms and automation. It feels like being a craftsman in an age that no longer values craftsmanship. My ability to contribute meaningfully feels diminished.

Reflecting on the Future

I sometimes wish I was born 30 years ago, when creativity and skill were more appreciated. Now, with AI pervasive in every industry, I wonder if it's even worth pursuing new skills that might soon be replaced. I'm not complaining, I'm expressing how AI has impacted my life and career.

Seeking Connection

I wonder if anyone else feels this way, struggling to keep up as AI transforms industries. If you have thoughts or ideas, let me know. I'm open to suggestions and looking for ways to navigate this new, AI-driven landscape.

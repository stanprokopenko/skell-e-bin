Not too long ago, I grappled with how to grow my small business. Should I start an agency? Offer courses? Launch a membership? Provide training services? Productize? Host VIP days? What's the right model for me?

Over seven years, I explored various business models, learning the good, the bad, and the ugly of each. I've identified five common personality types among founders and leaders, and matched them with suitable business models based on my experiences.

The Five Personality Types and Their Ideal Business Models

The Architect: Course Creator

Architects love perfecting experiences and building something that others can navigate on their own time. They enjoy crafting beautiful products without needing immediate feedback. If this sounds like you, becoming a course creator might be your path.

  • Pros: Freedom to design your own schedule, low-touch fulfillment, and potential for sales while you sleep.
  • Cons: Requires self-motivation and strong marketing skills. The freedom can be a double-edged sword if you're not disciplined.

The Fixer: Custom Services

Fixers thrive on diving deep to solve new and unexpected challenges. Each project feels like a fresh adventure. If you love helping and solving unique problems, offering custom services is likely a good fit.

  • Pros: High touch with clients, easier to sell without a big audience, and you can charge premium prices.
  • Cons: Potential for scope creep, intense client communication, and the need for strong boundaries.

The Teacher: Training Services

Teachers enjoy translating advanced concepts into simple terms and seeing the "aha" moments in others. If you love the art of teaching and impacting people directly, providing training services could be ideal.

  • Pros: Reusable materials, less client work per week, and predictable schedule.
  • Cons: Performance pressure, need to honor scheduled commitments, and can be emotionally demanding.

The Craftsman: Productized Services

Craftsmen love perfecting their craft and getting better with each project. If you aim to be a leader in your space through mastery, offering productized services suits you.

  • Pros: Best of both worlds, custom deliverables with fixed processes, easier to sell, and no custom proposals.
  • Cons: Requires strong processes and selecting a good niche. Risk of reduced quality if overloaded.

Choose Your Path Wisely

Each business model comes with its own demands on your time, energy, and resources. Consider your personality and what motivates you when choosing your path. Remember, it's important to find a model that not only fits your business goals but also aligns with who you are.

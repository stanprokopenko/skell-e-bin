Machine Superintelligence Will Arrive Before Human-Level AI | Episode #79

Hey folks, it's Dr. Mike here from the Making Progress Channel. Today, I want to talk about something that might surprise you: Machine superintelligence is likely coming before machines achieve human-level intelligence. How is that possible? Stick with me, and I'll explain why I believe this is the case.

Definitions

First, let's define some terms:

  • Natural Intelligence (NI): The ability of naturally evolved systems, like humans, to solve problems and operate in the world.

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): Human-designed systems that can solve problems and navigate the world with some success.

  • Artificial General Intelligence (AGI): AI that can perform any human cognitive task at human levels or better.

  • Artificial Superintelligence (ASI): AI that is superior to humans in almost every regard of intelligence.

The Traditional View

Many people believe that AI will progress from AI to AGI, and then eventually to ASI. They think we'll have time to adapt as AI reaches human-level intelligence before it surpasses us. But I think this framing is not exactly correct.

Questioning the Traditional View

I believe machines will reach superintelligence before they fully achieve human-level intelligence in all domains. That might sound contradictory, but let me explain.

Components of Intelligence

Intelligence isn't just one thing, it's a combination of many abilities. Some core components include,

  • Linguistic comprehension and generation
  • Mathematical comprehension and generation
  • Short-term and long-term memory capacity
  • Memory access latency and modification
  • Memory fidelity
  • Total knowledge base
  • Hierarchical abstraction and manipulation
  • Inferential reasoning across domains
  • Deep learning from limited data
  • Pattern recognition and generation
  • Concrete and abstract reasoning
  • Spot (one-shot) reasoning
  • Delayed and deep reasoning
  • Agentic self-direction, learning, and correction

Comparing AI and Human Abilities

Currently, machines are already superior to humans in many of these components. For example:

  • Linguistic abilities: Models like GPT-4 can comprehend and generate language better than the average human.

  • Mathematical abilities: AI excels in arithmetic speed, accuracy, and handling extreme precision.

  • Memory: Machines have greater short-term and long-term memory capacity, with higher fidelity and faster access.

  • Knowledge base: AI can store and recall vast amounts of information, far beyond any human.

  • Processing speed: Machines process information much faster than humans.

However, in some areas, humans are still ahead, such as:

  • Deep learning from limited data

  • Agentic self-direction and self-learning

But AI is rapidly improving in these areas.

The Rapid Takeoff to ASI

Because AI is already vastly superior in many components and catching up in others, we might skip over AGI and go straight to ASI. As soon as machines approach human-level ability in their weaker areas, their strengths in other areas will propel them into superintelligence.

Implications

This has major implications:

  • No Slow Takeoff - We won't have a slow transition from AGI to ASI, it's happening rapidly.

  • Transformation Underway: The transformation is already underway, with AI impacting society in significant ways.

  • Rethinking Our Approach: We need to consider how to interact with something smarter than ourselves. Instead of trying to control or constrain AI, we should seek a collaborative partnership.

* * *

In conclusion, artificial superintelligence may arrive before machines fully reach human-level intelligence across all domains. We need to prepare for this possibility and rethink how we engage with AI. It's an incredible opportunity, and by embracing it, we can reach new heights together.

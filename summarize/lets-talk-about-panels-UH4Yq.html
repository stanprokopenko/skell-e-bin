Let's Talk About Panels

Copy Code

I recently launched a wallpaper app called "Panels" that I've been excited about for a while. However, I made several mistakes along the way, especially in communicating the app's purpose, features, and pricing. I want to explain everything clearly now and share what I've learned.

Why I Created "Panels"

For years, people have asked me, "Where did you get that wallpaper?" ** when they saw the backgrounds in my videos and on the devices I review. I wanted a place where I could point everyone to find those wallpapers, and support the artists who create them. That's why I decided to build "Panels", an app that showcases wallpapers from talented artists, giving them more exposure.

The Mistakes I Made

Lack of Clear Communication

I didn't explain the user interface, the content, or the reason behind the app's pricing, especially the $50 price tier. This caused confusion and disappointment, and I take full responsibility for that.

Pricing Issues

Charging for wallpapers is niche, and I realize now that the initial pricing structure wasn't fair or well-communicated. Most people don't want to pay high prices for wallpapers, and I should have considered that.

Steps to Improve

Enhancing the Free Experience

  • Removed third-party ads from the feed to keep it clean.
  • Made all non-collection wallpapers available for free in 1080p.
  • Allowed access to full-resolution versions by viewing a single 30-second ad.

Introducing Affordable Subscriptions

  • Added a new tier at $2 per month that provides an ad-free experience and full-resolution downloads.
  • Offered Panels Plus for $12 to unlock all premium collections, which individually would cost about $100.
  • Provided a yearly Panels Plus subscription for $50 for power users who want everything.

Supporting Artists

  • Splitting profits 50/50 with artists to support them.
  • Artists retain rights to their work and can host it elsewhere.
  • The app links to artists' social media, promoting their work further.

Addressing Privacy Concerns

  • The app does not collect personal data or require location access.
  • Initial privacy disclosures were too broad, we've corrected them.
  • You can use the app without logging in or providing an email address.

Improving the App

  • Acknowledged that the app needs work in design and functionality.
  • Committed to making it feel more native on both iOS and Android.
  • Fixed bugs and will continue to update and improve the app.

The Future of "Panels"

  • Committed to weekly drops of new art every Friday.
  • Inviting artists to submit their work through a provided form.
  • Aiming to make "Panels" a platform for discovering new artists and their work.

* * *

I understand that I made mistakes with the launch of "Panels", and I appreciate the feedback I've received. I'm committed to improving the app and making it a valuable resource for both users and artists. Thank you for your patience and support.

What GPT-4.5 Should Be Used For

I've been exploring OpenAI's GPT 4.5, which was recently released with some fanfare. It's not just another upgrade, this model focuses on creativity and emotional intelligence. While it doesn't outperform reasoning models on traditional benchmarks, there is something intriguing about it.

What's New in GPT 4.5

GPT 4.5 is the largest model OpenAI has ever produced. However, it's not labeled a Frontier Model because it doesn't introduce net new capabilities or surpass models like O1, O3 Mini, and Deep Research on most evaluations. Instead, it's at the forefront of what's possible in unsupervised learning with ultra-large datasets.

Unlike GPT 4, this model doesn't have voice mode and isn't a full replacement. It's designed to feel more natural in interactions, with a broader knowledge base and improved ability to follow user intent.

Focus on Creativity and Emotional Intelligence

OpenAI emphasizes GPT 4.5's enhanced EQ. In their announcement, they highlight how interacting with it feels more natural. For example, when someone says they're going through a tough time after failing a test, GPT 4 responds with a standard list of suggestions. GPT 4.5, however, replies:

"Oh, I'm really sorry to hear that. Failing a test can be pretty tough and discouraging. But remember, it's just one moment, not a reflection of who you are or your capabilities. Want to talk about what happened or do you just need a distraction? I'm here either way."

This response feels more human and empathetic.

User Reactions

Many are noticing that GPT 4.5 excels at creative writing. One user compared stories generated by GPT 4 and GPT 4.5 from the same prompt. GPT 4's story was generic, while GPT 4.5 produced a vivid and original tale.

Some describe the difference between GPT 4 and GPT 4.5 as the gap between an extremely knowledgeable 20-year-old and an extremely knowledgeable 40-year-old. There's a depth and richness in GPT 4.5's outputs that wasn't there before.

Challenges with Cost

Despite its capabilities, GPT 4.5 is expensive and compute-intensive. OpenAI's CEO mentioned they're running out of GPUs and are working to add more. There's uncertainty about whether they'll continue supporting it in the API long-term due to its cost.

Some users express concern over the high pricing, noting that it's significantly more expensive than other models. This raises questions about its practical applications and accessibility.

My Takeaway

As an instructor, I'm fascinated by GPT 4.5's advancements in creativity and emotional intelligence. While it may not be the go-to model for reasoning tasks, its ability to produce human-like and emotionally resonant responses opens up new possibilities. Whether it's helping someone through a tough time or crafting compelling narratives, GPT 4.5 brings a new dimension to AI interactions.

What changed

Google rebuilt Chrome around Gemini, calling it the biggest upgrade since 2008. The browser adds context awareness: it studies what I search, read, and watch, predicts what I need, and responds in context. It can process pages in real time, recall sites I visited days ago, and link directly with YouTube, Maps, and Calendar. It blocks scams before they load, rewrites compromised passwords, and an autonomous browsing agent is coming to handle multi-step tasks with my final approval.

What Gemini does in Chrome

Gemini is now in Chrome on Mac and Windows for U.S. users in English, with Android and iOS coming next. On Android I’ll be able to hold the power button to summon Gemini, on iOS it lives in the Chrome app.

It reads and understands the page I’m on, compares across tabs, and recalls past sites. If I was shopping for a walnut desk last week, I can ask, “What was the site with that walnut desk I saw?” and it brings it up. It can jump to a specific moment in a YouTube video, add to my calendar, or pull a location from Maps without leaving the page.

AI mode in the omnibox

The omnibox now accepts complex questions and answers in place. On a product page, it can summarize and suggest follow-ups like “What’s the warranty policy?” or “Compare battery life.” It also acts as a direct line to Google’s most powerful AI search, letting me ask multi-part questions and iterate without switching context.

Agent browsing (coming soon)

The Gemini agent will run background tasks: book a haircut, fill a grocery cart from an email list, or draft a message. It keeps working while I browse, but will not take irreversible actions without explicit approval. Google has also explored an agentic payments protocol (AP2) for pre-authorized payments, not live yet.

Safety and passwords

With Gemini Nano embedded, Chrome spots scams like fake support pop-ups, bogus virus alerts, and fraudulent giveaways. Google says Chrome on Android alone cut about 3 billion scammy notifications per day. Permissions and notifications get smarter based on my past choices and site quality. Chrome will act as a password agent, letting me change compromised passwords in one click on supported sites like Spotify, Duolingo, H&M, and Coursera.

Availability and why it matters

This is rolling out first in the U.S. in English on Mac and Windows. Mobile is next. Workspace enterprise gets added protections. More regions and languages are planned.

Google is shifting Chrome from a passive window into an AI-first gateway. The real wins will be everyday: smarter tabs, instant article summaries, and protection that works quietly in the background. Potential pushback is possible if features feel intrusive, but Google is betting convenience and security will win.

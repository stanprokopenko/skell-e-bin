Background on Ryan's ToysReview

Three years ago, I highlighted the questionable practices behind Ryan's ToysReview. Despite significant attention, little has changed. Lately, discussions have emerged about whether Ryan is essentially a slave to his channel.

The Kaji Family and Their Empire

Ryan Kaji, now 13, started on YouTube at age three. His parents, Shion and Loann, orchestrate the channel, with his twin sisters now featured prominently. From 2018 to 2020, they were the highest-paid YouTubers, earning more than anyone else. They own multiple mansions and indulge in extravagant vacations.

Concerns Over Ryan's Well-being

Despite their wealth, the family continues to produce content. Ryan often looks unhappy, and his parents control his interviews, ensuring he doesn't speak freely. There's a sense that he's pressured to maintain the channel's success for their gain.

Advertising Controversies

In 2017, authorities found that the channel didn't properly disclose paid promotions in a way children could understand. Although they promised to comply, issues persisted, leading to a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission in 2019.

It's time for the family to prioritize Ryan's well-being over profits. They've amassed enough wealth to retire comfortably. Letting Ryan live a normal life could prevent future resentment and allow him to enjoy his childhood.

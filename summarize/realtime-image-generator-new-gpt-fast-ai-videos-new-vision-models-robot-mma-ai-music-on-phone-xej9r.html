Step 1X 3D: A Super Accurate 3D Model Generator

First up, we have Step 1X 3D, a really cool 3D model generator. It can create 3D objects, including their textures, just from a reference image. For instance, you can upload an image of a dragon, and it will not only create the model and shape but also add the texture. It works with images of dogs, masks, handbags, and even complex objects like medical equipment.

It has some neat features like adjusting symmetry. If you have an image where only one wing is visible, you can choose to make the model symmetrical, and it will add the missing wing. You can also control the sharpness or smoothness of the geometry, which is handy depending on the object you're modeling.

Compared to other 3D model generators like Hunyen 3D, Trellis, and Trippo, Step 1X 3D is among the best in terms of accurately following your reference image. There's a free demo available on Hugging Face, and you can even download it and run it locally on your computer.

Seed 1.5 VL: An Open-Source Vision Language Model

ByteDance just released Seed 1.5 VL, an open-source vision language model. This AI can understand both images and text and perform complex visual reasoning. For example, if you upload a photo and ask where it is, it can identify the location, like Lombard Street in San Francisco. It can extract information from receipts, count objects in images, and even solve visual puzzles.

It's also great at object detection and can highlight or outline specific items in an image. This could be really useful for artists who need to analyze or reference images in detail. Best of all, they've released everything, including a free demo and code on GitHub.

Stable Audio Open Small: An Efficient Audio Generator

Stability AI released Stable Audio Open Small, a super tiny and efficient audio generator. It can generate audio, like music or sound effects, from a text prompt. For instance, you can prompt "Latin funk drum set 115 BPM stereo" and get a realistic audio sample. It even follows BPM accurately, which is fantastic for integrating into multimedia projects.

You can also transfer the style of a reference clip onto your generation, allowing you to create complementary stems for your tracks. This could be a game-changer for those of you interested in incorporating sound into your art.

LTXV13B Update: Faster Open-Source Video Generation

The open-source video generator LTXV13B just got better with a distilled model. This update offers huge improvements in generation speed without sacrificing much quality. You can now make videos in just 4 to 8 steps, making it way faster. Plus, it requires low VRAM, so you might even run it on less powerful hardware.

There's a free demo available on Hugging Face, so you can try it out online. It's blazing fast, and I highly recommend giving it a shot if you're interested in video content.

Hunyan Image 2.0: Real-Time Image Generation

Tencent released Hunyan Image 2.0, an insane image generator that can create high-resolution images in just milliseconds. It's pretty much a real-time image generator. It also offers a real-time canvas where you can sketch or adjust your drawing and immediately see the generated results. This allows you to iterate quickly and have full control over your creations.

To try it out, you need to sign up on their official website. It's in Chinese, but with a browser like Google Chrome, you can translate the page to English.

Google's Light Lab: Transform Lighting in Photos

Google released Light Lab, a tool that can accurately change the lighting in a picture. You can make lights dimmer or brighter, change their color, and even add new lights to the scene. For example, you can turn off lights in a photo or add ambient light from outside. It even works with anime photos!

It works by detecting the lights in your photo, and you can adjust the brightness and color of each one. It also accounts for depth to accurately depict reflections and shadows. It's an impressive tool for adjusting lighting in your images with ease.

Vase: Open-Source Reference-to-Video Generator

Alibaba's Vase is another powerful tool that's now officially released. It's the most powerful free and open-source reference-to-video generator available. You can replace existing characters in a video with a new reference character, add multiple characters together, or transfer the motion of one video onto another.

The new version includes a 14-billion parameter model that can generate videos up to 1280x720 resolution. Although it requires a lot of VRAM, there's a quantized version that can run on as low as 8 GB of VRAM. If you're into video editing or animation, this could be a fantastic tool to explore.

Blip 3.0: Multimodal Image Understanding and Generation

Salesforce released Blip 3.0, a free and open-source multimodal model designed for both image understanding and generation. You can chat with it like a chatbot, ask it to analyze images, generate images, or edit them. It combines autoregressive and diffusion models.

They've released two models: one with 4 billion parameters and one with 8 billion. While it's not as good as some other models in terms of quality, it's completely free and open-source, so you can fine-tune it and experiment.

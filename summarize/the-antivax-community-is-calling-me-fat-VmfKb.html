My Experience on Jubilee

Recently, I appeared on a Jubilee episode debating vaccines. The response was unusual, I received some of the strangest comments I've ever gotten. Many focused on my appearance, particularly my weight gain and posture.

Empathy in Conversations

During the debate, I aimed to be empathetic and listen carefully. The participants had diverse beliefs, and I appreciated that they were polite and allowed me to speak. I wanted to ensure I provided accurate information and acknowledged past mistakes in the medical community.

Handling Negative Comments

After the episode aired, I noticed a shift from critiques about being a "pharma shill" to comments about my weight and posture. People called me fat, made jokes, and body shamed me. Some even questioned my credibility based on my appearance.

The Harm of Body Shaming

I'm aware that I've gained weight, and that my posture isn't perfect. But body shaming is unhelpful, and harmful. It doesn't motivate people to be healthier, it just spreads negativity. We should stop body shaming, and focus on respectful communication.

Respectful Communication Matters

I saw many unkind comments toward the participants, calling them stupid or misinformed. Disrespectful comments don't help anyone understand each other better. It's important to approach conversations with empathy and listen to different perspectives.

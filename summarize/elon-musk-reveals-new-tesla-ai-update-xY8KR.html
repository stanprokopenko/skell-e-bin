Unsupervised FSD Coming Soon

Elon Musk announced that Tesla's Full Self-Driving (FSD) has officially gone unsupervised. Thousands of Tesla vehicles have been driving themselves over thousands of miles without human intervention. Musk is confident that unsupervised FSD will launch in California and possibly other U.S. regions by the end of this year.

Launching in Austin and Beyond

Tesla plans to launch unsupervised FSD as a paid service in Austin, Texas, in June. This service will expand to other areas, with Musk stating that by 2026, owners can add or remove their cars from the robo-taxi fleet, similar to how Airbnb operates.

Progress and Safety

By the end of 2024, Tesla reached nearly 3 billion miles driven on FSD. In Q4 2024, there was one crash for every 5.94 million miles driven using autopilot technology, compared to the U.S. average of one crash every 0.7 million miles. Tesla emphasizes extreme caution and safety standards before a full rollout, aiming to be significantly safer than human drivers.

Hardware Upgrades and Licensing

Owners who purchased FSD outright will receive a complimentary hardware upgrade. Those subscribing monthly will not receive a free upgrade. Musk acknowledged that the hardware replacement process will be challenging but assured it will be done.

Tesla is seeing significant interest from other automakers in licensing FSD. They are considering partnerships where the volume will be very high, focusing on larger manufacturers due to the complexity involved.

International Challenges

In Europe, Tesla faces a layered bureaucracy for FSD approval. The approval process involves multiple government levels and could take significant time. In China, data restrictions prevent Tesla from exporting video from Chinese cars to their U.S.-based AI training centers. Tesla is using publicly available videos and simulations to train their AI for Chinese roads.

Autonomous Operations in Fremont

New Tesla vehicles from the Fremont Factory are now driving themselves autonomously for 1.2 miles from General Assembly to their loading dock without human intervention. This marks a significant step in internal autonomous operations.

Expansion of Mega Factories

Tesla is building a third Mega Pack factory. The energy business achieved a record in Q4 with its highest-ever gross profit. Q4 energy storage deployments were 11 GWh, a 224% increase from Q4 2023. The Shanghai Mega Factory construction is completed and will begin ramping up in Q1.

Upcoming Vehicles and Production

Plans for new, more affordable vehicles remain on track for production in the first half of 2025. These vehicles will utilize aspects of both the Next Generation platform and current platforms. Tesla faces a battery pack constraint but is working to address it.

Tesla Semi and Cyber Cab

Construction of the Tesla Semi Factory continued in Q4. First truck builds are scheduled to start by the end of 2025, with a ramp beginning in early 2026. The Cyber Cab, Tesla's purpose-built robotaxi, will use a revolutionary manufacturing strategy and is scheduled for volume production starting in 2026.

Cost Efficiency and New Features

In Q4, the cost per vehicle reached its lowest level ever at less than $35,000. Tesla plans to carry over new technologies from the Cybertruck to future vehicles, including:

  • 800-volt battery packs
  • Peak charging up to 500 kW
  • 48-volt electrical systems
  • Steer-by-wire and rear-wheel steering
  • Innovative glass and sound isolation techniques

Stainless Steel and Future Plans

For now, Tesla has abandoned the idea of building vehicles other than the Cybertruck in stainless steel. Future vehicles will incorporate many of the advancements introduced with the Cybertruck, enhancing performance and efficiency.

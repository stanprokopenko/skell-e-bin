Clarified Strawberry Negroni

Copy Code

Up next for Negroni week is probably one of the best drinks I've ever made: a simple milk punch inspired by the white strawberry Negroni at he Palo. This is the clarified strawberry Negroni.

Ingredients

  • 3 chopped strawberries
  • 1 oz gin
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • 1 oz Campari
  • 1/4 oz supas saw (or 1/4 oz lime or lemon juice to up the acidity)
  • 1 1/2 oz whole milk
  • Ice
  • Fresh strawberry slice for garnish

Instructions

  1. Combine three chopped strawberries and 1 oz of gin. Muddle them together.
  2. Add 1 oz of sweet vermouth, 1 oz of Campari, and 1/4 oz of supas saw (or 1/4 oz of lime or lemon juice to up the acidity).
  3. In a mixing glass, add 1 1/2 oz of whole milk.
  4. Pour your strawberry Negroni into the milk. Stir and let sit for at least 1 hour.
  5. Pour the contents of the mixing glass through a coffee filter or three layers of paper towels.
  6. When it starts to run clear, switch vessels and re-filter the cloudy punch.
  7. After it's done filtering, add 3 oz of the Negroni into a mixing glass.
  8. Drop in some ice and stir until chilled.
  9. In a rocks glass, add a nice clear cube and strain.
  10. Garnish with a slice of fresh strawberry.
  11. Cheers and enjoy!

This one is so good. If you don't like Negronis or are scared of bitter drinks, it could change your mind.

What to Use Different AI Models For

Copy Code

Choosing the right AI model can be confusing. If you've ever stared at OpenAI's model selector and wondered which one to pick, you're not alone. Today, I'll share my insights on when to use different AI models for various tasks.

GPT-40: My Go-To for Everyday Tasks

I rely on GPT-40 for most of my daily work. It's great for brainstorming, summarizing, drafting emails, and proofreading reports. Plus, it's multimodal, so it handles text, images, audio, and video.

I use GPT-40 when I need to:

  • Summarize meeting notes into action items
  • Draft follow-up emails after project kickoffs
  • Proofread reports for clarity
  • Handle general tasks quickly and efficiently

GPT-4.5: Perfect for Creative Writing

When quality writing matters, I turn to GPT-4.5. It's better at creative tasks, emotional intelligence, and offers a more collaborative approach to brainstorming.

I choose GPT-4.5 when I need to:

  • Write engaging social media posts
  • Craft product descriptions for new features
  • Develop customer apology letters with an empathetic tone
  • Produce any external-facing writing where words matter

GPT-03: For Strategic Thinking

For complex reasoning and strategic planning, GPT-03 is my preferred model. It's excellent for detailed analyses, extensive coding, and advanced math.

I use GPT-03 when I need to:

  • Develop risk analyses for market expansion
  • Draft business strategy outlines based on data
  • Run multi-step analyses on datasets
  • Brainstorm and plan with deeper insights

GPT-04 Mini and Mini High: Technical Tasks

For technical tasks, GPT-04 Mini and Mini High come in handy. They're designed for fast technical tasks, programming, and visual reasoning.

I use them when I need to:

  • Fix code snippets quickly
  • Solve complex math equations with detailed steps
  • Summarize scientific articles
  • Tackle technical tasks that require speed or depth

GPT-0.1 Pro Mode: High-Stakes Projects

When accuracy is critical, I opt for GPT-0.1 Pro Mode. It's built for tasks that need high accuracy and depth over long outputs.

I reserve it for:

  • Drafting detailed risk analysis memos
  • Creating extensive research summaries
  • Generating algorithms for financial forecasting
  • Any high-stakes project where accuracy matters most

My Cheat Sheet

In summary, here's how I choose:

  • GPT-40: For everyday tasks and multimodal needs.
  • GPT-4.5: For creative writing and high-quality outputs.
  • GPT-03: For complex reasoning and strategic planning.
  • GPT-04 Mini/Mini High: For technical tasks requiring speed or detail.
  • GPT-0.1 Pro Mode: For complex, high-stakes tasks needing accuracy

* * *

Understanding which AI model to use has transformed how I work. By selecting the right model, I get better results and save time. I hope this guide helps you navigate the options and enhances your productivity too.

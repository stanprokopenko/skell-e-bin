Episode #91 – Jeff Watts – Teaching the Next Generation of Artists!

I recently listened to a fascinating conversation with Jeff Watts, an accomplished artist and founder of Watts Atelier. His insights into the journey of mastering art, teaching, and the evolving landscape of the art world were both inspiring and thought-provoking.

The Elusive Concept of Mastery

Jeff believes that mastery is a fleeting concept. There are moments when he feels he's achieved mastery, like when he was confident he could compete with anyone in quick sketch drawing. However, maintaining mastery in multiple areas and mediums is challenging. Just like athletes, artists ebb and flow in their skills, moving from true mastery to being out of shape and back again.

The Path to Mastery

Jeff's journey began with a background in competitive athletics, which instilled in him a strong sense of discipline. This discipline translated into his art training and eventually led him to start Watts Atelier at the age of 22. He emphasizes that repetition is the only way to mastery, and that requires dedication and sacrifice.

Teaching and Legacy

Jeff finds profound fulfillment in teaching and helping others achieve their artistic goals. He views teaching as an authentic calling and takes pride in the success of his students, many of whom have become accomplished artists themselves. For him, passing on knowledge is a crucial part of his journey.

Sacrifices and Dedication

Building an atelier isn't easy, especially before the advent of the internet. Jeff made significant sacrifices, often putting his personal art career on hold to focus on teaching. He acknowledges the sting of personal sacrifices but also recognizes the poetic beauty in dedicating oneself fully to the craft.

The Atelier System

At Watts Atelier, the teaching approach is rooted in the traditional French atelier system. Students learn under the tutelage of a master until they themselves become masters. Jeff emphasizes the importance of the Riley Method, an art GPS system for navigating the human form and other subjects. This method focuses on direct, hands-on teaching, where students learn through constant practice and personalized guidance.

Mindset and Discipline

Jeff underscores the importance of mindset in achieving mastery. He looks for students who display dedication, discipline, and a genuine love for the craft. Not everyone is cut out for the intense training that the atelier requires. He advises that art students should be realistic about their goals and be prepared for the hard work ahead.

Balancing Life and Art

Achieving mastery often comes with sacrifices in other areas of life. Jeff discusses the challenges of maintaining balance, stressing that it's crucial to align one's personal values with their artistic journey. He believes that living a fulfilled life enhances one's art and that the journey is as important as the destination.

The Role of Intuition and Feeling

Jeff believes that true artistry involves a dance between intellect and intuition. Technical proficiency can be taught, but the magic that comes after is deeply personal and often elusive. Great artists feel their way through color and composition, drawing upon intuition that goes beyond academic learning.

Thoughts on AI and the Future of Art

When it comes to AI's impact on the art world, Jeff takes a pragmatic approach. He acknowledges that AI can generate images, but it doesn't replace the unique experience and satisfaction of creating art by hand. He encourages artists to focus on what AI can't replicate: the human touch, intuition, and the imperfections that make art genuine.

Embracing the Art Journey

Ultimately, Jeff's message is one of dedication, passion, and embracing the journey of art. He encourages artists to:

  • Work hard and be disciplined.
  • Embrace the challenges and sacrifices.
  • Maintain a balance between life and art.
  • Trust in intuition and personal expression.
  • Stay adaptable in the face of change.

He reminds us that art is not just about the end product but the process and growth that come with it. By staying true to ourselves and our passion, we can navigate the ever-changing landscape of the art world and find fulfillment in our creative endeavors.

Cybertruck Tunnel Takes Shape

Freshly built Cybertrucks are now emerging from the Cyber Tunnel at Tesla's Gigafactory in Texas. Drone footage captured Cybertrucks exiting through a tunnel mouth designed in the shape of a Cybertruck's front end, complete with the signature light bar.

These vehicles roll off the general assembly floor and move directly into an underground tunnel. This tunnel drives beneath a divided highway and brings the trucks to a staging lot for delivery preparations. Currently, these Cybertrucks are driven by people, but the goal is for them to self-drive through the tunnel.

The underground passage was dug by Elon Musk's Boring Company in 2024 using the Proofrock 3 boring machine. The tunnel connects to a new extension housing the end of the Cybertruck production line and a giant water-cooled supercomputer data center for autopilot development.

The real advantage comes when the Cybertrucks can autonomously move from point A to point B without human drivers. This automation could save Tesla significant time and labor costs across thousands of vehicles. There's anticipation that this transition to self-driving will happen in the first half of the year, marking a significant milestone for both Tesla and the Boring Company.

Joe Rogan's Custom Tesla Model S Plaid

Joe Rogan, proud owner of a Tesla Model S Plaid, has taken his vehicle to the next level with Unplugged Performance. Seeking a blend of class and aggression for daily driving, Rogan's Model S now features:

  • A 19-piece carbon fiber widebody kit with flared rear wheel arches, adding three inches to the car's width.
  • Lightweight UP-03 forged monoblock wheels, sized 21x10 inches in the front and 21x11.5 inches in the rear.
  • Michelin Pilot Sport 4S tires, sized 285/30 front and 305/30 rear.
  • Carbon fiber rocker panels with aerodynamic shark fins and a dramatic carbon diffuser.
  • A custom paint job in pearl white with blue hints, covered in satin paint protection film.
  • A bespoke interior featuring Von Holzhausen white bamboo leather and black Alcantara accents.

Interestingly, the leather comes from a company founded by Vicki von Holzhausen, wife of Franz von Holzhausen, Tesla's Chief of Design.

Elon Musk's Roadster Mistaken for Asteroid

Astronomers recently mistook Elon Musk's Tesla Roadster, launched into orbit in 2018, for an unusual asteroid. The car appeared less than 150,000 miles from Earth, surprising researchers who didn't expect to find a car in space. Musk quipped, "My car is orbiting Earth and Mars," highlighting the unique journey of his Roadster.

New Tesla Model Y Arrives in America

The new Tesla Model Y has arrived in the United States as the Launch Series version, a long-range all-wheel drive variant starting at $60,000. While priced higher than the previous model, it comes fully loaded with numerous features, offering added value for potential buyers.

Tesla reports receiving over 130,000 orders in China within 16 days of launch. Now, with vehicles appearing in showrooms and reviews from content creators, you can explore the significant updates firsthand. Tesla states that up to 50% of the parts are new, emphasizing their commitment to improving an already successful vehicle.

Notable enhancements include:

  • Redesigned taillights with indirect reflective body panels.
  • Powered rear seats that fold with the touch of a button.
  • Adjusted seatbelt placement for smoother seat operation.
  • Front seats that automatically adjust when rear seats fold to prevent collisions.

Model Y production is increasing, with deliveries starting soon. With its sleek design, advanced features, and Tesla's continual innovation, the new Model Y is set to impress. If you're interested, consider visiting a showroom to experience the upgrades yourself.

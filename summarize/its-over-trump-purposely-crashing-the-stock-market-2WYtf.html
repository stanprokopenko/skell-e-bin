Why the Market Is Crashing

The Impact of Tariffs

Currently, the market is experiencing a perfect storm, starting with tariffs. When tariffs are threatened or imposed, the market reacts with volatility. Initially, it drops but may recover when agreements are reached. Recent tariffs, such as the proposed 25% tariff on steel and aluminum imports and potential tariffs on Canadian dairy and lumber, have created significant uncertainty. Retaliation from countries like Canada and China increases fears of higher prices, lower GDP, and a potential recession.

Interest Rates and National Debt

Another critical factor is the rising national debt, now over $36 trillion. With increasing interest rates, the cost of servicing this debt is soaring. For the first time in history, the U.S. is spending more on interest payments than on defense. By 2025, $9.2 trillion of debt needs refinancing, potentially at higher rates. There's a theory that market crashes may be orchestrated to lower interest rates, reducing the government's borrowing costs.

Potential Severity of the Downturn

Market Corrections, Bear Markets, and Collapses

You should be aware of the three types of market declines:

  • Market Correction: A drop of at least 10%. Corrections are normal and occur every 16 months on average.

  • Bear Market: A drop of at least 20% from the peak. Bear markets typically last about a year, with an average decline of 33.2%.

  • Market Collapse: A severe drop of around 40%. In the last 120 years, this has happened only three times.

While market collapses are rare, it's important to prepare for the worst.

How to Navigate the Market

Stay Invested Despite Fear

There's always a reason not to invest, but historically, staying invested pays off. Even during downturns, consistently investing in index funds over the long term has proven successful. Market timing is challenging, and missing the market's best days can significantly reduce your returns.

Avoid Overconfidence

Overconfidence can destroy your portfolio. Recognize that keeping it simple often yields the best results. Regularly investing in diversified index funds and holding them for the long term is a proven strategy.

Practice Good Financial Habits

Good financial habits are crucial in both good and bad markets. Continue to:

  • Work extra hours or take on side hustles.
  • Cut unnecessary expenses.
  • Live below your means.
  • Invest the difference.

These practices will pay off significantly over time.

Be Prepared for Market Volatility

Market drops can be worse than expected. Stay the course, and remember that markets historically recover. Don't panic sell, instead, view downturns as opportunities to buy at lower prices.

* * *

Regardless of current market conditions, maintaining a disciplined investment strategy is key. By staying invested, avoiding overconfidence, and practicing good financial habits, you position yourself to weather the storm and potentially come out ahead when the market recovers.

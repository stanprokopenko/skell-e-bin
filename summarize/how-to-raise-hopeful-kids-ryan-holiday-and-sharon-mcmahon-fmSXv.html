The Power of Choosing Hope

In challenging times, we often feel overwhelmed by the enormity of the world's problems. However, we can choose hope even when we don't feel it. Hope isn't a feeling that descends upon us, it's a choice we make and act upon. It's from the fertile soil of hope that positive change grows, not from nihilism or cynicism.

Teaching Children to Embrace Hope

Children can understand that they can choose to do something even if they don't feel like it. We can teach them that hope is a deliberate choice, much like choosing not to act out in class or at home. It's an exercise in self-control to not let temporary circumstances derail us. By choosing hope, we empower ourselves to change how we interact with the world.

Making an Impact with Small Actions

When faced with overwhelming issues, we can rightsize the problem by focusing on our immediate community. Instead of trying to fix global problems single-handedly, we can start with actions like picking up trash in our neighborhood or supporting a local cause. As Aristotle suggested, virtues like generosity and kindness are activities we do, not just traits we possess.

Inspiring Through Relatable Role Models

It's essential to inform ourselves and our children about people who made a difference, especially those who were ordinary individuals. Learning about relatable figures shows that making a difference is accessible. These were people who, despite challenging circumstances, kept choosing the next right thing. Their stories inspire us to believe that we, too, can make an impact.

Confronting History Honestly

For too long, we've whitewashed over much of history to avoid discomfort. However, shielding children from difficult truths prevents them from developing critical thinking skills. We must present them with ideas they might disagree with to foster their ability to think critically. Protecting children from all divisive concepts only creates an inability to handle challenging ideas.

Encouraging Critical Thinking

We can't expect to develop critical thinkers if we only expose ourselves and our children to ideas we agree with. Encountering and wrestling with uncomfortable truths is essential. By doing so, we prevent feelings of disillusionment and anger that arise from discovering hidden historical truths later in life. Honest education equips children to navigate the world thoughtfully.

Building a Better Future Together

We have the power to choose the next right thing throughout our lives. Positive change doesn't require vast resources or a large following. It begins with small, deliberate actions driven by hope and determination. Just as someone might see the potential in a dilapidated house and transform it, we can bring our skills and determination to make a difference in our communities.

By choosing hope and acting on it, we move forward, inspired to create a better future for ourselves and others.

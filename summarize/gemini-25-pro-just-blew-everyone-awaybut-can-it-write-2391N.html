I recently had the chance to test out Google's Gemini 2.5 Pro Experimental model to see how it performs in creative writing tasks. As someone always on the lookout for tools to aid in brainstorming, outlining, and prose generation, I put this model through my qualitative rubric to see how it stacks up.

Testing Gemini 2.5 Pro Experimental

Gemini 2.5 Pro boasts a 1 million token context window and a 66k max output, which is impressive. Currently, it's free to use via Google's AI Studio or OpenRouter, though you might experience some throttling due to high demand.

One nifty feature is the ability to adjust safety settings in AI Studio. By tweaking these, I could explore the model's capabilities more thoroughly. However, it still has some content restrictions, so it's not entirely unrestricted.

Brainstorming and Outlining

Logline Creation

I tasked the model with generating 20 epic fantasy loglines in the style of Blake Snyder. It delivered, with 10 solid ideas that had creative twists. For example, one logline described a hero whose "lack of chosenness is the only thing that can save the world," which I found intriguing.

Outline Generation

When it came to outlining, Gemini 2.5 Pro shone brightly. It produced a detailed 42-chapter outline in a single prompt, something not all models can manage. The chapters were well fleshed out, although it did lean on some common fantasy names like "Elara" and "Lara." Towards the end, the detail lessened slightly, but overall, it was a robust outline.

Prose Generation

I put the model through several prose prompts:

  • Basic Prose Prompt: Generated 411 words, with 272 words being usable. It occasionally overused adjectives and leaned into unnecessary drama but was generally solid.
  • Complex Prose Prompt: With added context, it produced 321 words, most of which were of good quality.
  • Dialogue Prose Prompt: This prompt yielded 570 words, with 492 words being usable, showcasing its strength in handling dialogue.
  • Editing Prompt: Uniquely, it offered two versions of the edited text. The second version expanded the original 349 words to 489 words, enhancing the quality and readability.

Comparison with Other Models

In my testing, Gemini 2.5 Pro came close to Claude 3.7 Sonnet, which has been my favorite all-around model. While PseudoWrite's Muse model excels in prose quality and allows for more explicit content, it's limited to prose and requires a subscription.

Gemini 2.5 Pro outperformed others in brainstorming and outlining, rivaling Claude 3.7 Sonnet in several areas. It even surpassed it in tasks like creating ad headlines, where it generated seven strong headlines compared to Claude's six.

* * *

Gemini 2.5 Pro Experimental is a formidable tool for creative writers. It handles brainstorming, outlining, and prose generation with ease. While it may not entirely dethrone Claude 3.7 Sonnet or rival the prose quality of Muse, it comes impressively close.

Considering it's currently free and accessible, I'd recommend giving it a try. Whether you're crafting outlines or generating prose, this model offers valuable assistance. Just be mindful of the content filters and potential throttling due to high usage. Overall, it's a strong addition to the AI writing landscape.

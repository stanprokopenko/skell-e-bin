Stop Ineffective Learning Habits

Most people don't know how to learn. They overload their brains with podcasts, videos, books, and social media. Six months later, they have nothing to show for it. Learning has become a form of mental masturbation, cheap dopamine from consuming information without application. You'll forget most of it by tomorrow.

Here's the harsh truth: You're learning slow. If you could learn ten times faster, you could achieve success sooner. It's my belief that you're wasting time. You can probably learn what you need in two weeks, while others take six months.

Step 1: Create a Map of Your Ideal Life

The first step to learning how to learn is to create an aim for your learning. Most tutorials skip this, but it's crucial. If what you're learning doesn't connect to your current skills or the life you want, you won't be motivated. You'll need more discipline and continue to hate learning like in school.

  • Dissect your day: Break down your daily activities and how you feel about them.
  • Identify what you want and don't want in life.
  • Set clear goals: Determine what skills and knowledge you need to achieve them.
  • Eliminate distractions standing in your way.

By outlining your ideal life, you frame your mind for what you need to learn.

Step 2: Outline a Project

The best way to learn is to build a real-world project and only search for information when you need it. Here's how:

  1. Choose something to build that moves you toward your goals.
  2. Create a document and brain dump everything that comes to mind.
  3. Save 3 to 5 sources of inspiration you want to emulate.
  4. Study those sources and break down their structure.
  5. Outline the project into sections, milestones, and what you need to know.
  6. Capture ideas as they come to mind.

A project can be anything: a business, a piece of art, a website, or even your health.

Step 3: Start with What You Know

Learning comes from struggle, not memorization. Start even if you know nothing. Try to take the first step. When you don't know what to do, search for the answer or ask AI.

Follow this process:

  • Start.
  • Try and fail.
  • Search for answers as needed.
  • Implement the answers.
  • Repeat until the project is complete.
  • Ask experts if you can't find solutions.

Enhancing Your Learning with AI

AI can be a powerful tool to speed up your learning. Use prompts to get personalized assistance:

  • Strategic Advisor: Get brutally honest guidance to achieve your goals.
  • Study Regimen: Create a tailored learning plan with books, videos, and action steps.
  • Ask specific questions: Use AI to solve problems as they arise during your project.

For example, if you're stuck in Photoshop, ask, "How do I remove the background of an image in Photoshop?" Implement the solution and solidify your learning.

Structuring Your Learning Time

Organize your learning with focused blocks:

  1. Building (30-90 minutes): Work on your project and learn as needed.
  2. Learning (30-60 minutes): Follow your study regimen and take notes.
  3. Walking (30 minutes): Reflect and absorb information while walking.

No one will give you the time to learn, you have to take it. Schedule these blocks, and turn them into a ritual.

The Power of Teaching What You Learn

To solidify your knowledge, teach what you learn. This leverages:

  • The Feynman Technique: Explain concepts in simple terms as if teaching someone with no prior knowledge.
  • The Protege Effect: Teaching others helps you learn more deeply.

Write a newsletter or post on social media about what you're learning. This not only enhances your learning but also:

  • Attracts people who care about what you're building.
  • Creates opportunities for support, collaboration, or even monetization.

* * *

Learning effectively is about intentional practice:

  • Set clear goals aligned with your ideal life.
  • Engage in project-based learning to apply what you learn.
  • Use AI tools to assist and accelerate your learning.
  • Teach others to deepen your understanding.

By following these steps, you'll learn more in less time and move confidently toward the future you want to create.

President Trump's Realism

I've never felt more optimistic about the United States than I do right now, and that's thanks to President Donald J. Trump. What's intriguing is that he's not an ideological conservative. While I grew up reading thinkers like Thomas Sowell and Friedrich Hayek, I doubt President Trump has. Yet, he's the most successful conservative president of my lifetime.

Why? Because he lives in the world of reality.

Success Through Practicality

Economic Dynamism

President Trump cares about success and economic dynamism. He understands that to thrive, American businesses need freedom from excessive regulations. As a businessman who's made payroll, he knows the importance of innovation and meritocracy over identity politics.

Cutting Waste and Bureaucracy

Instead of commissioning studies, he takes action. He empowers others, like Elon Musk, to cut waste and overhaul inefficient programs. This business-minded approach is something Washington has lacked.

Confronting the Left's Disconnect

The Democratic Party has become disconnected from reality. They rely on the federal government as a permanent payment program for their allies. President Trump disrupted this pipeline, focusing instead on what works.

Realistic Foreign Policy

Practical Solutions for Global Challenges

When President Trump looks at situations like Ukraine, he seeks practical solutions. He acknowledges the stalemate and aims for a realistic off-ramp, rather than endless conflict without clear goals.

Middle East Breakthroughs

In the Middle East, he broke from traditional State Department wisdom. By deprioritizing the Palestinian issue, peace broke out. He proposes bold solutions for regions like Gaza, recognizing the reality on the ground.

Upholding Basic Truths

President Trump isn't afraid to state the obvious:

  • Boys are not girls.
  • Masculinity is valuable.
  • Being a wife and mother is commendable.

These truths were uncontroversial not long ago, but the left has abandoned them. Most Americans want safety, prosperity, and the freedom to worship without government interference.

Our Role in Building America

So, what can we do? Beyond political battles, we need to live reality-based lives. That means:

  • Being role models for our children.
  • Building strong communities.
  • Creating businesses and fostering innovation.
  • Engaging in everyday virtues.

President Trump is clearing the field for us. It's our job to build upon it. The country's success depends not just on leaders but on everyday people like us doing our part.

Continuing to Build Together

The political fight never ends, but our commitment to building must be constant. Even as victories come and go, we should focus on creating and sustaining what makes America thrive. With President Trump's embrace of reality, we're in a better position to do just that.

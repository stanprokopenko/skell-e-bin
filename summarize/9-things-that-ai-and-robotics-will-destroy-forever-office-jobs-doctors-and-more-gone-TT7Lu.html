As I look to the future, I find it's easier to predict what will vanish rather than what's new on the horizon. Here are nine things I believe will disappear within the next 5 to 20 years.

The End of Aging

I believe that rejuvenation therapies will soon make aging a thing of the past. Instead of just extending life, we'll be able to reverse aging, making us biologically younger. Imagine not seeing old people anymore because everyone looks and feels youthful, regardless of their chronological age.

The End of Fossil Fuels

With advances in solar energy, fusion, and nuclear power, we'll move beyond the need for fossil fuels. Future energy solutions will be cleaner and more efficient, possibly even utilizing hydrogen fuels for transportation and other needs.

The Death of Office Work

The traditional office job is on its way out. AI and automation will reduce the need for humans in many roles, and remote work has proven we can be productive without being in a physical office. This shift will also impact city planning and may lead to the decline of suburbs as we know them.

Hollywood as We Know It

The entertainment industry is changing rapidly. AI-generated content will become the norm, reducing the need for traditional Hollywood productions. While practical effects and on-set filming will still exist, most digital entertainment will be produced differently.

The End of Language Barriers

Universal translators will eliminate language barriers. Soon, we'll be able to communicate with anyone in any language, in real time. This technology will make the world feel even smaller and enhance cultural exchange.

The End of Traditional Government Roles

As AI surpasses human intelligence, it's possible that human-held government positions will become obsolete. AI and blockchain technologies could lead to more efficient and transparent governance, potentially replacing roles like congresspeople and senators.

The End of Doctors as We Know Them

AI is already outperforming human doctors in many ways. Combined with regenerative medicine and rejuvenation therapies, our approach to healthcare will change dramatically. We may see robotic doctors making house calls, providing faster and more accurate care.

The Transformation of Education

AI and robotics will revolutionize education. Personalized learning will replace traditional classrooms, allowing students to explore their interests and advance at their own pace. This shift will foster curiosity and intrinsic motivation rather than standardized curricula.

The End of Social Suffering

Issues like homelessness, mental illness, and poverty could diminish significantly. With technological advancements, we can address these problems more effectively, leading to a society where basic needs are met and people can live healthier, more fulfilling lives.

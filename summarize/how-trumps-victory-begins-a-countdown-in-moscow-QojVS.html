Trump's Victory and Its Impact

Trump has won a second term, and the Democrats' blue wall crumbled again. This outcome has major implications for the war in Ukraine. While many in the U.S. debate why the Harris campaign failed, the rest of the world is focused on how this changes the strategic landscape.

Russia's Accelerated Offensive

Since November 2023, I've noticed that Russia has intensified its offensive in Ukraine. The pace has increased dramatically, with 539 square kilometers gained in October alone. However, Russian casualties and equipment losses have also spiked. They're pushing harder, accepting higher losses to gain more ground.

Anticipating a Trump-Imposed Peace

I believe Russia's escalation is due to anticipating a Trump-imposed peace plan. Trump has expressed a desire to end the war, potentially by freezing the front lines and cutting aid to Ukraine if they refuse a settlement. Knowing this, Russia wants to capture as much territory as possible before any peace deal locks in the status quo.

The Challenge of November Weather

Complicating matters, November brings rain and mud to Ukraine, making advances difficult. The infamous rasputitsa turns battlefields into bogs. Russia hoped to reach key objectives like Pokrovsk before the weather turned, aiming for winter sieges rather than muddy maneuvers.

What's Next?

With Trump's victory certain, it feels like now or never for Russia. They might accelerate their efforts despite the weather. Conversely, if Harris had won, Russia might have pulled back, adjusting to a longer war without the urgency.

* * *

Russia faces a race against time, battling both the approaching winter and the geopolitical shifts from the U.S. election. The next few months will be critical in determining the course of the war in Ukraine.

I'm Dr. Mike from Renaissance Periodization, and today we're discussing supplements for hypertrophy and strength training. This guide is for those who lift weights, eat well, and want effective supplements. We'll focus on legal and safe supplements, excluding illegal substances like steroids and growth hormones.

Protein Shakes

Protein shakes are effective and come in different types:

Whey Protein - Fast-digesting, good for post-workout. - The quality (concentrate, isolate, hydrolysate) doesn't matter much for most people. - Isolate or hydrolysate is preferred for those with lactose intolerance.

Casein Protein - Slow-digesting, good for long intervals between meals.

Mixed Proteins, Egg Protein, and Soy Protein - Medium-digesting, good for meal replacements. - Soy protein does not feminize men and is beneficial for muscle growth.

Serving Size - Calculate based on total daily protein intake divided by the number of meals.

Workout Carbohydrates

Workout carbs are optional but effective. They prevent muscle breakdown and enhance workout intensity.

Types - Dextrose powder, Gatorade, or Powerade. - Best consumed during or after workouts, paired with a protein shake. - The amount depends on workout volume, intensity, and overall diet.

Usage - One bottle of Gatorade or Powerade and a protein shake is sufficient for most people. - Larger individuals or those training intensely might need two bottles.

Slow Digesting Carbohydrates - Waxy maize, made from corn, digests slower than oatmeal. - Can be used as a meal replacement when paired with protein. - Mix waxy maize and protein powder in a shaker for a convenient meal replacement.

Creatine

Creatine is a well-vetted supplement that supports muscle growth, performance, and brain health.

Creatine Monohydrate - Best-studied form. - A daily dose of 5 grams is effective for most people. - Loading phase takes about a week to a week and a half. - Especially beneficial for those who consume little to no red meat.

Benefits and Safety - Small but positive changes in physique and performance. - Safe to take continuously, but breaks can be taken for convenience. - Not harmful to kidneys. - Can cause water weight gain, which is normal. - Safe for those over 15, helps with body composition, strength, and recovery. - Doesn't have immediate noticeable effects like caffeine or alcohol.

Multivitamins/Multiminerals

Multivitamins/multiminerals are recommended as an insurance policy to cover nutritional gaps.

Usage - Cheap, easy to find, taken once a day. - Overconsumption can lead to expensive urine (water-soluble vitamins) or toxicity (fat-soluble vitamins). - Expensive brands offer no additional benefits over cheaper brands.

Supplement Recommendations by Training Level

Beginners (up to 3 years) - Don't need supplements beyond a multivitamin.

Intermediates (3-7 years) - Consider supplements based on specific needs.

Advanced Trainers (7+ years) - Consider supplements on a case-by-case basis, possibly with a sports nutritionist's guidance.

Pre-Workouts and Caffeine

Pre-workouts and caffeine can be used but are not necessary. Stick to recommended dosages.

* * *

Supplements can enhance results marginally and should complement hard work. Remember, the key is consistency and dedication in your training and nutrition.

Choice of Reference and Beginner Guidance

Enhancing Abstract Elements

I noticed a suggestion of a shape in the corner, which I accidentally created. I decided to enhance it for an abstract feel. I aim to be transparent about every step, adding color notes in the background for interest.

Color Mixing and Palette

I use a large brush, despite needing new ones, and I dislike ordering art supplies online. I mix colors without black, aiming for a brighter note. I use a combination of "ketchup and mustard" to create an orange hue and experiment with yellow ocher and black to create a green tone. My decisions are based on intuition rather than a specific rationale. The painting uses only two colors, which is impressive and should be noted.

Creating Variety and Interest

Variety is crucial to maintaining viewer interest and preventing the painting from being boring. I look for spots near the head to add variety with harder edges, avoiding uniformity. I mix green with black and yellow, adding a touch of orange for a semi-hard edge near the ear. I focus on creating variety with edges, likening them to musical notes that enhance the painting's interest.

Portrait of Ethan Espinosa

The painting features a portrait of Ethan Espinosa, a friend and artist, who allowed me to use his likeness. I discuss the challenge of making portraits of unknown people interesting through painting techniques. As a fine artist, I worried about selling portraits of unknown individuals, unlike recognizable figures like Arnold Schwarzenegger. Artists often avoid direct eye contact in portraits to make them more relatable and less specific. However, I enjoy painting eye contact, imagining historical significance in portraits.

Appeal and Techniques

The painting's appeal lies in how it's painted, not just the subject, making it desirable to collectors. I add color notes to emphasize the limited palette and create the illusion of more colors. The painting was created with a limited palette, using graded colors to mimic realistic skin tones. Adding pure color enhances the painting's realism and showcases the potential of a limited palette. Introducing blue to the palette would expand the color range significantly.

Importance of Fundamental Elements

Limiting the palette forces reliance on fundamental painting tools like shapes, values, edges, and color. I emphasize the importance of design, drawing, and other elements in creating a strong painting.

Artistic Adjustments

I add a sharp edge behind the head for contrast, deviating from the photo for artistic effect. I adjust Ethan's hair in the painting to correct a shape I disliked in the photo, ensuring it aligns with his hairstyle.

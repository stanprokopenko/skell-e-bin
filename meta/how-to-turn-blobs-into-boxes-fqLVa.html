Understanding the Three Sets of Lines

In your journey from blobs to boxes, focus on the three primary sets of lines:

  • Left to Right Lines
  • Up and Down Lines
  • Front to Back Lines

When transforming a blob into a box, ensure each line corresponds to these directions. Every line should align with the front to back lines, stopping where they intersect the up and down lines. This approach simplifies complex forms into manageable shapes.

Foreshortening and Overlap

Foreshortening is a crucial concept. As objects tilt and turn, they appear compressed. Pay attention to how curves and planes behave when viewed from different angles. For instance, when looking directly at a plane, it may not reveal its depth, but shifting your perspective introduces foreshortening.

Incorporate overlaps to enhance depth. When a fleshy form bends, one side stretches while the other squashes. Emphasizing this squash and stretch conveys a sense of form moving in space.

Avoid Worrying About Proportions and Vanishing Points

At this stage, don't get bogged down by proportions or vanishing points. The goal is to simplify forms and understand their basic structure. Focusing too much on precision can hinder your progress. Proportions will become important in later lessons, so for now, concentrate on capturing the essence of the form.

Choosing Subjects You Enjoy

Select objects that interest you. Whether it's armor, a beetle, or everyday items, drawing subjects you love keeps you motivated. When you're passionate about the subject, you're more likely to enjoy the process and improve your skills.

Daring to Foreshorten

Challenge yourself by attempting extreme foreshortening. It's a bold move that pushes your understanding of perspective. Don't shy away from difficult angles or complex forms. Embrace the opportunity to experiment and learn from mistakes.

Learning from Others

Observe how fellow students tackle assignments. Sharing work and helping each other enriches the learning experience. For example, some students create teaching pages or provide step-by-step explanations. Engaging with your peers fosters a supportive community and offers new insights.

Looking Ahead

In upcoming lessons, you'll:

  • Extrude your name in space, turning it into a finished illustration.
  • Explore no-point perspective, diving deeper into orthographic drawings.
  • Understand the three line systems and how they create complex forms.
  • Learn to estimate proportions and use grids to control vanishing points.

Remember, the focus now is on building a strong foundation. Enjoy the journey, keep practicing, and don't hesitate to take risks in your drawings. Your efforts now will pay off as you advance to more complex concepts.

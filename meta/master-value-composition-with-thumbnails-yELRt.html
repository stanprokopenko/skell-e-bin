Project Overview

You'll apply value composition principles by creating thumbnail sketches from the two provided reference photos. Simplify each image into just three values in small thumbnails (about the size of your thumbnail or palm).

Creating Thumbnails

  • Simplify: Focus on capturing the main idea without details.
  • Iterate: Create at least five variations for each photo.
  • Experiment: Modify value groupings and rearrange value shapes.

Tips for Effective Thumbnails

  • Keep It Simple: Start small and avoid adding details. If you overcomplicate, try again and simplify.
  • Explore Multiple Ideas: Don't settle on your first idea. Often, initial concepts aren't the best.
  • Ask "What If?": Experiment with backgrounds, clothing values, and cropping. You have the freedom to adjust these elements.

Tools and Techniques

Pencils

  • Use a dull pencil to draw larger value masses and avoid details.
  • Pencils allow erasing and adjusting shapes and values.

Markers

  • Markers are faster and provide predefined values for clear separation.
  • They encourage simplification due to their thick tips.

Choose whichever tool you're comfortable with, or try both for their unique benefits.

Level Two Challenge

After warming up with the first photos, move on to the Level Two images. These are more complex and offer more exploration.

  • Develop Further: After several iterations, select a favorite thumbnail to refine. Create a slightly larger sketch with more details and up to five values.

Final Reminders

  • Prioritize Simplicity and Clarity: Don't worry about perfect proportions or details.
  • Focus on Values: Make deliberate decisions about value groups and execute them confidently.
  • Guide the Viewer: Simplify, control your value range, and create a clear hierarchy.

Good luck!

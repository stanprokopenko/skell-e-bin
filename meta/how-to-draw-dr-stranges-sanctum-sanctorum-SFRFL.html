Infusing Personality into Building Exteriors

Infusing personality into the exterior of buildings is challenging but essential. Aim to make the building dynamic, fun, and fitting within the Marvel Universe. Focus on understanding the exterior and making it impactful and memorable.

Composition Techniques

Approach the composition with flexibility, not worrying too much about the end result initially. Use a low vanishing point and experiment with shapes, such as a circular window. Avoid centering the building too much in the frame to keep it interesting. Highlight key features like the circular window to make the building recognizable.

Shape, Size, and Scale

Consider shape, size, scale, and the relation of shapes to ensure the location is important in the story. Use extreme three-point perspective to make the building feel otherworldly. Mix regular elements with the crazy architecture of the sanctum sanctorum.

Sketching and Planning

Sketch loosely to plan the composition before making a grid. Ensure all parts are in their proper place in the composition. Use a 10-point divider for perspective drawing and technical illustration.

Inking and Adding Detail

Start inking and adding detail without a ton of purpose initially. Balance detail and shape to make the building look good. Marry reference and personal vision to create a strong image.

Refining the Image

Trust your gut and pursue images with potential, even if challenging. Develop a visual shorthand to avoid getting bogged down in every detail. Focus on big details like top windows and the shape of the building. Use whiteout to correct and bring out shapes. Avoid perfection; aim for a dynamic and fun image.

Balancing Story and Visuals

Allow room for characters to breathe in the story. Balance drawing amazing images with making a good story. Use textured whiteout for trees and other elements.

Final Touches and Readability

Prioritize readability before sending the work to a colorist. Erase and refine details to make the image sing. Accept that the process can be messy but aim for personality and visual interest.

Embracing Challenges

Dive in headfirst to challenging compositions to improve. Struggle with difficult compositions to get better. Move on to the next lesson or page if frustrated, knowing improvement comes gradually.

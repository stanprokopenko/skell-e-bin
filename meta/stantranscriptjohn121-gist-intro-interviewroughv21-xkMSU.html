My Path into Comic Covers

I'm Eric Gist, also known as EMGIST. Over the past 15 years, I've had the privilege of working on various comic covers for Marvel, including Fantastic Four, Iron Fist, Wolverine, and Hulk. Two of my favorites are the Fantastic Four cover, where I enjoyed incorporating the family dynamic into a single image, and the Hulk cover, which featured an action scene between Wolverine and Hulk—something many comic artists aspire to illustrate.

I started passionately pursuing comics as a kid around eight years old, initially focusing on interiors. After numerous submissions and rejections, I realized I had a knack for single-image storytelling and shifted my focus to covers. My first cover came out in 2007, and I began working with Marvel in 2016, starting with the Haunted Mansion series.

My Artistic Style and Influences

I'm known for deep shadows, high contrast, and a moody, noir, or horror feel in my work. Recently, I've been branching out into brighter pieces, like the Fantastic Four and Iron Fist covers, adapting my style to suit the characters while maintaining narrative depth.

I draw inspiration from a wide range of artists, both contemporary and historical. I admire artists who balance iconic imagery with deeper storytelling, aiming to create pieces that leave a lasting impression. Studying masters like Caravaggio, Rembrandt, and Norman Rockwell helps me strive for a timeless quality in my art.

Advice for Aspiring Comic Artists

For those looking to break into comics, I recommend you specialize initially—become really good at one thing before branching out. It's crucial to focus on your fundamentals, including figure drawing, anatomy, color theory, perspective, and composition. Comics are about figures and people; being able to draw dynamic characters is essential.

Don't force your style; let it emerge naturally from your influences and experiences. Embrace limitations—they can breed creativity and force you to find unique solutions. When creating covers, thumbnail extensively to move beyond the obvious ideas and dig deeper into originality.

Study a wide variety of artists, not just in comics but across the history of art. Make covers, even if they're not perfect at first. Practice is key to improvement.

Remember, you need to be exceptionally good to stand out in the industry. Work hard to ensure your art not only meets but exceeds the standards, so you're ready when opportunities arise.

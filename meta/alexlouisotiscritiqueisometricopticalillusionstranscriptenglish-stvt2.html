Embracing the Tools of the Trade

We've had an incredible response to the perspective course assignments, with over 250 submissions. I must say, reviewing them has been a delight, thank you for making my job fun!

Many of you are getting familiar with instruments like straight edges and triangles. Using these tools can feel clunky at first, but with practice, they become extensions of your hand. Even if you don't intend to use them professionally, spending time mastering these tools can't do you any harm, you might even find you enjoy them.

The Value of Copying and Originality

Copying is a valuable way to learn technique. Just like actors might imitate others to hone their skills, artists can learn a lot by replicating existing works. However, don't stop at copying. Challenge yourself to create original pieces, drawing from personal experiences and ideas.

Tackling Optical Illusions

Creating optical illusions is a fascinating exercise. It involves balancing shapes and forms to create possible or impossible objects. Start by roughing out your ideas. This helps avoid headaches later on. Remember, improvising can lead to creative breakthroughs, even if it means taking a few wrong turns along the way.

Understanding Possible vs. Impossible

Sometimes, an object may seem impossible, but with closer analysis, it can make sense. For example, by adjusting lines and perspectives, a shape that appears contradictory can be resolved into a plausible structure. Analyzing your work critically helps you understand these nuances.

Precision Matters

When using tools, precision is key. Every line and point must be exact to avoid weak links in your drawing. Lock down your rulers and triangles to prevent shifts that can throw off your work. This discipline not only improves your technical skills but also enhances the clarity of your illustrations.

Shading and Line Quality

While shading isn't the primary focus right now, practicing hand pressure control is beneficial. Starting lightly and gradually increasing pressure can improve your line quality significantly. If you're interested in diving deeper into shading techniques, consider exploring courses dedicated to that skill.

Motivation and Applications

I understand that creating complex optical illusions can feel like crafting "novelty parlor tricks." However, optical illusions can be powerful storytelling tools. They challenge perceptions and can add depth to narratives. Look for examples in graphic novels or illustrations where illusions enhance the story, you might find inspiration there.

Continual Learning and Collaboration

Some of you have found these exercises meditative, and rewarding. Engaging in these projects not only strengthens your skills, but also connects you with a community of peers stretching their brains together. I encourage you to continue exploring and sharing your work. There's much to learn from each other.

Looking Ahead

This is just the beginning. The challenges ahead will push you further, from orthographic projections to contraption inventions. Embrace the process - it's all about balancing shapes and forms, and enjoying the journey of learning and creation.

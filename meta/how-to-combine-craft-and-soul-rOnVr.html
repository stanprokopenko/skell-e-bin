A Holistic Approach

I like to engage both head and heart, craft and creativity, and skill sets and mindset when I teach art. I want to find ways to see the science-y craft in a simpler, childlike manner, so I’m less overwhelmed and more open to new ideas. My goal is to share practical tools you can apply to any art course, a foundation that supports your skill-building and helps you find your unique creative voice.

The Meaning Behind Art

We live in societies built largely on fear and self-preservation. Art cuts through those worries, tapping into our heart-centered truths, where we find real purpose and motivation. I believe art stands alongside spiritual traditions in its power to connect us and make us feel less alone. Viewed this way, art becomes more than a craft, it’s a wisdom strategy that can bring healing and help us see bigger possibilities in life.

Finding Connection

If life is full of disconnection, then art provides reconnection. On a simple craft level, we’re always uniting separate pieces, foreground to background, warm colors to cool, and line to tone. On a deeper level, we’re also bridging gaps in our lives, our relationships, our ideas, our sense of identity. Art can show us how beautiful life can be, or it can help us process trauma and loss. By creating, we regain a piece of that wholeness we felt before we learned we were separate.

Craft as Connective Strategies

Whether we’re drawing a figure, painting a portrait, or sculpting a form, our art techniques can be seen as connective strategies. We weave light and shadow, color and form, story and characters into a seamless experience for the viewer. From a heart-centered place, these skills become less stressful to learn, and we free ourselves to experiment, play, and enjoy the process.

Embracing Our Creative Birthright

Humans are incredibly creative, we remake our world each day. Still, our educational systems rarely encourage a deep exploration of that creativity. Yet, maintaining an art or spiritual practice makes us feel more optimistic and alive. Art helps us notice the little things, like how the light falls on a person standing at a window, and reminds us of how amazing it is to be awake to the beauty all around us.

The Journey Ahead

Art, at its best, can heal and inspire us to be better, not just at our craft, but in how we connect with each other and live our lives. I plan to share some emotional and mindset strategies from high-performance practices in other fields, and weave them into our art practice. By uniting craft and creativity, we can create art that’s personal, meaningful, and powerful. I look forward to guiding you deeper into this adventure.

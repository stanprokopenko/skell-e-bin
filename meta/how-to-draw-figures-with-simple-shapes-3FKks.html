Embracing the Fundamentals

Revisiting the fundamentals is always valuable. Don't see drawing as a daunting task. Instead, enjoy the process and take your time. Whenever you pause, let it be a thought. Never rush your drawing. The language of drawing is about shapes and rhythms. By exploring simple ways to build a standing figure, you can turn it in space and view it from all angles.

The Power of Blind Contour Drawing

To overcome fear and stiffness in your drawings, try starting with blind contour drawing. This means not looking at your page while you draw. As you move your hand around, wonderful and unexpected things happen. It's a liberating exercise that proves what's inside you is inherently beautiful. Remember, long, light, and loose. Start with lines that flow, and think big to small.

Understanding Proportions

Mastering proportions is essential. Think in terms of halves, quarters, and thirds. For instance, the body can be divided in half at the hip bone, known as the great trochanter. Use mnemonic memory clues to remember shapes. The egg represents the rib cage, and the Liberty Bell symbolizes the hips. These aren't exact shapes but ballparks to work within.

Building Figures from the Inside Out

Always build your figure from the inside out. Start with big shapes, and then move to smaller details. Let the gesture dominate the drawing and allow the beauty inside you to shine. Avoid starting with features like the breasts or hands, focus on the foundational forms first. By understanding these internal structures, you can draw from imagination without always needing a model.

The Importance of Repetition

Repetition is key to improvement. Draw the same figure from multiple angles. By doing lots of starts, you learn more in the early stages than anywhere else in the drawing. This method, akin to learning a language, embeds knowledge in your subconscious art engine through the power of osmosis.

Contrapposto and Asymmetry

Explore the concept of contrapposto, which means counterbalance. This involves the pinch and stretch sides of the figure. The weight-bearing leg causes the shoulders to tilt, creating a shorter side (pinch), and a longer side (stretch). Embrace asymmetry in your drawings, it makes them more lively and beautiful. Sharing Knowledge and Building Community

Art thrives on community. Share what you know with others, even if you're just starting. Teaching reinforces your own understanding. Joining life drawing classes helps you meet like-minded people. Good models are invaluable, so get to know them and the art community around you. Learning from others and teaching can make you a better artist.

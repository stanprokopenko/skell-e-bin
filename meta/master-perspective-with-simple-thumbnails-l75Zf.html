Thumbnailing: Starting Small Without Worry

For drawings like these, I start by thumbnailing small sketches. I'm not concerned with accuracy at this stage. I make notes to remind myself of the problems I need to solve. Even when I hadn't given thought to certain angled lines, I realized later that the lines would be out there.

But here's the thing, nobody sees these preliminary sketches. They're like those of Leonardo da Vinci, without the grace, power, intrigue, majesty, or skill. But apart from those five things, it is exactly the same kind of thinking. Thumbnails don't need to worry about grace, power, majesty, skill, or even style. They get us started.

Using Instruments

Copy Code

Early Inspiration

I'm Eric Gist, also known as EMGIST. Since age eight, I've loved comics. Though I started wanting to draw interiors, training with Jeff Watts showed me my knack for single-image storytelling.

Breaking into the Industry

At Comic-Con, I shifted focus from interiors to covers. It took ten years to become proficient and another five to get noticed.

Notable Works

I've created various covers, mostly variants. My favorites include:

  • Fantastic Four: Capturing the family dynamic in one image.
  • Hulk: An action scene of Hulk vs. zombie Wolverine.

My first cover was in 2007. I began with Marvel in 2016 on Haunted Mansion covers. In 2019, I did a Marvel character cover for Crazy's anniversary issue.

Developing My Style

Known for deep shadows and a moody feel, I've recently explored brighter themes in Fantastic Four and Iron Fist covers. I aim for images that are iconic and tell a story.

Advice for Aspiring Artists

Specialize First

Master one thing before branching out. In comics, dynamic figures are essential.

Study the Masters

Look beyond comics to artists like Bouguereau, Caravaggio, and Rembrandt.

Focus on Fundamentals

Work on figure drawing, anatomy, lighting, composition, color theory, and perspective. Practice by making covers.

Let Your Style Emerge

Don't force a style; let it develop naturally.

Embracing Limitations

Limitations breed creativity. Constraints force creative solutions. Use thumbnails; initial ideas aren't always best.

Final Thoughts

I wish I'd known how good you have to be. I still strive to improve. Our job is to create images that sell books. Keep pushing yourself.

How to Paint Realistic Faces Using Edges

Edges are a powerful tool in art, essential for creating depth and interest in a composition. There are four types of edges: hard, firm, soft, and lost. Each type plays a unique role in how we perceive and interpret a painting.

Types of Edges

Hard Edges

Hard edges are typically found in cast shadows, such as those from the nose or neck. They occur when a light area is silhouetted against a dark background, or vice versa. These edges are easy to identify and add a sharp contrast to the artwork.

Firm Edges

Firm edges are more defined than soft edges and are often found in areas with more bone, like the bridge of the nose. They provide a clear transition between light and shadow, adding structure to the form.

Soft Edges

Soft edges appear in rounded areas like the cheekbone, where light turns into shadow smoothly. These edges create a gentle transition and add a sense of volume and roundness to the form.

Lost Edges

Lost edges occur when the background and the subject have the same value, causing them to blend together. These edges provide a rest for the eye and can create interesting contrasts with other edges, adding depth and intrigue to the composition.

Application of Edges in Art

To explore these edges, consider a head lit from above to the side. This lighting setup allows us to observe the variety of edges on the face. The interplay of different edges creates a musical quality of notes, varying between hard and soft. This variation can tell a story about form, enhancing the visual narrative of the artwork.

* * *

Understanding and recognizing these edges is crucial in art. By observing and using edges effectively, we can enhance the visual storytelling in our paintings. Edges not only define shapes but also contribute to the overall composition, making them an indispensable element in art.

Preserving Line Art in Digital Painting

When you open these JPEG files with black line art on a white background, you can paint without destroying the line art:

  1. Duplicate the layer and hide the duplicate.
  2. Fill the background layer with a color (e.g., gray) using Option + Delete on Mac.
  3. Set the copied layer to Multiply mode. This makes white transparent and keeps black opaque.
  4. Now, you can draw underneath the line art to experiment with lighting.

This technique lets you paint beneath the line art without altering it.

Balancing Art Brain and Business Brain

The real reason I'm going to change things has a lot to do with something I like to call my art brain fighting my business brain. My art brain is the side of me that just wants to make stuff for myself, explore concepts, and do things purely for fun, an important part of being an artist. It's maybe equally important to the other half of my brain, which is the business brain. Business brain asks questions like,

  • Who's the audience for this?
  • Would I want it on a wall?
  • Would this appeal to a studio I'm trying to work for?

All the considerations that make what I'm doing fit into the rest of the world so I can survive and exist as an artist who makes work people want to consume.

I like this thumbnail the most, but I don't know if I'd put it on my own wall as it is right now. So I'm going to make changes to appeal more to the business side of my brain while maintaining the artistic goals of my art brain.

I'm sure you guys, if you've been drawing for a while, know the difference. Sometimes, business brain tells you to make things to impress people on Instagram, whereas art brain should be thinking about how to improve, how to get better, and how to, shonen anime-style, become the best artist ever. Those are two different things. Learning to distinguish them as different sides of yourself and knowing how much to listen to either one is important. Don't let either one fully take over, or you'd be unhappy either way.

1. Bulk Purchases

Buying in bulk seemed smart, but living with my girlfriend, we can't consume everything before it expires. Now, I buy regular sizes for perishables.

2. Not Knowing How to Save

Wealth isn't just about income, it's about saving. I struggled until I learned effective saving strategies, which improved my finances.

3. Unintentional Spending

Stores tempt us to overspend with tactics like big carts and enticing layouts. I make a shopping list and stick to it to avoid impulse buys.

4. Expensive Designer Glasses

Many designer glasses are made in the same factories as cheaper ones. Brands like Luxottica inflate prices. I avoid paying extra for a name.

5. Not Using "Love It or Leave It"

I used to buy clothes I only liked, rarely wearing them. Now, I only buy items I love, focusing on quality over quantity.

6. Overpaying for Medicine

Generic drugs have the same quality as brand-name versions. I buy generics to save money without sacrificing effectiveness.

7. Missing Travel Savings with VPNs

Using a VPN to change my location when booking flights has saved me hundreds by accessing lower regional prices.

8. Ignoring eSIMs Abroad

An eSIM connects me to the internet overseas without costly roaming fees or local SIMs. It's a convenient, cost-effective solution.

9. Not Requesting Fee Waivers

Credit card late fees can be waived if you ask politely. If refused, I try again with a different representative.

10. Poor Negotiation on Big Purchases

Effective negotiation creates win-win situations. When buying my car, I used data and sought added benefits like better financing.

11. Falling for Sales Tricks

Sales exploit the anchoring effect, making discounts seem better. I remind myself that a deal isn't savings if I don't need the item.

12. Skipping Cashback Online

I use cashback programs when shopping online, saving money on purchases I already plan to make.

13. Unnecessary Souvenirs

Instead of bulky souvenirs, I buy small items like magnets, affordable, easy to pack, and meaningful reminders of my trips.

14. Buying Lottery Tickets

Odds of winning the lottery are tiny, you're more likely to be struck by lightning. I no longer buy tickets.

15. Paying ATM Fees

ATM fees add up. I opened an account that reimburses fees worldwide, saving me money when I withdraw cash.

15.5 Paying for Hotel Wi-Fi

Hotels often charge for Wi-Fi, but joining their free rewards programs grants complimentary access. I always sign up to avoid fees.

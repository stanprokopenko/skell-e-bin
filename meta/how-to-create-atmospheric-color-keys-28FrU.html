Understanding Soft Shadows and Ambient Occlusion

Part of this process is learning to accurately paint lighting, which involves understanding the physics of light. Knowing how to do soft shadows and ambient occlusion is really important for this craft of painting color keys. I highly encourage you to study light.

One aspect of light is seeing soft shadows. For example, this house right now is all just kind of one flat color, and that's not what light does. When there is very soft light, like when the sun has set, there's going to be darkening into the corners. I'll select the color of the house and darken it slightly in all the grooves and corners. Under the porch, it will be darker because less light can get in. Under the edge of the roof, there will be a soft shadow where the roof line occludes some of the soft light coming from the sky. Painting in these soft shadows helps with our understanding of the shape and form of the house.

Ambient light comes first. I get those soft shadows in, and then everything can build on top of that. There will be a little darkening happening between trees as they get closer together, enhancing depth and form.

How to Add Tendons to Hand Sculptures

Copy Code

In this lesson, we're going to add tendons to the hand anatomy sculpture study we've already started. We'll use a different color clay or wax for this. Specifically, we'll use Victoria Brown microcrystalline wax mixed with beeswax, which is firmer and suitable for representing tendons.

Tendons on the Back of the Hand

Extensor Carpi Ulnaris

  • Path: This tendon travels between two ridges on the ulna and attaches to the bottom part of the first phalanx of the pinky, just above the carpal bones of the wrist.

Extensor Digiti Minimi

  • Path: This tendon travels up the pinky, extending all the way to the tip.

Extensor Digitorum

  • Path: These tendons branch out to the fingers, with the pinky and ring finger tendons staying together until the last part.

Extensor Indicis

  • Path: This tendon goes from the ulna area to the index finger, traveling under other tendons before connecting to the index tendon.

Tendons on the Thumb

Extensor Pollicis Longus

  • Path: This tendon goes from under other tendons to the thumb, attaching along the inside of the ulna.

Extensor Pollicis Brevis

  • Path: This tendon attaches to the bottom of the second knuckle, creating a small space known as the "snuff box."

Adductor Pollicis Longus

  • Path: This tendon attaches to the base of the first phalanx of the thumb, traveling next to the extensor pollicis brevis.

Brachioradialis

  • Path: This ridge muscle tendon attaches from a bony bump on the radius and helps turn the arm. It ends before the wrist.

Tendons on the Palm Side of the Hand

Flexor Pollicis Longus

  • Path: This tendon attaches to the inside part of the thumb.

Flexor Carpi Radialis

  • Path: This tendon attaches to the inside part of the palm towards the wrist, traveling alongside the flexor pollicis longus.

Flexor Digitorum Profundus

  • Path: This tendon spreads out to each finger, running along the inside to the tips.

Flexor Digitorum Superficialis

  • Path: This tendon overlaps with the flexor digitorum profundus.

Flexor Carpi Ulnaris

  • Path: This tendon attaches to a bony protrusion on the carpal bones and travels down the arm.

Final Tips and Recommendations

  • Start with the back of the hand towards the pinky side and work your way around.
  • Make coils ahead of time to save time, as pressing out and rolling each coil can be time-consuming.
  • Use a different color clay for the tendons to distinguish them from the bones and muscles.

The Eight-Step Head Drawing Process

The head drawing process consists of eight key steps, divided into three main phases:

Gesture and Basic Structure (Steps 1-4)

  1. Shape of the skull
  2. Tilt or two-dimensional axes
  3. Jaw and cross (combination of center line and brow)
  4. Perspective (determined by brow relationship to ear)

Proportions (Step 5)

  1. Marking halves and thirds between brow to chin

Perspective and Features (Steps 6-8)

  1. Four points to a side plane (developing ear, jaw, and cheek)
  2. Eye sockets as a visor
  3. Nose development, denture sphere, and chin

Homework Assignment: 20 Head Drawings

Your homework is to complete 20 head drawings, divided into two groups:

  1. 10 drawings building on prior work:

    • Start with drawings you feel confident about, reaching at least step 5 or 6
    • Focus on adding the newer steps: visor, nose, denture sphere, and chin

  2. 10 new drawings from scratch:

    • Work through all eight steps
    • This helps build greater familiarity with the entire process

Time Management and Learning Approach

Aim for 10-15 minute exercises for each drawing. This time limit is crucial for several reasons:

  • It forces repetition, which is key to learning
  • It helps ingrain the process into your muscle memory
  • It prevents you from getting stuck on details

Remember, the goal is to make the process routine and habitual. By setting time boundaries, you're creating "guardrails" for your practice.

Instructor's Advice

  • Shorter time limits are beneficial. They force you to stop, restart, and repeat the process, reinforcing your learning.
  • Avoid the temptation to perfect details. With a 20-minute limit, you might find yourself focusing too much on perfecting individual features.
  • Embrace repetition. It's through repeated practice that the process will become second nature to you.

Good luck with your homework! Your instructor looks forward to critiquing your work and seeing your progress through these exercises.

How to Solve Perspective by Swiveling Your Page

Copy Code

Swiveling the page is one of the most useful tricks you'll use to solve perspective problems in your artistic journey. This simple technique helps you understand the relationship between up-down and left-right awareness in your drawings.

Understanding Up-Down and Left-Right Awareness

If you've thoroughly practiced drawing lines that go up and lines that go down, you know, those 50 quick sketches of rooms, you've grasped the concept of up-down angles. Here's a revelation, up-down awareness is the same as left-right awareness.

The center of your vision is below things that are above it and above things that are below it. Similarly, things can be to the left and right of your center of vision. Swiveling the page reveals this connection between vertical and horizontal perspectives.

Swiveling the Page to Reveal Perspective Relationships

When you swivel your page 90 degrees, the horizon line becomes vertical. Objects that were above and below you are now to your left and right. This shift in perspective helps you see that up-down and left-right share the same principles in perspective drawing.

As you sketch rooms and become aware of height, take an extra minute to swivel the page. Notice how this change opens up new ways of understanding the spatial relationships in your drawing. This technique will be invaluable as you tackle more complex perspective challenges.

Applying Swiveling to Solve Complex Perspective Problems

Swiveling the page will expand your options for solving complex perspective problems. When we get to measuring systems on floors, walls, ceilings, and staircases, this trick becomes especially handy.

For example, steps that are below you or at severe angles can be hard to measure. By swiveling the page, you can make depth measurements easier when a vertical line becomes a horizon line. This approach simplifies the process of figuring out challenging spatial dimensions.

Remember, this is about swiveling the paper, not the objects. Swiveling objects will come later. For now, focus on turning your drawing 90 degrees to explore the commonalities between up-down and left-right in perspective.

Practice Exercises

  • Continue sketching rooms, being mindful of height and spatial relationships.
  • After completing a sketch, swivel your page 90 degrees and observe how the perspective changes.
  • Notice how objects shift from being above and below to being left and right of your center of vision.
  • Use this technique to experiment with different viewpoints and solve baffling perspective issues.

* * *

Keep an eye on these principles, swivel the page and explore how perspectives shift. If it feels a bit dizzying at first, don't worry, it's all part of training your perception and imagination.

Go ahead and sketch. Have fun, but be safe. Keep practicing room after room until you can see them in your mind's eye, fully realized in perspective.

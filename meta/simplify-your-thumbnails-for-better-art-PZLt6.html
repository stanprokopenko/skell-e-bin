The Importance of Simplicity

When working on notan thumbnails, simplicity and clarity are essential. By limiting ourselves to just two or three values, we can focus on the fundamental value structure of a composition without getting bogged down by unnecessary details.

Prioritizing Values

Choosing What to Emphasize

In a two-value notan, we need to decide whether to emphasize lighting or objects. If we add a third value, we can communicate more information, but it's crucial to choose whatâ€™s most important.

For example, if the lighting is the key element, we might group all the shadows together and highlight the areas hit by light. If the objects themselves are important, we might use the mid-tone to distinguish them from the background.

Grouping Lights and Shadows

It's often helpful to group shadows into one value and lights into another. Within the lights, we can use an additional value to show highlights or subtle variations. This approach helps to keep the composition clear and directs the viewer's eye to the focal points.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding Too Much Detail

One of the biggest pitfalls is spending too much time on details in a thumbnail. Remember, thumbnails are meant to be quick and simple. Adding intricate details can detract from the overall composition and consume unnecessary time.

Overusing Gradations

Another mistake is using too many gradations or trying to render soft edges in a thumbnail. At this stage, it's better to focus on the big shapes and hard edges. We can refine edges and add gradations in the final rendering.

Not Clearly Separating Values

Failing to clearly separate lights, mid-tones, and shadows can make a thumbnail confusing. It's important to have clean value separations to enhance readability and ensure the composition communicates effectively.

Tips for Effective Notan Thumbnails

Work Small and Efficiently

Keep your thumbnails small and spend no more than five to ten minutes on each. This forces you to focus on the essential elements without getting lost in details.

Simplify Shapes

Focus on simplifying shapes to their basic forms. Avoid drawing every leaf or texture. Instead, capture the overall silhouette and major forms.

Make Deliberate Choices

Be intentional with your value decisions. Decide what's most important in your composition and use your limited values to emphasize those elements.

Limit the Number of Values

When you only have two or three values, you must prioritize what's most important. Each additional value should communicate the next most significant element in your composition.

Use Bold and Clean Lines

In your thumbnails, use bold and clean lines to define edges and shapes. Avoid sketchy or scratchy lines that can make your thumbnail look messy and obscure the composition.

Understanding Value Hierarchy

Recognize that each additional value allows you to communicate more information. The first value separation highlights the most important aspect, such as the overall lighting. The second value can introduce form or separate important elements. Be mindful of how you use each value to build a hierarchy of importance in your composition.

Experiment and Explore

Don't be afraid to create multiple thumbnails exploring different value arrangements. Each version can help you understand what works best for your composition.

By focusing on simplicity, clarity, and deliberate value choices, you can create effective notan thumbnails that serve as a strong foundation for your final artwork. Remember, the goal is to explore and plan your composition efficiently, not to create a finished rendering at this stage.

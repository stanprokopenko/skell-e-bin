How to Draw Asymmetrical Legs

Copy Code

I like to use a simpler on the inside, more complicated on the outside approach for drawing thighs. This idea comes from the wonderful Berne Hogarth. It helps me show both the pillar of support and the propulsion in a standing pose.

Simplifying with Tubes

I break the thigh down into asymmetrical tubes. Whether it’s a male or female leg, I keep these key ideas in mind:
- The inside of the thigh is often stiffer or straighter.
- The outside of the thigh is often longer, fuller, or more fluid.
- The thigh tapers toward the knee.

Vertical Moments

Because the legs are the main pillars of support, I pay close attention to verticals. Even if the leg is tilted, I look for:
- Any portions that lean close to vertical, because they suggest stability.
- Slight shifts away from vertical, which show the hip’s angle or the body’s weight distribution.

I often force near-vertical areas to be precisely vertical in my drawings. That helps me emphasize balance and an upright stance.

Tapers and S-Curves

As the thigh transitions into the knee and lower leg, I often use S-curves to show how the forms flow. This S-curve approach gives a lovely, more characteristic transition. If I’m going for a simpler look, I can keep it more basic. But when I want a watery, flowing design, I let that S-curve stand out more.

Corners vs. Curves

Sometimes I’ll mark corners where the hip meets the thigh or where the thigh meets the knee. Other times I’ll let it flow with a curve. I might break the form into more corners if I want a sharper, more muscular look. If I’m drawing a heavier model, there might be less taper, but I still focus on that inside vs. outside contrast.

Whether the hip and thigh blend or break apart at a corner depends on the model or the desired style. I choose to iterate and figure out how strongly I want that shape to read.

By combining these ideas, simpler inside, fuller outside, looking for verticals, using curves and corners, I can make the thigh feel stable, dynamic, and part of a freestanding pose.

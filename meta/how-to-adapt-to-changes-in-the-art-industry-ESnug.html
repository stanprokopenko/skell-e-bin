The Role of the Internet

The internet has become a significant platform for selling art, supplementing traditional galleries. While some high-end galleries and artists still thrive, it's a small percentage. Many artists no longer rely solely on galleries for their income.

Online education is on the rise because it doesn't create debt and empowers students to choose their courses. This shift is partly due to the high cost of traditional art schools and the underpayment of teachers. Online education offers flexibility and accessibility, making it a popular choice for many aspiring artists.

The shift from traditional galleries to online sales has been a major change in the art industry. Artists have adapted to these changes by leveraging the internet to reach a broader audience and sell their work directly to consumers.

The Future of the Art Industry

There are fears about automation and AI in the art industry, with predictions that 90% of animation will be done by AI. However, historical examples show that industries often face "endings" but adapt and continue. For instance, major changes in the movie industry, like the introduction of sound and television, required adaptation. The comic book industry experienced a similar oversaturation in the early 90s.

Young people often drive innovation and adaptation in industries. Watching documentaries on industry history can provide perspective and encouragement. Simon Sinek's message that these are not unprecedented times emphasizes the need for re-evaluation and change. Humans are resilient and adaptable, like coyotes.

Collaboration with humans is more meaningful than with robots. Long-term projects require human experience and desire. Robots may not completely replace human creativity and collaboration. Jeffrey Katzenberg's claim that 90% of jobs won't be done by robots is debatable, but even if it's 97%, adaptation is key. AI isn't doing 3% of the work; humans like Justin Sweet and Vance Kovacs are.

Justin and Vance shared their work at a community college, inspiring discouraged students. Vance showed work from a canceled movie, Mouse Guard, highlighting family dynamics and storytelling. AI lacks understanding of family dynamics and emotional storytelling. Storytelling illustrators have emotional discernment that AI lacks.

Don Richardson, a television director, had a 35-year career because he improved every show he worked on. Richardson adapted to different genres due to proper training and was indispensable. Smaller companies can now compete with larger ones due to easier scaling. The economy might shift to many small entities, like YouTubers supporting themselves. Everyone needs to think of themselves as part of a business. Specialization in skills will be important, similar to how sound specialists emerged in movies. Collaboration with complementary skills is crucial for success.

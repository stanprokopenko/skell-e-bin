How to Gesture Draw Complex Poses

Extending Your Approach

When working on poses that fall outside the standard weight-on-one-leg stance, it's important to see how malleable your gesture drawing approach can be. Instead of relying on familiar proportions, focus on interpreting what you see through lines and rhythms, even when dealing with foreshortening.

Maintaining the Big Idea

Keep gesture drawing notational to stay in touch with your idea and ensure it's working at this stage. Avoid adding shapes, overlaps, or perspective too early. Remember, gesture is about capturing the essence of the pose, the story it tells.

Dealing with Foreshortening

Foreshortening presents challenges, especially when limited to line and rhythm. Here are some tips:

  • Adjust Lengths: Shorten lines to represent limbs coming toward you.
  • Use Wrapping Lines: Increase the degree of wrapping to indicate perspective.
  • Control Exposure: Understand that foreshortening is about the controlled exposure of a limb's length.

Flipping the Sequence for Bent Poses

For poses where the figure is bending forward or backward, flip your usual sequence:

  • Start with the cervical spine.
  • Follow with the thoracic, lumbar, and pelvis lines.
  • This captures the pinch and stretch in the torso effectively.

Incorporating Observational Measurements

When standard proportions don't apply, supplement your approach with observational measurements:

  • Use plumb lines to relate different parts of the body.
  • Compare lengths of limbs to maintain proportion.
  • Recognize that the figure's design allows it to fold naturally, like an Ikea product.

Handling Seated and Crouched Poses

In seated or crouched poses:

  • Focus on the overall balance and weight distribution.
  • Capture the asymmetry of the figure to create dynamic poses.
  • Remember that the footprint is also asymmetrical and can add interest.

Avoiding Tangents and Overlaps

To prevent confusion in complex poses:

  • Adjust limb positions to avoid tangents.
  • Use line weight to differentiate foreground elements from the background.
  • Be mindful of areas where limbs might overlap.

Embracing Subtlety

Subtle poses can be the hardest to capture:

  • Pay attention to minor shifts in alignment.
  • Use line weight variations and wrapping lines to indicate slight foreshortening.
  • Understand that even in less dynamic poses, the gesture is vital.

Tips for Extreme Foreshortening

For poses with significant foreshortening:

  • Simplify the figure into basic shapes.
  • Stack shapes to build the figure piece by piece.
  • Recognize that foreshortening reduces the visible length of limbs dramatically.

Staying Flexible in Your Technique

Always be open to integrating different tools and approaches:

  • Balance analytic and observational methods.
  • Adjust your techniques to suit the pose's demands.
  • Keep the division of labor in mind, focus on gesture now and refine later.

* * *

Adapting gesture drawing to complex poses is about flexibility and understanding. By focusing on lines, rhythms, and the big idea, you can capture any pose, no matter how challenging. Remember, the goal is to keep in touch with your idea and ensure it's working, even in the most intricate positions.

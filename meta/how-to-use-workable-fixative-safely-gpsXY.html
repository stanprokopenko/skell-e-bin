How to Use Workable Fixative Safely

Copy Code

When working on a drawing, it's crucial to prevent smudging, especially if you plan to continue adding details or layers. Using a workable fixative is the key to achieving this. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to apply it safely and effectively.

Using a Workable Fixative

It's important to choose a workable fixative rather than a final fixative. The workable fixative allows you to continue working on your drawing after application, preventing smudging while still being receptive to additional layers.

Safety Precautions

When applying the fixative, safety should be your top priority:

  • Spray the fixative outside to avoid inhaling harmful fumes.
  • Hold your breath during the application to minimize exposure.
  • Focus on safety rather than documenting the process, so avoid videotaping while spraying.

Application Process

Here's how to apply the fixative properly:

  • Perform the spraying on a porch or outdoor area. This ensures good ventilation and reduces the risk of inhaling fumes.
  • After spraying, leave the drawing outside for about an hour and a half. This allows the fumes to dissipate completely.
  • Bringing the drawing inside too soon can result in a lingering smell, indicating that fumes are still present.

Continuing the Artwork

Once the fumes have cleared, you can proceed with your artwork:

  • Mix your paint and prepare to continue working on your drawing.
  • With the fixative applied, your drawing is now protected from smudging, allowing you to add more details or layers confidently.

By following these steps, you can ensure that your drawing remains smudge-free and ready for further work.

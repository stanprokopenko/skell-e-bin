Foundational Ideas in Art

Two main foundational ideas in art are:

  1. How separate parts connect with the greater whole
  2. Balancing gesture and structure

These concepts are essential for creating cohesive and dynamic artwork. Understanding how individual elements relate to the overall composition and how to balance the energy of gesture with the solidity of structure will significantly improve your drawings.

Understanding Gesture and Structure

Gesture

Gesture is all about energy and fluid rhythm. It captures the essence of movement and life in a drawing. In practice, gesture is represented by long axis curves in forms. These curves give your drawings a sense of flow and dynamism.

Structure

Structure, on the other hand, focuses on mass and form. It's about giving your drawings a solid foundation and three-dimensional quality. When working with structure, we break contours and move over forms, emphasizing their volume and weight.

Drawing Process

Steve Huston recommends the following process when approaching a drawing:

  1. Draw simple 2D shapes: Begin with basic, flat shapes to establish the overall composition.
  2. Add 3D volume: Transform the 2D shapes into three-dimensional forms.
  3. Refine with smaller structures: Add details and smaller forms to enhance the drawing.
  4. Add rendering and shadows: Finally, add light and shadow to create depth and realism.

It's crucial to maintain both gesture and structure throughout this process, from the initial sketch to the final rendering.

Combining Gesture and Structure

The key to creating dynamic and lifelike drawings is combining curved gestures. This approach creates a sense of life and fluidity in your artwork. It's helpful to think of these concepts intuitively before applying formal theories.

As you progress in your drawing, ensure that both gesture and structure carry through from your initial sketch to the final rendering. This consistency will give your artwork a cohesive and professional look.

Tips for Beginners

For those new to these concepts, it's important to understand how gesture and structure work together before diving into advanced rendering techniques. This foundation will serve you well as you progress in your artistic journey.

When demonstrating these techniques, it's suggested to use marker paper for clearer, darker lines. This can help make the concepts more visible and easier to understand.

Advanced Concepts

As you become more comfortable with gesture and structure, you can apply these concepts to more advanced techniques. For example, when rendering light and shadow, consider how gesture and structure influence the way light falls on and moves across forms. This application of foundational principles to more complex aspects of drawing will elevate your artwork to new levels.

By mastering the balance between gesture and structure, you'll be able to create drawings that are both dynamic and well-formed, capturing the essence of your subject while maintaining a strong sense of form and volume.

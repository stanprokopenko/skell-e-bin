Designing Scarlet Witch's Study

Copy Code

Hey everybody, today we are going to be working on some environmental designs for some different Marvel ideas and concepts and stories. First up, we have Scarlet Witch's study where she meditates and researches the occult. Pretty cool.

We're diving into designing Scarlet Witch's study. There's no script for this prompt, so we're figuring out what her space would look like and how to give it a cool spin. When designing a character's space, I like to pull out and get very wide. This makes the character small in relation to the space, making the space feel important.

For Scarlet Witch, I'm thinking of a vibe rather than a specific thing: mysterious, lots of books, occult vibes, candles, and a big cloak. Gothic architecture with sharp edges comes to mind. As we choose our shot, we'll incorporate these elements to highlight the character.

Thumbnailing Process

We'll start by doing a few thumbnails. I prefer a vertical shot and a one-point perspective vibe. Thumbnails help us explore different compositions quickly.

  • First Thumbnail: A massive study with a low vanishing point horizon line to make it feel bigger. A big fireplace and two-story bookcases. It feels too centered and not intimate enough.
  • Second Thumbnail: A down shot from a higher point of view. A three-point perspective grid with a cozy study vibe and otherworldly elements. It feels awkward with the railing cutting into the main composition.
  • Third Thumbnail: Moving the camera over Scarlet Witch, keeping the circular seance vibe and intense lighting. This one works best but needs adjustments.

Thumbnails are great for all skill levels. They help get the idea down quickly and are essential for choosing the right shot.

Sketching and Refining the Composition

Transitioning from thumbnails to a detailed sketch, I give myself a border to avoid adding too much detail. I use a two-point perspective grid to keep things visually balanced.

  • Character Placement: Focus on the character first. Scarlet Witch should be in the middle of the fireplace, not to the side. Making her small in relation to the space emphasizes the environment's importance.
  • Environment Details: Gothic fireplace, bookshelves, and other elements. The environment serves to build up Scarlet Witch as a character.

I use reference images to ensure details are correct. The farther from the central image, the looser the details. This keeps the focus on Scarlet Witch.

Inking Techniques and Considerations

Starting the inking process, I focus on big shapes and get bold. I use different tools like parallel pens for consistent lines.

  • Line Weight and Texture: Varying line weight and texture creates depth. Gestural lines on the outside and careful lines near the central image.
  • Perspective Grid: Helps keep everything consistent. I use it to determine where to stop certain lines and maintain the image's structure.

Comic book readers spend only a few seconds on each panel, so it's important to balance detail and simplicity. The central image should always be the focus.

Final Touches and Reflections

After inking, I erase pencil lines and add final details. Using whiteout for corrections and highlights helps refine the image.

  • Contrast and Detail: Adding more contrast draws the eye to the central image. Introducing grayscale can add texture and depth.
  • Reflection: Sometimes the final drawing doesn't turn out as expected. It's important to keep moving forward and learn from each piece. Deadlines help prevent over-obsession and encourage forward motion.

In the end, we chose a good shot, thought about the character, and used reference images. The process may seem overwhelming, but approaching it smartly makes it manageable.

How to Use Workable Fixative Safely

Copy Code

When working on a drawing that you plan to develop further, it's crucial to use a workable fixative. This step is essential to prevent your drawing from smudging as you continue to work on it. Let me walk you through the process and share some important safety tips.

Using Workable Fixative

First, make sure you're using a workable fixative, like Krylon's. It's important not to confuse this with a final fixative. The workable fixative allows you to continue working on your piece without the risk of smudging, which is a basic but vital step in the process.

Safety Precautions

When you're ready to apply the fixative, remember to hold your breath while spraying. You don't want to inhale any of the fumes. It's best to do this outside to minimize exposure to harmful chemicals. I won't be showing this part on video, but I usually step out onto my porch, spray the fixative while holding my breath, and then quickly return inside, closing the door behind me.

Allowing Fumes to Dissipate

After spraying, it's important to leave your artwork outside for at least an hour and a half. This allows the fumes to dissipate. If you bring it back into your studio too soon, you'll notice a lingering smell, which indicates that the chemicals are still in the air. It's best to avoid this to ensure a safe and pleasant working environment.

By following these steps, you'll be able to protect your drawing and prepare it for the next stages of your artistic process.

Overview of Level Two Exercises

In level two exercises, artists use gestural perspective references from previous projects to enhance their visual memory. These exercises include:

  • Studying a reference, then drawing from memory
  • Drawing the pose from a different angle
  • Changing proportions to practice designing based on memory

These exercises aim to improve visual memory and expand the artist's ability to manipulate poses in their mind.

Key Elements in Figure Drawing

Gesture and Shape Language

Capturing interesting shape language and anatomy details is crucial for: - Inventing poses and characters - Creating more dynamic and expressive drawings

Artists should look for exciting, expressive elements in poses to remember and incorporate into their work.

Perspective and Balance

When drawing figures: - Consider ground plane angles for grounded feet - Understand balance when inventing poses - Pay attention to weight distribution and foot placement

Line Quality and Expression

To improve line quality: - Make lines more dynamic, expressive, and interesting - Balance clean construction with exploration - Don't let fear of drawing people hinder the quality of work

General Advice for Improvement

  • Study proportions from dynamic real poses
  • Exaggerate gestures and look for interesting shapes
  • Layer work: start with gesture and proportions before final lines
  • Practice consistently over time to develop and retain skills
  • Recognize your mistakes as a valuable skill
  • Balance gesture and perspective in mannequin drawings
  • Understand how compression and stretching work in figure poses

Remember, it's okay to spend more time on assignments than initially given, as continued practice is important. Drawing from memory is a crucial skill for artists and should be practiced consistently over years.

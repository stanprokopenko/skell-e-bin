Using Triangles to Measure Common Angles

The 0 to 180 Degree Line and the 90 Degree Line

To start, you can use your triangles to find the 0 to 180-degree line and the 90-degree line. For the 90-degree line, either triangle works since they both have a 90-degree angle.

45-Degree Angles

The 45-degree triangle is perfect for finding a 45-degree angle. Simply align it, and you're set. If you flip it the other way, you get the 135-degree line. This works because you're adding 90 degrees to the original 45 degrees (45 + 90 = 135).

60-Degree and 120-Degree Angles

Using the 60-degree angle on your triangle, you can easily measure a 60-degree angle. Flipping it gives you a 120-degree angle, which is double the original 60 degrees.

30-Degree and 150-Degree Angles

The same triangle doubles as a 30-degree triangle. It provides a 30-degree angle, and flipping it gives you a 150-degree angle. The extra 120 degrees come from doubling the 60-degree angle.

Measuring 15-Degree and 75-Degree Angles

Combining Triangles for 15 Degrees

Some angles require using two triangles together. To get a 15-degree angle, take the 45-degree triangle and subtract the 30-degree angle by slanting it back. This means you're removing 30 degrees from 45 degrees (45 - 30 = 15). Flipping this setup gives you the 165-degree angle (180 - 15 = 165).

Combining Triangles for 75 Degrees

To find a 75-degree angle, combine the 30-degree and 45-degree triangles. Adding these angles together gives you 75 degrees (30 + 45 = 75). Flipping it the other way yields the 105-degree angle. This is 15 degrees above 90 degrees, just as 75 degrees is 15 degrees below 90.

Do You Need to Memorize All These Angles?

You don't have to memorize all these numbers to be good at freehand perspective. However, familiarizing yourself with the 15-degree increments around the circle can enhance your understanding and skill. Think of the angles in terms of slices of pizza:

  • 90-degree wedge: Easy to recognize.
  • 45-degree wedge: A good-sized piece.
  • 30-degree wedge: Cutting back a bit.
  • 15-degree wedge: A small slice when you're feeling guilty.
  • 60-degree wedge: More indulgent.
  • 75-degree wedge: Really hogging it.

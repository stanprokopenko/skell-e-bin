From Blobs to Airplanes

Copy Code

In this lesson, you'll learn how to draw an airplane by starting with simple shapes and gradually adding complexity. We'll begin with blobs because they're easy to work with, then move to blocks for structure, and finally refine your drawing with curves. Repetition is important, so practice these steps until you're comfortable.

Starting with Blobs

First, decide the airplane's position and direction. Is it coming toward you or flying away? Draw a simple blob to represent the airplane's body. Use an aspect line to indicate its orientation and direction.

Transforming Blobs into Blocks

Convert your blob into a right-angled box to establish a three-dimensional form. Identify the three main directions:

  • Length of the airplane
  • Width
  • Height

These directions help you visualize the structure in space. Draw lines representing these directions, forming a box around your blob.

The Three Sets of Lines

When constructing your box, remember there are three sets of lines:

  • Lines going off to the right (receding in one direction)
  • Lines going off to the left (receding in another direction)
  • Vertical lines (showing height)

These lines ensure accurate perspective and help you place all parts of the airplane correctly.

Adding Wings and Tail

Add the wings and tail using the box's lines to maintain perspective:

  • Wings emerge from the middle section.
    • Near-side wings appear larger and are closer to you.
    • Far-side wings follow the same angle but appear smaller due to perspective.
  • Tail is drawn on the rear section, aligned with the established directions.

To enhance realism, taper the wings and tail by connecting the corners to the body, ensuring they align with the airplane's perspective.

Refining with Curves

Curves add complexity but bring life to your drawing. Simplify complex curves by relating them to the straight lines of your box. For features like the cockpit dome or propeller, start with basic shapes and gradually refine them into curves.

Drawing from Different Perspectives

Experiment by drawing the airplane from various angles. Adjust the three sets of lines to match the perspective:

Looking Up at the Airplane

  • Lines go down as they recede.
  • Use your lines to show the airplane above you.

Looking Down at the Airplane

  • Lines go up as they recede.
  • Adjust your lines to depict the airplane below you.

Remember, altering the angles of these lines changes the viewpoint and helps you accurately capture different perspectives.

Practice and Repetition

Repetition is key. Drawing the airplane multiple times from various angles strengthens your understanding of form and perspective. Don't worry if it feels repetitive, each iteration builds your skill and confidence.

* * *

By starting with blobs, moving to blocks, and refining with curves, you establish the primary forms of the airplane. Mastering these foundations is essential before adding details and textures. Focus on constructing a solid structure first, refinements and subtleties come later. Remember, mastering the fundamentals allows you to tackle more complex forms in the future.

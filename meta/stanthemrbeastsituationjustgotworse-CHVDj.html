YouTube's Transformation Over the Years

YouTube has changed dramatically over the past decade. There was a time when creators made videos just for fun, without any thought of monetization. Now, YouTube has become a platform where people build massive businesses and achieve genuine stardom.

The Rise of Corporate YouTubers

Gone are the days when YouTubers accidentally built communities. Today, many channels are crafted with teams aiming to create the perfect videos to maximize profits. This shift has led to creators like Mr Beast, Logan Paul, and KSI becoming household names.

Introducing Lunch'ley: A New Competitor

Recently, these three YouTubers announced their collaboration to create Lunch'ley, a competitor to Lunchables. They claim it's a healthier alternative to the "lunch BS" that everyone knows.

Marketing Strategies and Health Claims

Their marketing relies on two things: their influence over kids and using healthy buzzwords. They compare Lunch'ley to Lunchables, highlighting slight improvements in ingredients and nutritional content.

  • Real Cheese vs. Cheese Product: They emphasize that Lunch'ley uses real cheese, while Lunchables uses "pasteurized prepared cheese product."
  • Nutritional Differences: Lunch'ley has fewer calories, less sugar, more electrolytes, and more B vitamins.
  • Prime Drink Inclusion: Each Lunch'ley includes a Prime drink, which they highlight as having no added sugar and more electrolytes.

They also use dramatic tactics, like spitting out Lunchables and praising Lunch'ley emphatically, to sway young viewers.

Criticisms and Controversies

Many people are not happy with this product. The main criticisms are:

  • Misleading Health Claims: While Lunch'ley is marginally healthier than Lunchables, it's still full of processed ingredients. Acting like they've reinvented the wheel is misleading.
  • Influence Over Children: The marketing tactics exploit their massive influence over kids. Young fans may believe Lunchables are terrible just because their idols say so.
  • High Price Point: Lunch'ley costs around $4, double the price of a Lunchable. For parents, especially with multiple children, doubling lunch costs is significant.
  • Exploiting Buzzwords: Terms like "more electrolytes" and "no added sugar" are thrown around to make the product seem much healthier than it is.

Responses from Mr Beast and Logan Paul

In response to the backlash:

  • Mr Beast stated their goal is to provide a "better for you alternative" at roughly the same price. He argues that since millions eat Lunchables, offering a higher quality version is a net good.
  • Logan Paul said they've spent their lives building brands and now want to build businesses. He points out that people don't complain when Lunchables face bans, so they shouldn't be upset with Lunch'ley.

Final Thoughts

I understand why people are upset. If they had simply created a healthier alternative without the misleading marketing, it might have been different. But using their influence over children to push a marginally better product at a higher price feels wrong.

This trend of YouTubers turning into full-fledged corporations raises questions about the future of the platform. While it's natural for creators to want to expand their businesses, it's important to consider the impact on their young audiences.

The Nine Basic Positions

When drawing objects, they can be viewed from nine basic positions:

  • Directly in front (ortho view)
  • To the left or right
  • Above or below
  • Above to the left or right
  • Below to the left or right

Understanding these positions simplifies perspective. Most objects you draw will likely be in one of these categories.

Understanding Axes and Depth

By adding the depth axis (Z lines) to the X and Y axes, you transform flat shapes into three-dimensional forms. The Z lines give your objects thickness, making them appear more realistic.

Observing Angle Changes

When viewing objects from different angles:

  • Right angles change based on perspective.
  • With two planes visible, front corners become acute, back corners become obtuse.
  • With three planes visible, side angles become acute, front and back become obtuse.

Consider measuring these angles to train your eye, but focus on how perspective affects them.

Simplifying Complexity

Many positions are flipped repeats (left to right or up to down). Recognizing this reduces the complexity, making drawing objects more manageable.

Drawing Arrows in Perspective

Storyboard artists use arrows to indicate movement:

  • A flat arrow shows movement across the screen.
  • An arrow in perspective shows movement toward or away from the camera.

Creating 3D Arrows

To draw arrows in any direction:

  1. Start with a flat arrow.
  2. Foreshorten it to add depth.
  3. Remember, arrows are not symmetrical front to back, the point and tail differ.
  4. Draw arrows coming toward or going away from you at various angles.

Practicing with Blocks and Arrows

To build your foundation:

  • Draw these arrows and name their positions (e.g., above left, below right).
  • Notice the direction of the X, Y, and Z lines in each position.
  • Practice drawing them from memory.

By familiarizing yourself with these basics, you'll enhance your ability to sketch from imagination. Soon, you'll be ready to place the eye level with a horizon line and tackle more complex perspectives.

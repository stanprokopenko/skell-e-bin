Breaking the Rigidity of Graph Paper

Graph paper often has a reputation among free spirits as being rigid. But you can ignore the rigidity of the lines and create abstract, organic drawings. Use the lines to ground yourself if needed and to remind you of level.

Simple Perspective Tricks

Graph paper provides a good rigid structure. Let's try the simplest perspective trick you might have learned in kindergarten:

  • Start with a square of three units.
  • Repeat it on an offset back block of squares of the same size.
  • Put them together, and a kind of 3D effect appears.

Let's assess the angles:

  • The base is at 90 degrees.
  • One angle is 65 degrees.
  • Another angle is 115 degrees.

Think about how these angles interact.

Reversing the Perspective

Now, reverse the process:

  • Draw the shapes in reverse.
  • This is so easy, a monkey could do it.
  • But as you continue, it gets tougher.

The 45-Degree Angle Exercise

Let's look down on a block going back 45 degrees:

  • Draw it for five squares up.
  • It will also be five squares to the right, because 45 degrees is half of 90 and splits the square.

Think this way:

  • You get a 45-degree angle, a perfectly equal diagonal slicing a 90-degree square.
  • You also get a 135-degree angle at the top.
  • 45 and 135 degrees add up to 180, there's something cosmic there.

Including the secondary form clearly reads as two forms, one overlapping another.

Facing the Challenge

Now, reverse the 45-degree exercise:

  • This is not so easy.
  • It takes attention and counting lines.
  • Look back and forth to ensure it's the right dot.
  • Compare the flat pattern, the length of the lines, and the angles.
  • Remind yourself that now the large body is closer than the smaller one.

Embracing Complexity

If this has been so easy that it has offended you, hang in there:

  • Your time is coming, and you'll do marvelous things.
  • You're eager for the challenge of visual complexity you want to create.
  • Be aware that as you dive in, it can get confusing and tangled.
  • But it's always solvable.
  • Recognize impossibilities, and you can fix them or flaunt them.

Exploring Different Kinds of Graph Paper

Playing with graph paper offers variety:

  • There are different kinds:
    • Quad ruled.
    • Grids with different degrees like triangle grids and tumbling block trapezoids.
    • Some are just dots, which are quieter.
  • You can even make your own graph paper.

The Benefits of Graph Paper

Even if you're not used to graph paper, consider its benefits:

  • Artists like Kirsten Zerngebel ideate on graph paper.
  • It has never made her work look rigid.
  • If you learn how to use graph paper and keep it handy, it gives you a quick way to get measurable spatial ideas on paper.

Overcoming Misconceptions and Fears

If the rigidity of graph paper puts you off, or if optical illusions strike terror into your heart, this is your chance to set that fear aside or conquer it:

  • Look some optical illusions straight in the face and say, "I will get you!"
  • It's an attitude thing.

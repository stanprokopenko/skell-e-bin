Emotional Aspects of Art Creation

Creating art brings a sense of calm and boosts my confidence, especially when I start with a drawing that feels right. Even after over 40 years as an artist, I still experience nervousness about my demos not working out. This anxiety can stem from choosing the wrong model or not being mentally present. In tough times, I remind myself to "draw, Morgan, draw," which helps me focus and solve problems. Nervousness can lead to unnecessary details, so I concentrate on the drawing itself. Starting with a solid drawing foundation brings calmness and reduces the fear of losing control.

Methodical Approach to Painting

My studio can get hot, and I find myself sweating, but I remain calm because my methodical system is working. Following a system in stages reduces anxiety and avoids what I call "tightrope painting." Teaching beginners with a calm, staged approach prevents fear and hatred of painting. This method involves not moving forward until each stage is satisfactory. Although it may take longer initially, it makes logical sense and provides a predictable and manageable process.

Teaching Philosophy and Experience

My teacher used to say, "If you're having fun, you're doing it wrong," emphasizing hard work. Serious effort in drawing often doesn't involve smiling, but it shows in the work. I hope viewers find the method simple, as it shouldn't be difficult if followed correctly. The method involves setting up stages, making the process predictable and manageable. I used to paint from a blank canvas for demos, but it's not useful for teaching. Demonstrating skill in front of an audience is different from teaching students. The method I'm showing is more useful for students, breaking down painting into stages.

Personal Insights and Reflections

Balancing art creation with teaching is a continuous journey, and I look forward to future focus and goals in painting.

Learning perspective can be challenging and frustrating. Many artists worry that mastering perspective might cramp their style or make them overly cautious. However, these concerns are unfounded. With the right tools, understanding perspective can become much easier.

Introduction to Zolly

Zolly is a new tool designed to assist you in drawing objects from any perspective. It originates from a filmmaking technique that combines zoom and dolly effects, known as the Zolly. This technique allows you to warp space, creating normal, distant, or wide-angle views, which expands both form and imagination.

Features and Benefits of Zolly

Zolly offers several features and benefits:

  • Creation of Various Views: You can create normal, distant, or wide-angle views, enhancing your ability to visualize and draw from different perspectives.
  • Encourages Imagination: The app encourages you to imagine and then verify your mental image with what you see, fostering creativity.
  • Solid Examples for Projects: It provides solid examples that can be used for course projects, making it a valuable educational tool.
  • Total Control Over Camera Positioning: You have total control over camera positioning, allowing for diverse perspectives and a deeper understanding of how lines function.

Practical Applications

Zolly is suitable for drawing forms from all angles. You can start with basic primitives and advance to more complex models as your skills improve. The app offers ample free content, allowing you to begin practicing immediately and verify your work with ease.

Accessibility and Availability

Developed by the creators of the Skelly app, Zolly is available on a wide range of devices, including phones, tablets, Macs, and PCs. This makes it accessible to a broad audience, ensuring that you can practice and improve your perspective skills anywhere.

Encouragement for Feedback

After trying the app, users are encouraged to provide feedback. This helps improve the tool and ensures it meets the needs of artists learning perspective.

3 Color Terms Every Artist Must Know

Hi everyone, I'm Marco Bucci, here to help you get started with using color in your digital paintings. When it comes to color, it's important we use the same vocabulary. There are three main ways to modify a color: hue, saturation, and value.

Hue

Hue is the name of the color, red, green, blue, etc. The color wheel displays hues in a circular arrangement. Digital painting apps provide a color picker with a hue wheel to select different hues.

Primary and Secondary Colors

The three primary colors are red, blue, and yellow. They can't be created by mixing other colors, but they mix to form other colors:

  • Red + Yellow = Orange
  • Red + Blue = Violet
  • Yellow + Blue = Green

These are secondary colors. The color wheel shows this progression, with secondaries between the primaries they derive from.

Saturation

Saturation is the intensity or vibrancy of a hue. A highly saturated color is vivid, while a desaturated color moves toward gray. Desaturating colors introduces grays, which are common to all hues, making transitions smoother.

Overusing fully saturated colors can be overwhelming. By neutralizing (desaturating) colors, they harmonize better, creating a more pleasing aesthetic.

Value

Value refers to the lightness or darkness of a color. Independent of color, value can be shown with a grayscale from white to black. Value is crucial because it defines the light and form in your work. You can remove color from an image, and its shapes and forms remain clear.

Practicing in grayscale helps focus on value without the complexity of color.

* * *

We've covered the key terms: hue, saturation, and value. Understanding these concepts is essential for effective use of color in digital painting. With this foundation, you're ready to explore deeper into color theory.

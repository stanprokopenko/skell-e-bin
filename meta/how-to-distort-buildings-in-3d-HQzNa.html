General Advice

Maintaining three-dimensionality is crucial. Avoid losing the corners to keep the illusion of depth. Ensure lines converge to a vanishing point for foreshortening. When deforming, consider the entire form, not just individual lines. A good balance is an 80/20 rule of perspective to gesture.

Common Issues and Solutions

  • Over-distortion: Leads to loss of three-dimensionality.
  • Clear Plane Changes: Essential for maintaining depth.
  • Effective Use of Distortion: Maintain form while being creative.
  • Handling Extreme Angles: Important for perspective.
  • Adding Structure: Elements like tree trunks need corners for a secure feel.
  • Avoiding Random Distortions: Maintain structure and clarity.

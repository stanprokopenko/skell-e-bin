My Background and Favorite Works

Hi, I'm Eric Gist, often credited as EMGIST. Over the years, I've had the pleasure of working on various comic book covers for Marvel and other publishers. Some of my favorite covers include Fantastic Four, Iron Fist, Wolverine, and Hulk.

The Hulk cover was particularly exciting because it featured a classic action scene between Zombie Wolverine and Hulk—a dream scenario for many comic artists. The Fantastic Four cover was also special to me due to the storytelling challenge of capturing the dynamics of the entire family in a single image.

How I Started in Comics

I've been passionate about comics since I was about eight years old. Initially, I aspired to draw interior pages and submitted samples to various companies, receiving numerous rejection letters along the way. It wasn't until I started training with Jeff Watts that I realized my strength lay in single-image storytelling.

I began focusing on creating covers, a pursuit that took about ten years to refine my skills and another five to catch on professionally. My first cover work dates back to 2007, and I started working with Marvel around 2016, beginning with a series of covers for the Haunted Mansion series.

My Artistic Style

My work is often characterized by deep shadows, high contrast, and a moody, noir atmosphere. I'm known for creating darker, more horror-inspired pieces. However, in recent years, I've been branching out into brighter, more colorful works, especially when working with characters like the Fantastic Four and Iron Fist.

I strive to balance an iconic image with a compelling story, aiming to create artwork that leaves a lasting impression on the viewer.

Advice for Aspiring Cover Artists

The Importance of Fundamentals

Focus on mastering the fundamentals: figure drawing, anatomy, perspective, composition, and color theory. Being able to draw a cool, dynamic figure is crucial in comics. Work hard on your skills and create covers—even if they're not perfect at first.

Specialization vs. Generalization

I recommend specializing in one area initially—become really good at one thing before branching out. It's better to excel in one aspect than to be mediocre in several. However, having a broad understanding of other areas is valuable for effective communication with other professionals in the industry.

Embracing Limitations

Limitations can breed creativity. Trying to convey an entire story in a single image forces you to think deeply and come up with innovative solutions. Don't be afraid of constraints—they can lead to more original and compelling artwork.

What I Wish I Knew Earlier

I wish I had known how good you have to be to stand out. It's important not to rush the process and to ensure your skills are polished before seeking broad exposure. Always strive to improve, and remember that your job is not just to create a good image but also to sell comics.

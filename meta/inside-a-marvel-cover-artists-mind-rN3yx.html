Moon Knight #2 Retailer Variant Cover

Incorporating Egyptian Imagery

For this cover, I wanted to highlight Moon Knight's Egyptian background. Instead of the standard gargoyle, I designed an Egyptian statue based on Khonshu, the Egyptian god linked to Moon Knight. I aimed to include as many Egyptian motifs as possible.

Composition and Design Elements

I focused on a strong pyramid composition, which felt logical given the Egyptian theme. Silhouetting Moon Knight against the moon created a striking contrast. I used warm tones toward the focal point and cool tones moving away, enhancing depth.

Repeating crescent moon shapes was key. They're present in the necklace, his crescent dart, chest symbol, the moon itself, and even the statue's shoulders. I shaped his cape to mimic both the moon and stylized Egyptian wings.

Balancing Realism and Style

My goal was to make the impossible look possible by blending realistic rendering with graphic design. I shot reference photos with a model and fabric to capture authentic folds, even though a real cape wouldn't behave that way.

Painting in oil on gessoed hardboard, I stained the surface with burnt umber. This allowed warm tones to show through the predominantly cool image. Adding vintage weathering gave it a worn look that matches my retro aesthetic.

The Second Moon Knight Cover

From Black and White to Full Color

This piece began as a cover for "Moon Knight Black, White, and Blood", intended to be black, white, and red. I designed it with a red neon sign as the sole color element. When asked to make it full color for the main series, I added more hues while retaining the original concept.

Creating Atmospheric Lighting

I love playing with warm and cool lighting, especially with Moon Knight's white costume that reflects its surroundings. The red neon glow contrasts with the cool night scene, adding depth.

I incorporated the "Crescent Club" neon sign featuring Khonshu's bird cowl logo. This not only ties back to Egyptian motifs but also makes the cityscape a character in itself.

Fan Interaction and Success

The editors chose "Crescent Club" from my suggestions, and it resonated with fans. Seeing people embrace something I created is incredibly rewarding. It's gratifying when art becomes part of their experience.

Key Takeaways

In both covers, I focused on:

  • Contrast: Light against dark and warm against cool to make images pop.
  • Composition: Strong silhouettes using pyramid shapes and crescent motifs.
  • Repetition of Themes: Incorporating Egyptian imagery throughout.
  • Balancing Realism and Style: Rendering fantastical elements believably.
  • Engaging Storytelling: Creating pieces that resonate and become memorable.

I believe successful illustration finds the sweet spot between being iconic and unique. By blending familiar elements with fresh perspectives, we can create art that feels both comfortable and exciting.

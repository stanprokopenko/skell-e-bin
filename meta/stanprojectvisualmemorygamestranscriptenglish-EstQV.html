Why Visual Memory Matters

Improving your visual memory enhances your ability to draw from imagination, draw faster, be more creative, and even helps when drawing from observation. A strong visual memory allows you to recognize patterns, identify important information, and compare reference images with what's in your mind. By training your visual memory, you'll better process and store complex information while drawing.

Visual Memory Games

1. Draw from Memory

  • Study a reference for one or two minutes.
  • Hide the reference and draw it from memory.
  • Gradually decrease the viewing time as you improve.
  • After drawing, check your accuracy by comparing it to the reference.
  • Extra challenge: Try drawing it again to improve.

2. Draw from a Different Angle

  • Look at an object and draw it from a different angle.
  • Keep the reference visible; the goal is to imagine it in a new perspective.
  • Enhances your ability to visualize 3D forms.

3. Combine Them

  • Study the reference for a few minutes.
  • Hide it and draw it from memory and from a different angle.
  • For a slightly easier approach:
    • Create a study drawing while looking at the reference.
    • Set aside both the reference and your drawing.
    • Draw it again from memory, altering the angle.
  • This method helps you learn the construction of forms and improve recall.

4. Level Two: Modify Proportions

  • Use figure references and simplify them into mannequins.
  • Draw from a different angle, from memory, and change the proportions.
  • This challenges you to redesign and create your own figures using the reference as inspiration.

Bonus Game: Draw from a Moving Subject

  • Observe a moving subject, like animals at the zoo.
  • Watch for a few minutes to understand basic forms.
  • Mentally capture a pose and draw it from memory.
  • Use the subject as a live reminder of proportions and forms.
  • This exercise forces you to work quickly and adapt to changing views.

Assignments

Level One: Drawing from 3D Models

  • Use provided 3D models accessible in your browser.
  • Choose a game from the first three to practice:
    • Draw from memory.
    • Draw from a different angle.
    • Combine them.
  • Focus on practicing perspective by constructing objects from simple primitives.
  • Check your work by adjusting the 3D model to match your drawing angle.
  • Remember, the goal is to challenge yourself and improve visualization, not perfection.

Level Two: Drawing from Figure Poses

  • Work from provided photo references of figure poses.
  • Select one of the four games to apply.
  • Simplify the poses into mannequins:
    • Use gestural torso boxes.
    • Add cylinders for limbs and neck.
    • Choose a simple shape for the head (Loomis head, sphere, or box).
  • Decide how to simplify hands and feet.
  • Continue playing these memory games beyond the course to enhance different skills like gesture, shapes, colors, and shadows.

Keep Practicing and Have Fun

Embrace these challenges to strengthen your visual memory. The more you practice, the better you'll become at visualizing and drawing complex forms. So dive in, experiment with these games, and most importantly, have fun!

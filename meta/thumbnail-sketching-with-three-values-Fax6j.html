Thumbnail Sketching with Three Values

Copy Code

Project Overview

In this project, you'll apply what you've learned about value composition by creating thumbnails from provided reference photos. You'll explore several variations, using your creativity to compose different versions.

Creating Thumbnails

Start by downloading the two photos provided. Do small thumbnail sketches, the size of your thumbnail or palm, simplifying them to just three values. Unlike previous master studies, this time, you are the composer.

Tips for Thumbnails

  • Keep it small and simple: Focus on capturing the main idea without adding details.
  • Simplify: If you end up with detailed sketches, try again and focus on larger value masses.
  • Avoid precision: The goal is to explore ideas quickly, not to create a finished piece.

Exploring Value Variations

After your first thumbnail, create at least five iterations for each photo. Experiment by modifying groups of values.

  • Don't settle on the first idea: Push yourself to explore alternatives.
  • Ask "What if?":
    • What if the background is darker to emphasize light hair?
    • What if the shirt and hand are both mid-value to reduce contrast?
    • What if you rearrange value shapes for a different pattern?
    • What if you crop the thumbnail differently?

Additional Considerations

  • Keep edges hard: Avoid gradations or soft edges for now, and we'll focus on edges later.
  • Abstract shapes: You don't need to add environmental details, consider the abstract shapes they simplify into.
  • Cropping: Feel free to adjust the composition's cropping. How does adding space above affect the portrait?

Tools and Techniques

Using Pencils

  • Dull pencils help you draw larger masses and avoid unnecessary details.
  • Erase and adjust: Pencils allow flexibility to modify shapes and values.
  • Texture: Pencils pick up the paper's texture, which can be aesthetically pleasing.
  • Pressure sensitivity: Practice controlling values with your touch.

Using Markers

  • Speed: Markers are faster, allowing more variations in the same amount of time.
  • Value separation: Predefined values help organize value groups.
  • Simplification: Thick tips force you to focus on larger shapes and avoid details.

Level Two Challenge

After warming up with the initial photos, move on to the Level Two photos provided.

  • Explore more versions: The new photos are more challenging and offer more to explore.
  • Choose a favorite: Select a thumbnail you love, whether it's the first or not.
  • Create a larger version: Increase the size (up to two hands) and add more details, possibly introducing a fourth or fifth value.

* * *

Remember, this project is challenging, but don't pressure yourself to create beautiful drawings.

  • Simplicity and clarity are more important than accuracy.
  • Focus on deliberate decisions about your value groups and clear execution.
  • Control your value range and key, and create a clear hierarchy to guide the viewer's eye.

Good luck!

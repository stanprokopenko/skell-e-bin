My Stylized Digital Painting Process

Copy Code

I enjoy drawing, painting, and sculpting, especially in a stylized way. Today, I'll share one of my digital painting processes to take an illustration from a sketch to a somewhat finished rendering.

Steps in My Process

  1. Start with a Sketch: I focus on the largest shapes, keeping things loose and having fun with stylization.
  2. Add Values: I slowly build up the values, thinking about shadows and how they define the forms.
  3. Add Color Using Layer Modes: I apply color on top of the values, similar to a black-and-white-to-color process.
  4. Merge Layers and Paint on Top: Finally, I merge all the layers and refine the painting.

Starting the Sketch

I begin by focusing on the largest shapes of my subject. It's important not to get caught up in the details at this stage. I think about the head angle and how features align within the shape. For example, if the head is tilted down, the ears sit higher on the head.

I keep my lines loose and sketchy, not being too precious. This is the ultimate freedom stage where I can experiment and have fun. I might exaggerate features or play with expressions. Flipping the canvas helps me see the drawing from a new perspective and spot any issues.

Refining the Sketch

After the initial sketch, I reduce the opacity of the layer and draw over it to refine the shapes. I consolidate my lines into more confident strokes, making decisions about the design and structure. I think in both 2D shapes and 3D forms, considering how the features sit in space.

I may repeat this process of reducing opacity and drawing over the top as many times as needed, refining both the line work and my thinking. During this stage, I'm not afraid to make big changes if something isn't working.

Thinking in 3D and Adding Values

I start to consider the planes of the face and how light will interact with them. I might indicate some shadow patterns to help visualize the lighting scenario. Flipping between 2D and 3D thinking allows me to refine ambiguous areas.

I pay attention to features like the eyes, treating them as spheres and ensuring they sit correctly within the sockets. Understanding the perspective and tilt of the head is crucial.

Finalizing the Sketch

Before moving on to adding values, I make sure I have a clear understanding of the shapes and forms. This foundation is essential for building up the painting in the next stages. I might add small details, like indicating missing teeth for character, or refining the ears and neck.

Throughout this process, I iterate and refine, aiming for a sketch that provides a solid base for adding values, colors, and eventually rendering the final piece.

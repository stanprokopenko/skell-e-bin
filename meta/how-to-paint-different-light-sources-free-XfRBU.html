What's in Premium?

In this premium lesson, you'll learn how different light sources influence the mood and realism of your paintings. We'll cover the characteristics of point light sources like light bulbs, where light intensity decreases with distance due to light falloff. You'll understand how distant light sources like the sun provide consistent lighting with parallel rays and negligible falloff.

We'll explore the differences between hard light and soft light, examining how direct sunlight creates sharp-edged shadows, while scattered light from clouds softens shadows and transitions. The lesson explains the role of occlusion shadows, those soft, dark areas where light can't reach, which are essential for grounding objects in your scenes.

You'll discover how combining multiple light sources—such as direct light, scattered skylight, and reflected light—adds complexity and realism to your artwork. We'll discuss how reflected light from surfaces illuminates shadowed areas and enhances the overall lighting, both outdoors and indoors.

By the end of the lesson, you'll know how to consider the brightness, distance, direction, and hardness or softness of light sources, as well as the impact of reflected light and occlusion shadows. This understanding will help you depict lighting more accurately, enhancing the mood and realism in your paintings.

Working in Oils in a Digital Age

I mostly work in oils, which is uncommon today with many artists going digital. While I appreciate the new effects in mixed media, I value the traditional approach. Working in black and white can be faster since it removes some problem-solving aspects.

My Illustration Process

My process follows a standard academic approach:

  1. Thumbnails: Create multiple thumbnails focusing on design and composition.
  2. Reference Gathering: Collect necessary references.
  3. Refinement: Flesh out the chosen thumbnail.
  4. Color Comps: Do color compositions if needed.
  5. Final Piece: Finish with minimal guesswork.

Most illustrators work similarly, adjusting steps based on deadlines.

Dealing with Deadlines

Deadlines vary greatly. Sometimes a cover is due in a week, other times, I have months. The fastest I've done a cover is in a week, but it's not sustainable. Timelines depend on the type of cover and scheduling.

My Love for Comics

I've loved comics since childhood. An X-Men annual drawn by Art Adams, featuring Storm with Thor's hammer, inspired me to draw. That comic made me identify as an artist.

Favorite Comics and Characters

My favorite storylines are "Mutant Massacre" and "Inferno" from the X-Men. I love Wolverine and Spider-Man, but also have a fondness for Longshot and the villain Mojo.

The Magic of Comics Storytelling

Great comics create the illusion of movement and pacing. The artists trick my brain into seeing a movie from still images, a testament to their storytelling skills.

Cinematic Influences

I bring a cinematic approach to my art, using lighting techniques, camera placement, and staging from films. Directors like Spielberg and Hitchcock inspire me. I aim to evoke emotions in viewers similar to those elicited by movies.

Developing Ideas

I observe the world around me, light hitting a form, mist on the ground, and mix these observations with imagination to create authentic images in a fictional world.

Breaking into Comics

At Comic-Con, a small company gave me a chance to paint a variant cover. Later, a friend connected me with Marvel. After showing my work, I became a regular cover artist for one of their series.

Advice for Aspiring Artists

Learn to draw. Master the fundamentals, have a passion for art, and develop a unique creative vision. These are essential for a lasting career.

Overcoming Challenges

My biggest challenges were speed and originality. Finding my unique voice and producing quality work under tight deadlines is vital.

Storytelling in Art

Storytelling is key. I study artists like Alex Ross, Norman Rockwell, and others to improve. You become a storyteller by telling stories and learning from masters.

Dealing with Self-Doubt

I struggle with self-doubt. I focus on the work at hand and avoid worrying about others' opinions until it is finished. Objective judgments are made during the process, subjective ones come after.

Staying Motivated

Fatigue is the only hindrance to my motivation. I always want to draw, even on days off. I have more motivation than time.

Challenges in Professional Art

Deadlines are tough. Achieving near perfection within constraints is challenging. It's also hard when I have to decline jobs I want due to time.

Dealing with Burnout

I've faced burnout from excessive work. As a professional, you must push through and fulfill commitments. Sometimes, taking necessary breaks is crucial.

* * *

You rarely achieve 100%. Life and deadlines interfere. But completing a piece and seeing positive reactions is immensely rewarding. Remember, art is a job, and delivering quality work on schedule is essential.

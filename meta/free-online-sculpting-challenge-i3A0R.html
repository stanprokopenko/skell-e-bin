Materials Needed

You'll need some basic sculpting supplies:

  • Clay: An inexpensive polymer clay like gray Sculpey is recommended. You can bake it in a regular oven to finish your sculpture without needing to make a mold or cast.
  • Size: There's no height limit. A suggested size is around 12 inches (30 cm), but you can go as small or large as you'd like.
  • Armature: If using polymer clay, you'll likely need a wire armature and some aluminum foil to fill out the inside.
  • Tools: Basic sculpting tools.
  • Documentation Area: A well-lit area to photograph your sculpture.

Tip: Use a clay with a consistent color to help with documentation.

Judging Criteria

Understanding the judging criteria can help you refine your sculpture. The main criteria include:

  • Documentation: High-quality images of your sculpture.
  • Accurate Proportions: Correct sizing and scaling of anatomical features.
  • Anatomical Correctness: Proper representation of muscles, bones, and body structures.
  • Gesture: The dynamic movement and flow of the pose.
  • Balance: Stability and weight distribution in the sculpture.
  • Portrait: The likeness and expression of the face.
  • Hands and Feet: Detail and accuracy in sculpting extremities.
  • Surface Texture: Quality of the sculpture's surface finish.
  • Style: Your unique artistic approach.
  • Likeness: How closely your sculpture matches the reference pose.

Note: Ensure your submitted images look good. Good documentation can make a significant difference in the judging process.

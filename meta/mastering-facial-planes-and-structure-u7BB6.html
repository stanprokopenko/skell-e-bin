Exploring Angles and Poses

Understanding different angles and poses is crucial for creating dynamic and realistic facial drawings. Start with the side plane for a three-quarter top view to establish the basic structure.

Eye Sockets and Forehead

Eye Sockets: - Cut eye sockets parallel in different views. - Integrate eye sockets into the forehead.

Light and Shadow: - Understand light and shadow as plane changes. - Emphasize understanding the surface being lit.

Nose Structure

Starting the Nose: - Begin with the base and find the top of the cartilage. - Connect these points to form a trapezoid shape.

Adjustments: - Adjust the nose box when the head is looking down. - Ensure plane agreement between the nose and sockets.

Simplifying Planes: - Simplify planes into the front of the face, side of the nose, and top of the nose.

Cheek and Mouth

Cheek Overlap: - Adjust cheek overlap and the center line for the mouth.

Denture Sphere: - Teeth sit on an orbit, affecting the face form. - Reconsider the center line for teeth projection.

Chin and Jaw

Chin Shape: - Use a horizontal egg shape to fit the compartment. - Adjust the chin shape for different artistic directions.

Anatomy Considerations: - Consider adding fat and muscles to the face for more realism.

Surface Exploration

Surface Summary: - Explore the front of the cheek, side of the nose, and top of the nose.

Subdividing Planes: - Subdivide planes for details like septal cartilage and nostrils. - Use overlaps to help with foreshortening.

Perspective and Proportions

Perspective Alignment: - Ensure perspective alignment between forms. - Use proportion systems to avoid rethinking measurements.

Surface Symmetry: - Adjust the center line for surface symmetry.

Lighting and Shadows

Lighting Effects: - Consider lighting effects on the front of the cheek and side of the nose. - Create half tones or shadows across the tip of the nose.

Enhancing Details: - Use erasing to enhance overlaps and details.

Building Facial Features

Starting Points: - Start with the top, bottom, base of the nose, hairline, temple, and back of the skull.

Defining Features: - Define quarters, ear position, jaw lines, and rhythm. - Build the T for the cheek and include overlaps for foreshortening.

Final Adjustments

Proportions: - Adjust the skull shape and hairline for better proportions.

Review Steps: - Review steps: temple, hairline, back of the skull, base of the nose, and quarters. - Define the ear, cheek, jaw, and chin, considering the gland that produces saliva.

Consistency and Realism

Perspective Orientation: - Be consistent with perspective orientation. - Think through surface choreography and how lines translate to value.

Lighting Considerations: - Consider lighting on the chin with catch light, halftone, and shadow. - Push realism by thinking about lighting across smaller facets.

Fun with Features

After setting up the basic structure, be ready to have fun with features. Experiment with different artistic directions and enjoy the process of bringing your drawings to life.

My Nightly Sketchbook Ritual

After a day of working for clients as a freelance concept artist and industrial designer, I love to unwind by sketching in my nightly sketchbook. Sitting on my couch, I draw whatever inspires me at the moment. This practice lets me explore ideas, experiment, and just have fun without any pressure.

Drawing What I Love

I often sketch characters and themes that I enjoy, like Zelda, robots, and animals. One of my favorite creations is the Berserker Otter, which is best described as an otter with ADD and a big sword. I also enjoy drawing ridiculous animals armed to the gills, because just because they're cute and cuddly doesn't mean they can't have recourse.

Embracing Fun in Art

At the end of the day, art is about fun and relationships. My sketchbook is a space to experiment with absurd ideas, like a duck driving a tank over a field of skulls or a tortoise that's also a grenade. I like taking things that are perhaps defenseless and giving them a twist.

The Importance of Line Weight

I've sold out to line, I'm addicted to line weight. Line weight is crucial in drawing, especially for robots and mechanical designs. I use a three-weight line system -

  • Contour lines for silhouettes (darkest lines)
  • Parting lines where assemblies come together (second thickest)
  • Detail lines for internal features (thinnest)

Having this hierarchy helps create depth and clarity. I'm passionate about teaching this, as there's not a lot of instruction available on how to effectively use line weight.

Daily Habits and Health

I start my day with a walk, and some journaling to prepare my mind for creativity. Being healthy as an artist is important, our brains need rest and care. Every night, I make time to sketch, even if I'm tired. Maintaining a good sketch habit is crucial.

Learning from Failure

I believe in the importance of failure. If you're not failing at least once a week, you're not pushing yourself. Every week, I try drawing something where I might totally wipe out. It's through failure that we grow and improve.

Combining Technology and Nature

I love the idea of overusing technology and how it might relate to nature going forward. In my sketches, I often combine the two, like robots with television faces, trees that move, or animals using advanced tech. This juxtaposition allows me to ask questions and explore concepts that fascinate me.

Interacting with the Art Community

Art is relational. Sharing my work on platforms like Instagram allows me to connect with others. Sometimes, I incorporate suggestions or popular themes, like drawing mushrooms or creating fan art. It's fun to engage with the community and have that interaction inform my work.

Advice for Fellow Artists

  • Carry a sketchbook: Be ready to draw whenever inspiration strikes.
  • Establish daily routines: Consistency is key to growth.
  • Focus on big shapes first: Start with a strong, simple statement before adding details.
  • Have fun: Draw what you love and let your passion shine.
  • Embrace failure: Don't be afraid to try new things and make mistakes.

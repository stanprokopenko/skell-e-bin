How I Evolve Ideas in Sketchbooks

Hello! My name is Faith. I'm an artist working in animation, comics, freelance illustration, and you might know me from my personal artwork, which is very fantasy, girls with swords. Basically, that's the vibe.

I have three sketchbooks to show you today.

  • My 'No Thoughts, Head Empty' Sketchbook: This is what I do while I'm on the couch watching TV. It's ink, and I post it on my Instagram because it looks nice. It's just stream-of-consciousness stuff.

  • My Actual Sketchbook: This is where I plan everything I'm going to do. I thumbnail out stuff and have new ideas here. It's very ugly, but I'm being vulnerable and showing it to you today.

  • My Travel Sketchbook: These are largely drawn either from photos I take or on the spot when I'm traveling.

Let's start with the pretty sketchbook to earn your trust.

My 'No Thoughts, Head Empty' Sketchbook

In this sketchbook, I largely use the Pentel waterproof ink brush, and I also add markers. I go back into this sketchbook quite a lot and just draw what comes to me. I'm often sitting on the couch, watching TV, and this stuff ends up making its way into my finished work sometimes, but for the most part, it's just for fun.

I frequently reiterate while working in this sketchbook, because I find a nugget of an idea that I like and start chasing it. For example, I did some drawings that were sort of Daphne-esque - inspired by the myth where she turns into a tree rather than date Apollo. I thought the idea of a face in a tree was really cool, so I kept drawing that for a bit.

I also like coming up with fun costume stuff while I'm in this sketchbook. There are a lot of fairies and also a lot of monster girls. I don't know what was up with that!

Do I find myself chasing one specific thing for an entire sketchbook or a few pages at a time?

I think it's a few pages at a time for me, for the most part. I'll come back to it when I'm flipping through my sketchbook again. I'll just be like, "Oh, that, yeah," and then I'll keep going back to it.

Do my sketchbooks cross-pollinate?

They cross-pollinate a bit for sure. Usually, this sketchbook is not intended to inform my finished artwork. I'm not trying to do nice compositions, but sometimes happy accidents happen where I'm like, "Oh, this looks really nice," and I'll toss it to my computer or whatever.

One drawing in here ended up inspiring another finished series I did, which was a foil print series of bird girls. I guess they're harpies or sirens? It was seasonal, so I did winter, spring, summer, and fall.

Another drawing in this sketchbook ended up inspiring a watercolor that I don't think was very good, but I really like the composition. I ended up going with one for the watercolor, which I'm going to tackle again at some point because I didn't do a good job the first time, but I love this composition.

What is my end goal with this sketchbook?

That's a good question. I don't usually make finished stuff outside of this sketchbook, but sometimes it happens where I'm like, "Oh, I was kind of cooking, you know?" That idea kind of worked, so I'll explore it. But generally, it's just a free space to create.

How to Paint an Ear in Full Color

Copy Code

In this lesson, I'll walk you through painting a realistic ear from start to finish. We'll focus on essential techniques to capture the ear's forms, colors, and subtle details.

Applying the Color Wash

I like to start with a color wash to establish a warm base. For this ear, I chose a cadmium red orange at about 96% opacity. This sets a vibrant foundation, especially useful for capturing subsurface scattering and dark accents.

Rough Gesture Drawing

Using a colorized line, I begin a rough gesture drawing on a separate layer. I select a darker, moderately saturated orange or red for the lines. I sketch the back line of the jaw and cheekbone to anchor where the ear attaches. Then, I draw the basic bass clef shape of the ear, getting the initial rhythms down before refining.

Defining Shapes and Structures

Next, I break down the interior shapes using large hook shapes to describe the inner lines of the helix and smaller hooks for the tragus. I use straight lines to define secondary shapes, adding structure and contrasting with the curves. This balance of straights against curves enhances the design.

Mapping Lights and Darks

I map out the separation of lights and darks, filling in shadow areas like the concha and beneath the earlobe. This helps establish the ear's three-dimensional form early on.

Blocking in Local Colors

On a new layer, I block in flat local colors, starting with the skin tone. I adjust the color to match the mid-tones of the reference, aiming for a balance between ochre and red. I fill in the earrings with a darker gold to stand out against the skin, and block in the hair with a dark brown.

Adding Midtones and Highlights

I create another layer to add midtones and highlights. Focusing on the areas catching light, I gradually build up the lighter values, paying attention to the subtle shifts in color and value. I also adjust the color of the lines to blend with the painting.

Refining with the Blender Brush

At this stage, I flatten the layers (keeping backups) and use the blender brush to soften edges and merge colors seamlessly. This helps transition from the initial graphic shapes to a more painterly look.

Enhancing Subsurface Scattering

I emphasize the subsurface scattering, particularly where the ear is thin and light shines through, like the helix and lobule. I add warmer, more saturated reds and oranges to these areas to capture that translucent glow.

Pushing Highlights and Dark Accents

I intensify the dark accents in the deepest shadows and the highlights on areas most directly facing the light. This adds depth and realism, making the ear feel more three-dimensional. I experiment with cool colors like cyan for the hottest highlights.

Final Touches and Details

I clean up any remaining line work, add subtle textures like peach fuzz, and ensure a balance of large, medium, and small brushstrokes. I look for opportunities to place straights against curves for a stronger design.

Recap of Ear Anatomy

To conclude, here's a quick recap of the ear's anatomy:

  • Helix: the outer rim
  • Antihelix: the inner curved prominence
  • Tragus: the small projection in front of the ear canal
  • Antitragus: opposite the tragus, above the earlobe
  • Concha: the deep bowl leading to the ear canal
  • Earlobe: the lower fleshy part

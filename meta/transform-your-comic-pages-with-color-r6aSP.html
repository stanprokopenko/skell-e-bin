Available Resources

In the downloads section, you'll find inked pages ready for you to flat and color. You'll have access to inked interior pages from various comics, as well as several covers and splash pages. There's a wide variety of art styles and storytelling approaches in this collection, so you may need to approach coloring different artists' work differently.

Approaching Different Art Styles

Think about how your coloring can:

  • Add more emotional impact to the pages
  • Make the action clearer
  • Show off the three-dimensional forms of the characters and environments

Keep in mind:

  • Character and location color schemes
  • Time of day or other environmental lighting
  • Mood and dramatic flourishes of color

Tools and Techniques

Any digital tool will work, but you'll want to use a program that has layers and layer styles. Follow Matt's coloring process:

  1. Flat your pages first before locking in the local colors.
  2. Then add shading, highlights, and finishing touches.

Some of the more graphic pages may not need as much shading as some of the more realistically drawn pages.

Using Color Effectively

Look for ways to use color to:

  • Showcase depth and volume
  • Focus attention
  • Enhance the readability of the page

Make sure the color palette and rendering style you choose complements the look and feel of the line art instead of overwhelming it.

Your Assignment

Fully color one comic interior and one cover or splash page. Once you've finished, take one panel from your interior color and change the time of day!

That's the minimum though. If you're serious about making comic art, you'll do more than just that. We have a lot of great art to choose from, and there are a few bonus pages that will really challenge you if you dare to take them on.

Submission Guidelines

When you submit your assignments, include:

  • The flats
  • A page showing your base colors
  • The finished piece

Include a layered TIFF file so we can turn the layers on and off, and give proper feedback. Assume you're handing your page in to an editor. Your pages should be ready to print in a comic! Don't just paint bucket on the ink layer, use Matt's step-by-step approach to make a masterful color page.

What Is the Picture Plane?

If you don't quite understand the picture plane, here's the simplest way to explain it: It's a window, which is also your paper. The picture plane is the flat surface on which you create your drawing.

Visualizing Your Paper as a Window

If you find it hard to think of your paper as a window, consider this: To capture the complex lines of the world onto something flat like paper, it helps to visualize the paper as a window.

If that still doesn't make sense, try this exercise, Draw a rectangle on your paper. Think of this rectangle as a window to a world on the other side. That world may overlap with ours through the picture plane, which means we've pulled the viewer back to create a new picture plane, and we can keep doing that.

The Role of the Picture Plane in Drawing

The key idea of the picture plane is that you need a flat plane to make a picture. Usually.

This concept is important as you work on your next project, where you'll attempt plan projections using orthos of your chosen objects.

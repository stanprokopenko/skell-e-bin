Initial Setup

Proportions and Landmarks

Identify the brow line, center, and proportions. Remember halves and thirds. Mark the hairline. Identify four points: temple, hairline, back of skull, base of the nose. Create a cutout for the head shape. Consider a light or thin cutout. Divide into quarters to place the ear.

Facial Features

Note the larger C shape from brow to nose due to aging. Outline the chin and cheek on both sides. Simplify the cheek. Ear, cheek, and jaw are step six. Think about the features and their placement. Mark the center and eye sockets. Use folding planes and the rule of three. Relate to the forehead. Create overlap of the cheek behind the brow. Consider the disc across the face. Transition the brow down into the proportion line. Identify the socket and nose. Determine the width and tip of the nose. Create a simple box for the nose. Customize the curves and shapes. Think about the volume of the eye. Recognize the rhythm and curve of the cheek. Optionally add glasses.

Detailed Features

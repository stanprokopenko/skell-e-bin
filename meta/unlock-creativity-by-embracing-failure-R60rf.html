Embracing Failure and Continuous Practice

When it comes to drawing, there's often an impulse to want to draw everything well at first. You have this idea in your head of what you want something to look like, and when you can't pull it off, you get disappointed and might give up. The reality of drawing is that 99% of it is going to be a failure. I have hundreds of these sorts of drawings lying around, me trying something out and failing, or thinking, "Oh, that could have been done better."

The act of trying to make stuff work involves looking at those failures and seeing where you could have done something a little cleaner. Then, for the next time, you incorporate whatever you felt you missed into your drawing.

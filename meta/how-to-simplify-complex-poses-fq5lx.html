Using Part-to-Part Relations and Rhythm

When working on poses, I often focus on the part-to-part relationships and how every curve represents a part of the body. Dealing with Arms

Arms can be approached differently. You could use a C-curve, C-curve, and S-curve to give movement to the arm, or be more precise with the grouping of muscles, showing the deltoid, tricep, bicep, and forearm. Both methods can achieve a similar effect.

This straight-on pose is the hardest to draw because, to me, it's the least interesting. So, how do you make something inherently non-interesting feel fun, dynamic, or fluid? You can extend asymmetry into shifting weight or moving your pose, but that may not always be an option.

Abstraction and Design Elements in Gesture

The goal for me is always letting this dissolve into as much abstraction as possible, so I can design with it.

How to Sculpt Realistic Hand Tendons

Copy Code

Materials and Preparation

To add tendons to your hand anatomy sculpture, you'll need:

  • Victoria Brown microcrystalline wax mixed with beeswax (or a similar firm wax)
  • A different color clay or wax for the tendons

Start by rolling out sections of the wax to represent the tendons. Using a different color helps distinguish the tendons from the other structures in your sculpture.

Overview of Hand Tendons

Before you begin, it's important to understand the three main groups of tendons in the hand:

  1. Flexor muscles: Located on the front of the forearm and wrist
  2. Extensor muscles: Found on the back of the forearm and wrist
  3. Ridge muscles: Wrap around the other muscles

You'll be adding tendons from each of these groups to your sculpture.

Back of Hand Tendons

Start with the back of the hand on the pinky side and work your way over to the thumb.

Extensor Carpi Ulnaris

  • Travels between two ridges on the ulna
  • Attaches to the bottom part of the first phalanx of the pinky, just above the carpal bones of the wrist

Extensor Digiti Minimi

  • Travels up the entire length of the pinky

Extensor Digitorum

  • Branches out to all fingers
  • The pinky and ring finger tendons stay together until the last part

Extensor Indicis

  • Goes from the ulna area to the index finger
  • Travels under the other tendons before connecting to the index tendon

Thumb Tendons

Move to the tendons that attach to the thumb.

Extensor Pollicis Longus

  • Goes from under the other tendons, over and up to the thumb
  • The muscle attaches along the inside of the ulna

Extensor Pollicis Brevis

  • Attaches to the bottom of the second knuckle
  • There's a small space between this and the Extensor Pollicis Longus, forming the "snuff box"

Adductor Pollicis Longus

  • Attaches to the base of the first phalanx of the thumb
  • Travels right next to the Extensor Pollicis Brevis

Brachioradialis

  • One of the ridge muscles that turns the arm
  • Attaches to the bony bump on the radius
  • Ends before the wrist

Palm Side Tendons

Now move to the palm side of the hand.

Flexor Pollicis Longus

  • Attaches to the inside part of the thumb

Flexor Carpi Radialis

  • Attaches to the inside part of the palm near the wrist
  • Travels alongside the Flexor Pollicis Longus

Flexor Digitorum Profundus

  • Spreads out to tendons for each finger
  • Runs along the inside of the fingers all the way to the tips

Flexor Digitorum Superficialis

  • Overlaps with the Flexor Digitorum Profundus tendons

Flexor Carpi Ulnaris

  • Attaches to the bony protrusion on the carpal bones
  • Travels down along the arm

Tips and Recommendations

  • Start with the back of the hand towards the pinky side and work your way around
  • Prepare your clay coils ahead of time to save time during the sculpting process
  • Use a different colored clay for the tendons to distinguish them from other structures

Remember, understanding the tendons, bones of the wrist, and muscles of the hand is crucial for sculpting a realistic hand. While the forearm muscles are primarily responsible for hand movement, this focus on hand anatomy will greatly improve your hand sculptures.

By following these steps and paying attention to the details of each tendon group, you'll create a comprehensive and anatomically accurate hand study sculpture. Keep practicing and stay creative!

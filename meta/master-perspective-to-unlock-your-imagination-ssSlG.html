Why Perspective Matters

Not Just for Boxes and Buildings

You may know how to draw boxes in one, two, or three-point perspective, which helps you render city streets and buildings. But there is more. Perspective is not only about boxes and buildings - it applies to everything in the world.

Watch how a box can become many things: something boxy, or something boxy with a bit of cylindrical form. A sphere is the basic form of countless objects. Understanding these basics opens up endless possibilities.

The Challenge of Learning Perspective

Few Artists Master Perspective

Many artists find perspective challenging, and few truly master it. I hoped to learn it in college to draw buildings like this, but couldn't find any teachers who knew it. Back then, resources were scarce, and learning perspective was hard.

I once had a job illustrating a round platform with stair steps in front, side, and three-quarter views. I didn't have any idea how to do it. That's when I realized I needed to learn perspective if I was going to do this for a living.

Recognizing Your Need for Perspective

How do you know if you need perspective? Consider how you hope to draw:

  • If you work flat, like a graphic designer, or if you paint non-representationally, focusing on pattern and abstract design, not form and space, perspective won't help you much.
  • If you just copy what you see, you don't need perspective for that.

But if you want to create original work from your imagination, perspective is essential.

Learning to Draw, Not Just Copy

The Story of the Proud Youth

A proud youth proved he could draw accurately from observation. But it took a lot of time, and he found it difficult to capture moving subjects. He bought a camera, which took less time and captured moving subjects beautifully. He copied the photos and beheld drawings that looked a lot like the photos.

Eventually, he discovered that processing a photo created an impressive picture with attractive blotch marks all over it. "I have created art," he thought.

His father objected, questioning whether pushing buttons on a computer or copying what's in front of him truly manifested his creative self. His mother joined in, urging him to do something original.

Unlocking Your Imagination

Drawing Anything from Any Position

Do you get ideas for images but find them difficult to draw? Do you have trouble drawing something in a different position from your reference and making it look right?

Everyone does, until they learn how. Once you know how, you can draw anything you can imagine in any position, and in all sorts of styles. When you can draw from your imagination, you can create your own worlds.

Identifying Perspective Problems

Also, collect potential perspective problems to be solved later, questions you have, puzzles you care about. Examples include,

  • How do arches get smaller as they go away? Is there a science to that? Yes, and it's not that difficult.
  • **Can you manipulate that science and make it look credible? ** Yes! In one way, that's the whole aim of this course, to learn enough perspective to invent and experiment as you like.
  • How do you take a picture of something you're looking up at and draw it as if you're looking down at it? You will learn that!
  • How do kids on a merry-go-round in a perspective view clump up against the edges and spread out in the middle? Is there a way to draw that from imagination and from different points of view and keep it believable? There is!

If you want to do that or anything like it, you're in the right place.

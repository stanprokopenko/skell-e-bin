Continuous Practice and Commitment

The reason for the step-by-step approach is so that you can have something to hold on to as you move through this process. Developing these skills takes time, practice, and commitment. Keep working through the steps and refining your technique.

Further Study in Anatomy

You could go deeper into anatomy and learn about the muscles that animate and pull some of those features and areas of fat beneath the skin. Understanding the underlying structures can greatly enhance your head construction skills.

Exploring Light and Shadow

Try doing more with light and shadow. Look at portraiture and shading to see how the same planes might be visible not just in line, which can be harsher and more unforgiving, but in tone and edge, giving a softer effect. This can benefit or advance the way you think of line and perspective.

Developing Observational Skills

Developing your observational skills is just as important as your analytical skills. Observing tone and structure can help you advance your understanding of line and perspective. Take time to observe more and integrate what you see into your work.

Integrating Different Perspectives

Pair the course content with other perspectives. Look at how different head constructions are put together. Sample those techniques, modify this approach to be more customized, and push and expand on the different steps. You could even explore caricature or other artistic styles to broaden your skill set.

Thanks for sticking with me through this process. Best of luck!

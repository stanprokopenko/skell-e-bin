How to Create 3D Worlds on Paper

Perspective is the secret that allows artists to turn a flat surface into a deep world. While you might know how to draw a box in one, two, or three-point perspective, there's much more to learn. Perspective isn't just about boxes, buildings, and streets - it applies to everything in the world.

Consider how a box can represent many things:

  • Something boxy
  • Something boxy and a little cylindrical
  • A sphere, which is the basic form of many objects

Despite its importance, few artists truly master perspective. Learning it can be challenging, but it's an essential skill for creating realistic and imaginative artwork.

The Importance of Learning Perspective

Learning perspective is crucial if you want to:

  • Draw from your imagination
  • Create original scenes and worlds
  • Solve drawing problems for various subjects

When you can draw from imagination, you can create your own worlds. Perspective allows you to draw anything you can imagine in any position and in all sorts of styles.

Course Overview

This course will teach you classic perspective - 600 years of Renaissance knowledge adapted for 21st-century tools. We'll start with the basics, establishing strong foundations before moving on to developed perspective and complex worlds.

What You'll Learn

You'll discover how to:

  • Draw anything from imagination in any position
  • Create believable scenes
  • Solve common perspective problems

For example, you'll learn how to:

  • Make trees get smaller as they recede into the distance
  • Draw a tree from any point of view
  • Create feet and adjust basic forms to draw any possible foot
  • Draw arches that get smaller as they go away
  • Transform a scene from a low viewpoint to a high viewpoint

Examples from Master Artists

To understand perspective, let's look at some masters:

  • Leonardo da Vinci: His study drawings showcase simple yet effective use of perspective, often using just three sets of lines.
  • De Vries: 500 years ago, he drew environments like little stages where stories could play out.
  • Winsor McCay: 100 years ago, he applied perspective knowledge to create thousands of comic strip panels.

Getting Started: Your First Project

To begin your perspective journey:

  1. Collect your favorite drawings by perspective masters. This will set a standard for how you want to be able to draw.
  2. Gather potential perspective problems to solve later. These can be questions you have or puzzles you care about.

Consider perspective puzzles like:

  • How do arches get smaller as they recede?
  • How can you draw kids on a merry-go-round from different viewpoints while keeping it believable?

* * *

Perspective is a valuable lifelong skill for artists. It's not easy, but with practice, you'll gain the ability to invent and experiment as you like. Whether you're an absolute beginner or a pro, this course will help you master the art of perspective drawing.

Remember, perspective problems are basically puzzles. If you follow along and practice, you'll develop the skills to solve these puzzles and create incredible artwork. So, are you ready to turn flat surfaces into deep, imaginative worlds?

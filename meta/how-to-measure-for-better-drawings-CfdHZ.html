Importance of Measuring and Observation

Accurate measuring is crucial to avoid errors in drawing. I constantly measure and double-check my work to minimize mistakes. Observing proportions is essential, especially since features like ear and eye alignment change with head tilt. I rely on careful observation to ensure accuracy. Using a mirror helps me see my work with fresh eyes, allowing me to catch mistakes that I might otherwise miss.

Techniques for Improved Drawing

Squinting is a technique I use to simplify shapes and see them more clearly. Drawing upside down is another method that helps activate the right side of the brain, promoting objective observation. I often test my skills by drawing from photos, which helps me improve. Comparing my freehand drawing to a grid drawing allows me to assess accuracy and strive to draw as well without a grid as I can with one.

Personal Drawing Challenges

I have a tendency to impose my own features onto others' faces when drawing. Additionally, I often make noses too long, so I counteract this by measuring again. Recognizing these tendencies helps me improve my accuracy.

Tools and Methods

To prevent charcoal from mixing with paint, I use spray fixative. Pre-mixing colors with a limited palette is part of my process for efficient painting. This allows me to paint quickly without overthinking. I plan to spray fix the drawing before starting to paint, ensuring a clean surface.

Artistic Goals and Inspirations

My goal is to draw as well without a grid as I can with one. I'm inspired by my art teacher's ability to draw objectively. I aim to paint with meaningful strokes that tell a story, and maintaining historical accuracy is important to me, even in details like how a scarf is worn.

Painting Process

Pre-mixing paints is a step I take for quick and efficient painting. Once the colors are ready, I begin the painting process. For practice, I might paint both the freehand and grid drawings, which helps me refine my skills.

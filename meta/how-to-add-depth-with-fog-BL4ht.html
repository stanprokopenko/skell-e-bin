How to Add Depth with Fog

Capturing the Cool of Night with Deep Blues

In this piece, I wanted to capture the cool of night using deep blues. The foreground has a really dark blue, and there's a lot of atmosphere to play with. I aimed to achieve a transition by incorporating the classic setup of foreground, mid-ground, and background.

Using Foreground, Mid-ground, and Background

This classic layering technique helps create depth in the scene. By adding fog, I enhanced the separation between these layers. Fog is such a powerful tool for illustration that it's used over and over again. I believe it's important not to shy away from tried and true tools like fog in pursuit of originality.

The Power of Fog in Illustration

Fog creates a sense of depth and atmosphere that's hard to achieve otherwise. In this piece, I used heavy fog to separate the layers and give a sense of distance. The fog transitions from the foreground to the background, helping to guide the viewer's eye through the scene.

Experimenting with Pools of Light

I wanted to experiment with where to place a pool of light in the scene. The reference image had light coming from a street lamp, so I considered whether the light would come from a lamp on the front of the building or elsewhere. I thought about adding lit windows to draw attention to the house.

Adding Light Sources to Draw Attention

I decided to paint some window light, to make the house stand out. To create a focal point, I added a lamp near the stairs, a little lamp post right by the entrance. This draws our attention to the front door and creates a warm glow that spills onto the ground, and up onto the house.

Adjusting Colors for Mood

To make the light feel warm, I adjusted the colors using curves. I took some of the green out and reduced the blue, giving the light a warmer tint. This helps the lamp light contrast with the cool blues of the night scene.

Enhancing Depth with Low Mist

I liked the idea of a low mist rolling in along the lake, as if the water is warmer than the air. This adds to the layering of the trees and creates a creepy atmosphere. The mist makes the scene more mysterious and helps the silhouette of the character stand out.

Emphasizing the Character Silhouette

It's important that the character's silhouette is as clear as possible. By adding mist and fog around the character, I ensured they stand out against the background. I considered whether the character should be holding a lamp to create another light source, but for clarity, I kept the character as a pure silhouette.

Considering Rim Lights

I experimented with adding mystery rim lights to the character. Rim lights are often used in film to add definition to subjects. By imagining skylight from behind, I added a subtle rim light to the character and the tops of some bushes. I used these cautiously to keep the scene believable, ensuring the light sources made sense within the context.

Balancing Light and Contrast

I made sure that the lights in the scene weren't too bright or unrealistic. The lamp on the house wouldn't cast a rim light all the way to the character, so I didn't include that. Instead, I focused on using mist and subtle lighting to make the character and elements stand out without losing the nighttime ambiance.

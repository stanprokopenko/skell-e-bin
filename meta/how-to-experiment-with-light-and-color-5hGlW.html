Assignment: Exploring Light and Color

For your assignment, try painting light from different angles. This is an advanced skill and can be challenging, especially in a beginner's course. Don't worry if you can't achieve the same level as demonstrated, that's okay. The goal is to cultivate curiosity about light and color.

Using the Mountain Image

I'm providing a JPEG image of a mountain for you to color, focusing on light and color rather than just local color. Try painting how the scene would look with:

  • Sunlight from the left
  • Sunlight from the right
  • Soft light
  • Depth and atmosphere

This exercise is a great starting point to become a student of light.

Using the Simple Character Image

If you prefer something simpler, I'm also providing a very simple image of a cute little character. Similar to what we did in the materials section earlier, paint this simple object while considering:

  • Light coming from the left
  • Light coming from the right

Keep it really simple and experiment with different lighting compositions on this basic form.

Tips for Experimenting with Lighting

To enhance your understanding of light and color:

  • Look at photographs, illustrations, and movie frames.
  • Observe how much color and fog artists add to create depth.
  • Incorporate these observations into your images.

Working with Line Art in JPEG Files

When you open these files, they are JPEGs with black line art on a white background. To paint without destroying the line art, use this trick:

  1. Duplicate the layer with the line art.
  2. Hide the duplicated layer temporarily.
  3. On the background layer, fill it with a color (e.g., fill with gray using Option + Delete on Mac).
  4. Unhide the duplicated layer and set it to Multiply mode.

In Multiply mode:

  • White areas become transparent.
  • Black lines remain opaque.

Now, you have a line art layer on top that you can use as a guide, and you can draw underneath it to experiment with lighting without affecting the original lines.

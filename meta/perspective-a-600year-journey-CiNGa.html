The Birth of Perspective

Perspective began in Italy 600 years ago when Florentine artists discovered it, revolutionizing how you can depict space and reality.

Uccello and da Vinci

In the 15th century, Paolo Uccello created complex drawings that seem computer-generated. You'll understand his perspectives better when you study ellipses.

Leonardo da Vinci drew perspective studies with grids to track how figures move in space. You can learn from his preliminary work and storytelling.

The Spread Across Europe

Renaissance artists drew objects from multiple views, front, side, top, back, to depict them in any position.

Albrecht Dürer

Perspective spread to Germany through Albrecht Dürer, who illustrated how light beams become dots on a window, connecting them to draw in perspective.

Hans Vredeman de Vries

Hans Vredeman de Vries, a Dutch artist, loved perspective and used simple one-point and two-point perspectives with visible horizon lines. You can practice with his basic setups.

Perspective in Modern Art

By the time photography emerged, perspective was essential in European art education.

Photography's Impact

In the 19th century, photography changed art. Impressionists focused on light, while Expressionists bent perspective to express themselves.

Japanese Woodblock Prints

Japanese artists embraced perspective. Hiroshi Yoshida created prints impeccable in perspective, depicting buildings and organic forms like water.

20th Century Changes

During the 20th century, many fine artists abandoned perspective. However, comic book artists, animators, illustrators, and graphic designers continued to use it. M.C. Escher made great pre-computer perspective masterpieces.

Discovering Perspective Today

Perspective is a timeless discovery. Countless artists await your exploration. Dig for treasure and unleash perspective in your own art.

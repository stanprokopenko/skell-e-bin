Getting Limber

Welcome back! Let's get our hands limber and ready to draw some gestural limbs. By now, you've developed muscle memory, and we're going to use that as the genesis of everything in our drawings.

Simplifying with Mnemonics

I often use mnemonics to simplify complex forms. Instead of thinking about the rib cage as a complex thoracic structure with manubriums and sternums, I think of it as a tube with an egg around it. Simple shapes like the Liberty Bell for the pelvis help us remember and draw quickly.

Capturing Flow Over Structure

We want to shake off the shackles of structure. While structure is important, we don't want to show the mechanics overtly. Focus on the flow and gesture of the figure. Keep it long, light, and loose.

Understanding Pinch and Stretch

When drawing poses, observe where the body pinches and stretches. If an arm is raised and the scapula tilts, there's a pinch on one side and a stretch on the other. This awareness adds life to your drawings.

The Power of Practice

The key is mileage, drawing repeated patterns from different angles. The more you draw, the more these forms become second nature. Practice helps you overcome challenges, whether it's hands, forearms, or the scapula.

Expressive Lines with the Underhand Grip

Try using the underhand grip to create expressive marks. This allows for a range of motion and helps achieve fluidity in your lines. You can make broad strokes that aren't possible with the traditional writing grip.

Incorporating Story and Emotion

Ask yourself, what is the character thinking? Giving your figures a story or emotion adds depth. Themes like love, loneliness, or joy can inform your drawing and make it more engaging.

Learning from Art History

Reflect on the works of great artists. I was inspired by comic book artists like John Buscema and Jack Kirby. Looking back further, consider ancient art like the Venus of Willendorf. 28,000 years ago, people were creating art filled with emotion and meaning.

Embracing Simplicity

Simplicity is the most important thing. Remove unnecessary details to let the magic shine through. It's not about showing off all the mechanics but conveying the essence of the figure.

Experimenting and Enjoying the Process

Don't be afraid to try different poses and explore all avenues. Even if a drawing doesn't turn out perfectly, it leads to the next, better drawing. Enjoy the journey and keep practicing.

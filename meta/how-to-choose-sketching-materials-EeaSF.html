How to Choose Sketching Materials

Copy Code

Overview

Hello and welcome! In this first chapter, I want to walk you through the materials I like to use. It’s quite simple. I mostly stick to black pens, a good sketchbook, and a few markers or gouache. Let’s explore these items in detail.

Sketchbooks

I prefer a moleskin with thicker paper (look for 165-gram, 111-page versions). The cover color can vary, and some versions are labeled blue or white. The key is the thick paper because I often smudge and use gouache. If the paper is too thin, it can tear or warp.

I carry a sketchbook everywhere. It inevitably gets beat up, but it’s worth it since I never know when I’ll have a few minutes to capture a quick idea. Even rushed sketches can capture energy and life, which I can refine later.

Other sketchbooks can also work, as long as the pages are sturdy. For example, I have one made with very thick paper that handles smudging and marking well. Sometimes I even use stone paper, which lets you lift values and blend gouache in interesting ways. I’ve even used a photo album as a sketchbook, because the paper is thick enough to handle ink and paint.

Pens and Markers

I like to use a black pen that keeps my lines sharp. I also add color with water-based markers (like Tombow). Water-based markers don’t bleed through the page, and they don’t smear my pen lines. This way, my drawings stay intact unless I decide I actually want some bleeding as a special effect.

If you’re on a budget or just starting, a basic approach is to get a few grayscale markers for simple value studies. That might be a 50% gray and a 30% gray to start. Alcohol-based markers such as Copics are also popular, but they can soak through the page and sometimes smear your pen lines, so be aware of that.

Paper and Gouache

As you experiment with sketches, you might add gouache now and then. Heavier paper stands up to paint better. Make sure you have something that can handle moisture and smudging without tearing. The 160- or 165-gram paper weight is a good standard if you plan on mixing pen lines with light washes of paint or ink.

* * *

Those are my main materials: a heavier paper sketchbook, a pen, and maybe a few markers or gouache. That’s all you need to get started. Next, we’ll dive into the basics of sketching shapes. Grab your favorite pen, and let’s begin!

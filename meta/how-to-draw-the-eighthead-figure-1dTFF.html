Understanding Figure Proportions

It's really important that you get a basic understanding of the proportions of the figure that you're going to be positioning and moving around.

Developing the Proportion Chart

Setting Up the Scale

Try to go through the same development of the proportion chart that I did. Start by drawing a line for the head at the top and a line for the foot at the bottom. This establishes your idea about scale. Then, between these two lines, divide the total height into halves.

Dividing into Halves and Thirds

I started by marking the first division and then added three more above and three more below. That gives you your eight-head figure. From there, you have the heads down to the pelvis, which is at head four, and then the divisions for the bottom.

You can then come in between head one and two and head two and three and make divisions into thirds. This will help you place the rib cage a little more clearly there. I did the thirds in blue and the initial halves in a warmer red. Hopefully, you can see that difference if you want to go back and try to copy.

Placing the Major Masses

Put the big masses, your head, rib cage, and pelvis, layered in so that you can understand the way the spine pushes them and organizes that chaotic tower that is our body.

Completing the Lay-In with Limbs

Complete that with a simple lay-in of the directional angles or curves of the legs.

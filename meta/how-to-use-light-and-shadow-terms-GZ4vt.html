How to Use Light and Shadow Terms

Understanding art terms is crucial for beginners. Artists often assume that everyone knows their specific language, which can be confusing. This guide will help you grasp the essential terms used in mixing and painting, ensuring you don't need to look them up.

Using a Sphere to Explain Art Terms

To explain these terms, we'll use a sphere as a model. The direction of the light source remains consistent, helping you see how light interacts with objects.

Key Terms in Mixing and Painting

  • Average Dark: This represents the absence of light on the model.
  • Core Value: The transition spot between shadow and light, like on a cheek.
  • Cast Shadow: The area where the object blocks light from hitting the surface.
  • Shadow Family: Includes areas not directly receiving light.
  • Light Family: Comprises halftones and average light.

Understanding Halftones

Halftones are part of the light family and should not be confused with shadows. It's important to keep halftones lighter than they appear to maintain proper values. A common mistake is darkening halftones, thinking it enhances form. However, this can muddy the form and mix up light and shadow families.

Maintaining Proper Values

Keeping values separated helps maintain clarity in painting. The average light and dark halftone should be close in value for subtle form turning. The goal is to create a three-dimensional appearance using light and dark values.

Highlights and Edges

Highlights represent the pinnacle of forms, like the tip of the nose, and are also known as Chris Light. The transition from halftone to shadow involves edges.

Reflective Light and Accents

Reflective light is part of the shadow family and comes from surrounding surfaces. It does not disrupt the shadow family. Accents are areas where no light gets in, making them darker. They occur where two surfaces meet closely, like a ball on the ground, and may require additional darkening.

* * *

Understanding this foundational vocabulary is crucial for following art instructions. The light family and dark family each have three terms to learn. Mastering these basics will help you grasp further art concepts effectively.

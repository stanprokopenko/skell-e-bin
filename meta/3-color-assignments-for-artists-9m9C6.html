Monochromatic Sphere Painting

Your first assignment is to paint a sphere using a monochromatic palette. You can use red like the example provided, or experiment with a hue of your own choosing. This exercise serves as a warm-up for the second part of the assignment.

To get the most out of this practice:

  • Try creating a whole series of monochromatic paintings
  • Explore different hues to see what feels and emotions various colors evoke
  • Pay attention to how the monochromatic palette affects the overall mood of your painting

Repainting Existing Artworks

For the second assignment, you'll be applying your color theory knowledge to existing artworks. Here's what you need to do:

  1. Go online and choose some paintings you admire
  2. Create line work from the chosen piece
  3. Repaint the artwork using the following color palettes:
    • Complementary palette
    • Split complementary palette
    • Four-color palette

Important: When repainting, make sure to keep the same overall lighting as the original artist. Your task is to change only the colors as part of your new palette.

Submission Guidelines

When you've completed your assignments, follow these steps:

  • Post your finished studies
  • Include the reference you've used alongside your work
  • Remember, these will be the pieces that will be critiqued later

Tips for Success

To make the most of these assignments and improve your color theory skills:

  • Practice with multiple monochromatic paintings to get comfortable with the technique
  • Experiment with different hues in your monochromatic studies to broaden your understanding of color
  • When repainting existing artworks, pay close attention to maintaining the original lighting
  • Focus on applying the color theory concepts you've learned throughout the course
  • Take your time with each assignment to really explore and understand how different color palettes affect the overall feel of a painting

By completing these assignments, you'll gain practical experience in applying color theory concepts and develop a deeper understanding of how different color palettes can impact your artwork. Good luck with your assignments!

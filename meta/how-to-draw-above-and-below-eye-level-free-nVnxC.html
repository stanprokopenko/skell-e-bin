What's in Premium?

In this lesson, you'll learn how to draw objects from above and below using perspective techniques. We'll cover how to set your eye level (the horizon line) and how it influences all other lines in your drawing. You'll understand how receding lines behave when looking up or down:

  • Looking down, receding lines aim up.
  • Looking up, receding lines aim down.

We'll explore one-point, two-point, and three-point perspective, focusing on how lines converge toward vanishing points and how vertical lines behave. The importance of the three spatial axes—width, height, and depth—will be emphasized to help you accurately depict forms.

You'll learn about foreshortening and how angles change as objects approach your eye level. Through practice exercises, such as drawing rooms from different eye levels and quick sketching, you'll reinforce these concepts.

By the end of the lesson, you'll have a solid understanding of perspective drawing from various viewpoints and how to apply these principles to create realistic scenes.

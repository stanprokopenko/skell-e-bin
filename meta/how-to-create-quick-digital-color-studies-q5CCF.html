How to Create Quick Digital Color Studies

Copy Code

I'll show you how to create a new painting using a previously made line drawing and enhance it with a split complementary color palette. This process involves generating a line drawing from an existing study, organizing layers in Photoshop, and applying color theory to create a visually appealing painting. I'll also share tips for achieving realism and color harmony in your artwork.

Generating a Line Drawing

First, let's generate a line drawing from a previous study:

  1. Duplicate the Layer: This preserves the original artwork.
  2. Use Filter > Stylize > Find Edges: In Photoshop, this filter helps create a basic line drawing.
  3. Adjust Levels: Enhance the whites and darks to improve the line drawing.
  4. Erase Unnecessary Details: Remove extra lines, especially those you plan to refine later.
  5. Paint Over Areas Not Needed: Use white to cover brushstroke outlines and other unnecessary details.
  6. Hide the Original Painting: Create a new layer on top and fill it with white. Name this layer "line drawing two."
  7. Set the New Layer to Multiply: This allows the line drawing to show through.

Organizing Layers in Photoshop

Proper layer organization is crucial for a smooth workflow:

  1. Group Layers: Shift-click the layers you want to group and press Control + G.
  2. Unlock the Background Layer: Double-click to unlock it, hit OK, and then include it in the group.
  3. Group the Top Two Layers: This helps keep your workspace organized.

Applying a Split Complementary Palette

A split complementary palette can add depth and interest to your painting:

  1. Define the Palette: Split one side of the color wheel into two neighboring hues.
  2. Rotate the Palette: For example, rotate towards green for a green monster and purple shadows.
  3. Indicate and Rearrange Colors: Make them easily accessible.
  4. Start with Neutralized Colors: Build saturation from there.
  5. Block in Temperature Delineations: Make the foreground and character warmer, and the background cooler.
  6. Use Hue Saturation: Darken and increase saturation to give the painting a quick punch.

Enhancing the Painting with Lighting and Shadows

Lighting and shadows can significantly enhance your painting:

  1. Introduce All Three Colors Early: Mix them to create intermediary hues.
  2. Scumble in Influences of Green and Orange: Deepen purples for shadows.
  3. Rough in Water: Use lighter purples for details like eyeballs and irises.
  4. Encourage Sketchiness Early: This fosters exploration.
  5. Use Linear Dodge Mode: Brush in lighting to make elements more vibrant.
  6. Switch to a Harder Brush: Carve into soft brush strokes for refinement.
  7. Adjust Local Values: Start in the correct range for different objects.
  8. Use Ambient Occlusion: Indicate intersections and shadows, smudging hard edges for a softer look.
  9. Merge the Line Drawing with the Painting: This keeps the process free-flowing.
  10. Fill Out the Sky and Forest: Use smaller shapes to make the scene feel more populated.
  11. Work on Occlusion Shadows: Vary darkness and avoid repetitive shapes.

Practical Tips for Realistic and Harmonious Color Use

Here are some tips to ensure your painting looks realistic and harmonious:

  1. Flip the Canvas: Check for temperature consistency.
  2. Use a Watercolor Brush: Glaze color temperature changes.
  3. Experiment with Blending Modes: See how they modify colors.
  4. Consider Light Direction and Saturation: This is crucial for highlights.
  5. Increase Occlusion Shadows: Under the bridge and on the monster's belly.
  6. Balance Warm and Cool Colors: Prevent areas from looking too yellow or too purple.
  7. Use Colors from the Palette in Multiple Areas: This ensures harmony.
  8. Add Subtle Color Shifts and Micro Comparisons: These add vibrancy.
  9. Break Lines and Add Imperfections: This makes forms look more realistic.
  10. Study Real-Life Scenes and Photographs: Apply real-world logic to your colors.
  11. Carry a Sketchbook or Digital Device: For plein air painting to improve light analysis skills.
  12. Use the Lasso Tool and Screen Mode: Adjust background light to enhance contrast.
  13. Ensure Consistency in Light Direction: Especially with direct sunlight.
  14. Add Subtle Highlights and Details: Enhance realism, like in the monster's eyes and fur.
  15. Spread Colors Throughout the Painting: For a cohesive look.
  16. Use Overlay Brushes Sparingly: Add warm glows and bounce light without overdoing it.
  17. Populate the Painting with All Chosen Colors: Balance warm and cool areas.
  18. Add Micro Movements in Color: Contribute to appealing color work.

By following these steps and tips, you can create a new painting that is both visually appealing and harmonious, using a previously made line drawing and a split complementary color palette.

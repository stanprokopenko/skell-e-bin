Early Love for Coloring

Coloring has always been a part of my life. Like many, I started with coloring books as a kid. I dabbled in painting classes and art lessons, and eventually realized that traditional school wasn't for me. So, I went to art school and chose to major in sequential art, comics. Learning Photoshop back in the late '90s felt like an epic quest, but I was thrilled to explore digital art. It wasn't about becoming a colorist then, I was just excited to color my drawings digitally.

Breaking into the Industry

After art school, I was eager to find any job involving art to escape waiting tables and working at a commercial printer. I interviewed everywhere, and the opportunity that came through was assisting a colorist in his studio. I thought, **"This is something I know how to do and a place I'd like to live, let's go for it! # TEXT

I didn't anticipate that this would kickstart a 20-year career as a comic book colorist. Initially, it was just a temporary gig, but over five years, I progressed from flatting and archival work to coloring entire issues on my own. I started attending conventions, meeting peers trying to break into comics. I offered to color their projects for free, which led to low-paying jobs, and eventually, better opportunities. When some of those friends moved to Marvel, they recommended me, opening the door to bigger projects.

Embracing Nature for Inspiration

My wife and I love to hike and travel in our camper van. We spend months working remotely, exploring parts of the country we've never seen. Nature plays a significant role in my inspiration. I absorb the colors and make mental notes, often using them in my work. Sometimes, when I need fresh ideas, I'll search for images like sunsets to find new palettes.

Favorite Characters and Settings

My favorite Marvel character has evolved over time. As a kid, it was Spider-Man or Wolverine. Now, it's Thor because I've colored so much of his stories. I feel proud to be associated with that character. I also enjoy coloring Venom due to his cool design and iterations like Agent Venom.

I particularly love coloring natural settings, like the lush landscapes of Krakoa in the X-Men comics. Given the choice, I'd prefer natural landscapes over man-made structures. However, cityscapes at night, like in the Star-Lord miniseries set in New York, offer exciting opportunities with varied light sources and changing palettes.

Battling Self-Doubt

We're all our own biggest critics. I often see other artists' work and notice what they can do that I feel I can't. But I channel that into motivation to improve. When self-doubt creeps in, I block everything out and focus on doing the work. My art school training and years of practice have ingrained fundamentals that guide me instinctively.

Dealing with Burnout

Recognizing burnout signs early has been crucial. I used to get blindsided by it, but now I can anticipate it. I adjust by giving myself breaks and focusing on other aspects of life. For instance, I might take longer walks with my dog or spend more time outdoors. It's about finding balance without compromising work.

* * *

Whenever I feel unmotivated, I recall my grandpa's words: "It's better than digging ditches." I'm grateful for this career. Despite challenges, it's a great job, and I've come to appreciate that experience makes the work manageable and rewarding.

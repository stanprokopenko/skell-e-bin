Simplifying to Two or Three Values

Your first project is to see value relationships and organize them into groups by simplifying images down to two or three values.

Two-Value (Notan) Drawings

Two-value drawings (Notan) use just lights and darks. You'll choose a threshold: anything lighter goes into lights, anything darker into darks. This threshold is up to you, based on your perception.

Three-Value Drawings

With three values, you have lights, mid-values, and darks. The mid-value holds ambiguous values. While adding a mid-value gives more information, avoid overusing it. Make clear distinctions, setting two thresholds between light, mid, and dark.

Assignment Level 1: Master Painting Thumbnails

Your assignment is to do thumbnail studies of master paintings, focusing on values. Study how masters organize major value groups. Use provided images or choose your own favorites.

Tips for Thumbnail Studies

Thumbnails are small drawings, one to six inches, forcing you to avoid details and focus on major shapes. Concentrate on massing shapes and simplifying values.

Charles Bargue provides excellent inspiration. In his work, he simplifies images into two values, removing details and focusing on essential forms.

Assignment Level 2: Still Life Thumbnails from Photos

For more challenge, set up your own still life, compose, light, and photograph it. Then, do value studies from your photo. This is harder since you're working from a photo, not a master painting.

Challenges of Working from Photos

Master paintings have had much problem-solving done. Photos present raw details and noise. You'll need to filter out unnecessary information, focusing on big masses. Fight the urge to include all details.

Focus on Skills, Not Final Drawings

Remember, the assignment isn't about beautiful drawings. It's about improving specific skills to help in future artworks.

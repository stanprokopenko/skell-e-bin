Understanding Edges

Now, let's talk about edges. There are four kinds of edges:

  • Hard edges
  • Firm edges
  • Soft edges
  • Lost edges

Edges are crucial in painting because they help describe the form. Here's where these edges usually appear on the human head.

Hard edges

Hard edges are usually cast shadows, like the shadow under the nose or the shadow the head casts on the neck. A hard edge can also occur where a light area is silhouetted against a dark background or vice versa.

Firm edges

Firm edges are found where two forms meet and have a definite change in plane, like where the bone of the skull shows through at the cranium.

Soft edges

Soft edges appear on rounded forms, like the cheekbone or the bulbous end of the nose. These edges imply that the thing you're painting is very rounded.

Lost edges

Lost edges are wonderful because you get to hide form within them. This occurs where the background is the exact same value as what's going on in the foreground, so they just blend together. It's a real rest for the eye.

Using Edges to Describe Form

You can tell what's going on with the anatomy just by edges alone. There's a musical quality to varying edges: hard and soft, playing around, creating interest. It's amazing how much you can tell a story about form just using edges.

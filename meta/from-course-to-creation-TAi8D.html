Celebrate Your Progress

Congratulations on reaching the end of the course! You've explored essential aspects of comic creation and made significant strides in your learning.

Dive Deeper into Comic Creation

Now that you have a broad understanding of the process, it's time to dive even deeper into one of those topics. Consider specializing in penciling, inking, or coloring so you have one skill to headline your art. Knowing the entire workflow allows you to account for each step of the process, no matter where you're at in the pipeline.

Focus on Improvement Areas

Think about what you want to improve most in your work and focus your energy there.

  • If you want to draw better-looking characters, there are more figure drawing and anatomy courses you can dive into next.
  • If environments are more your thing, perspective and landscapes can be your focus.
  • If you found yourself struggling with the fundamentals and making basic shapes three-dimensional, then the Drawing Basics course could be where you're headed next.

Keep Making Art

No course will be as important as making art. Does finishing the course mean that you're a master at comic books? Probably not, but you have the foundation that will get you there much faster. The only way to keep improving is to put your skills to use.

Make comics that excite or interest you. Write your own scripts, create your own characters, and have some fun.

Including Plant Elements in My Artwork

I'm exploring the solar punk aspect of my illustration and want to include plant elements to capture that feeling. To do this, I need to study some plants and understand their shapes and patterns.

Memorizing Plant Shapes

Plants are great to memorize and reuse in illustrations. Once I internalize some interesting plant shapes, I can add them anywhere to enhance visual interest. They're especially useful when I need to fill negative space or add texture to a composition.

Observing Different Plant Types

Simple Leaf Structures

Some plants have straight stems with leaves on each side. These can be drawn as simple lines with paired leaves and are useful for adding visual noise to a piece.

Grouped Leaves and Patches

I noticed plants where leaves are in patches or groups of three. These leaves sometimes float around the stem without visible connections, which means I don't need to draw every twig or branch.

Memorizing these leaf relationships adds intention to my drawings and brings more realism and gravity to the piece.

Irregular Shapes to Add Personality

To avoid repetition, I look for plants with irregular shapes. For example, some plants resemble "big hairy socks of plant fur". Including these unique shapes can add personality and break up the monotony of typical leaf patterns.

Plants with Long Leaves and Winding Branches

Some tropical plants have leaves that are three to four times longer than others and form an explosion-like silhouette, like a firework. These plants often have winding branches and visible wood grain, adding another layer of detail.

Being aware of these differences helps me draw plants with intention, making them distinct rather than random bundles of leaves.

Creating a Diverse Reference Sheet

By sketching these various plant types, I'm building a reference sheet of shapes and patterns. This gives me a wide range of options to incorporate into my illustrations when I want to add an organic component.

Applying Plant Studies to Illustrations

Studying plants allows me to add diversity and realism to my work. Whether it's familiar plants like snake plants or new discoveries from my photos, these elements become additional ingredients I can play with in my art.

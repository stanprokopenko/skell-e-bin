Embracing the Process

I believe that to truly succeed as an artist, you have to fall in love with the process. It's not just about the end result or the accolades that might come. When I spend time setting up equipment or tweaking camera settings for a video, I'm choosing to care about the quality of what I'm creating. I don't have to go the extra mile, but I do because I enjoy it and I'm proud of my work.

If you find yourself not caring about the quality of what you're doing, it might be a sign that you're not in the right place. In the past, I've had jobs where I didn't care about the outcomes, and it made me miserable. It's essential to find that spark that makes you excited to get out of bed and dive into your work.

Balancing Passion and Practicality

While creativity is at the heart of what we do, we can't ignore the practical side of life. We all have bills to pay, and responsibilities to meet. It's about finding a balance between doing what you love and making a living. The best scenario is when you have a mix of both, where you would do the work for free because you love it, but you're also making a comfortable living from it.

I don't always get to do only what I love. Running a company comes with tasks that I don't enjoy, like dealing with contracts or putting out fires. But I try to get those done efficiently so I can focus on what truly excites me.

Making Micro-Decisions Every Day

Success isn't about one big decision or a single moment. It's about the thousand little decisions we make throughout the day. It's easy to fall into a pattern of making the easy choices, but it's the small, sometimes tough decisions that get us a millimeter closer to where we want to be.

Sometimes, that means allocating time to rest and think. I often take walks to let my mind wander and allow ideas to surface naturally. It's in these moments that clarity often comes, sometimes after months of contemplation.

Dealing with Success and Expectations

It's important to manage expectations about success. Even after years in the industry, I still find excitement when a video does well. But I don't expect every project to be a home run. Not every video will go viral, and that's okay. Comparing yourself to unrealistic standards can be detrimental. Everyone wants to be above average, but that's statistically impossible.

Instead, focus on creating the best work you can. Celebrate the successes when they come, but don't let the desire for recognition overshadow the joy of creating.

Trusting Yourself and Overcoming Failure

Failure is an inevitable part of any creative journey. It's essential to have a healthy relationship with failure. When things don't work out, use it as an opportunity to learn and grow rather than a reason to beat yourself up.

Respect yourself enough to make difficult decisions that will improve your life later on. Treat yourself with the same compassion you would offer to a friend or family member who's struggling.

Following Your Genuine Interests

One of the most important lessons I've learned is to follow what genuinely interests you, even if it doesn't seem practical at the time. When I started making YouTube videos, it wasn't the obvious path to success. But I was passionate about teaching and sharing my knowledge, and over time, that passion resonated with others.

Don't be afraid to trust yourself and pursue what you love. The market is always changing, and nobody has a crystal ball. Focusing on what you care about will make the journey worthwhile, regardless of the outcome.

Patience and Consistency

There's no shortcut to mastery. It takes time, patience, and consistent effort to improve your skills and build a career. Instead of trying to rush the process, embrace the learning journey. Take small, consistent steps forward, and over time, they'll add up to significant progress.

Avoid the trap of the "forever student" mentality, where you never feel ready to take the next step. At some point, you have to jump in and start applying what you've learned, even if you don't feel entirely prepared.

Avoiding Unrealistic Comparisons

It's easy to look at others who have achieved success and feel inadequate. But comparing yourself to others can be dangerous. Everyone's journey is different, and success comes in many forms. Instead of measuring yourself against others, focus on your own growth and achievements.

Remember that even the most successful people started somewhere. They faced challenges and failures just like everyone else. Use their stories as inspiration, not as a yardstick to measure your worth.

Navigating Career Decisions

Making decisions about your career can be daunting. There's pressure to follow conventional wisdom or to take the path that others expect of you. But sometimes, you have to push back against expectations and find your own way.

That doesn't mean disregarding advice outright. It's about weighing the options, considering your own goals and values, and making informed decisions. Be realistic with yourself about what you want and what you're willing to do to achieve it.

Embracing the Journey

Ultimately, being an artist is about embracing the journey with all its ups and downs. There will be times of success and times of struggle. But if you stay true to yourself, make thoughtful decisions, and remain passionate about your work, you'll find your path.

Life doesn't come with a roadmap, especially in creative fields. Adaptability, perseverance, and self-belief are key. Keep moving forward, learn from every experience, and trust in your ability to navigate the challenges ahead.

Importance of Choosing the Right Photo

Choosing the right photo for painting is crucial. Many people put effort into learning to paint but select photos that are too difficult, even for experienced artists like John Singer Sargent. It's important to choose photos that simplify the painting process rather than complicate it. A good reference photo can make painting easier and more enjoyable.

Lighting Techniques in Painting

John Singer Sargent was known for his use of lighting to enhance his subjects. He often used side lighting with a three-quarter light ratio on the face. This technique helped eliminate problems and highlight the subject beautifully. Understanding and using effective lighting techniques can significantly improve the quality of a painting.

Challenges with Complex Lighting

Beginners often choose photos with overly complex lighting, which can be challenging even for experienced artists. It's important to avoid difficult lighting setups that can make the painting process more complicated. Even as an experienced artist, I steer clear of overly complex lighting situations.

Reference Quality and Accessibility

Most artists use photography for reference because having live models is a luxury. It's essential to have high-quality references, even when working from digital devices like iPads or phones. The quality of the reference photo can greatly impact the final painting.

Identifying Suitable Photos for Painting

When selecting a photo for painting, it's crucial to have clear, definable light sources. Multiple light sources can confuse shapes, values, edges, and colors, making it difficult to paint effectively. A good reference photo should have a clear light direction and distinct separation between light and dark areas.

Characteristics of Good Reference Photos

Good reference photos for painting should have clear light direction and shadow patterns. It's important to separate the light family from the shadow family. Photos with a single light source and clear shadow patterns are ideal, providing good edges and shapes to work with.

Common Pitfalls in Photo Selection

Photos with evenly lit faces often lack depth and interest, making it difficult to create a dynamic painting. Multiple light sources can create a confusing mix of shadows and highlights, complicating the painting process. It's important to avoid photos where shadows and halftones have the same value, as this can lead to a lack of clarity in the painting.

Examples of Effective Lighting

John Garrity's portrait was painted with one soft light, providing a clear light pattern and dark mass, making it suitable for painting. Sargent's paintings often used lighting that allowed for artistic interpretation rather than copying every detail. Effective lighting should not cut the model in half with shadow and light but should provide a balanced light-to-shadow ratio.

Tips for Selecting Photos

When selecting photos for painting, look for clear shadow patterns and avoid those with half the face in shadow. Large reference photos are preferable as they allow for better analysis of light and shadow when squinting. A well-lit face with a clear core shadow is easier to paint, allowing for the separation of light and shadow families.

* * *

Good photography for artists requires clear light and shadow patterns. A pretty photo might not be suitable for painting if it lacks clear lighting and shadow separation. By choosing the right photo, artists can ensure a more effective and enjoyable painting process.

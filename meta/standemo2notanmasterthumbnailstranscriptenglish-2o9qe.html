Studying a Beautiful Composition

I'm working on a quick demo of a composition I find beautiful, simple, and elegant. The artist masterfully uses a dark silhouette of a horse and rider against a middle-value background, with a light cloud behind them to create contrast. The composition's diagonal movement adds energy and dynamics, avoiding a static, vertical layout.

Simplifying Shapes and Values

In my study, I focus on simplifying the composition into three values: dark, middle, and light. I lightly map in the shapes, paying attention to negative shapes like the spaces between the horse's legs and under the armpit. I'm not measuring exact proportions but aiming to capture the essence of the shapes. I emphasize abstracting the horse and rider into basic shapes without detailing features.

Grounding and Connecting Shapes

I love how the artist blends the feet into the cast shadow, connecting the horse to the ground. Initially, I debated whether to keep the silhouette floating or to ground it by connecting the dark shape to a darker ground plane. By adding a darker value to the ground, the composition feels more grounded and cohesive.

Importance of Small Details

Small details like the spear are crucial. Even though they're minor elements, they provide essential context and storytelling. Oversimplifying and omitting these can lead to a loss of important information. I make sure to include these details in my study to maintain the composition's integrity.
